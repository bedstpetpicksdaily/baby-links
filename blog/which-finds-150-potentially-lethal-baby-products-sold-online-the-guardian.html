150 Unsafe Baby Products Found Online: What Parents Need to Know Right Now

A recent Which? investigation uncovered 150 potentially lethal baby products being sold through online retailers, raising serious safety concerns for parents shopping for nursery essentials. If you've been purchasing baby gear on Amazon or other platforms, it's crucial to understand what these risks are and how to shop more safely.

Why This Matters

The products flagged in the investigation included items like cot bumpers, sleep positioners, and drop-side cots—many of which fail to meet UK and EU safety standards. These aren't niche items; they're commonly searched-for products that appear in regular search results alongside legitimate, certified alternatives. Parents often assume that if something is available on a major platform, it must be safe, but this investigation reveals that assumption can be dangerous.

The concerning part is that many of these unsafe products are cheaper than their certified counterparts, making them attractive to budget-conscious parents. Some sellers deliberately mislead customers about certifications or country of origin to bypass safety regulations.

What Parents And Caregivers Should Know

  • Look for safety certifications: Check product listings for BS EN 1130 (cots), BS 1877 (prams), or CE marks. These indicate the product has passed rigorous safety testing.
  • Check seller ratings and reviews carefully: Read recent customer reviews for any mentions of quality issues, broken parts, or safety concerns. Be wary of suspiciously new products with only positive reviews.
  • Avoid certain product types online: Items like sleep positioners, cot bumpers, and drop-side cots have been banned or heavily restricted in many countries. If you see them for sale, question why.
  • Purchase from established retailers: While Amazon hosts third-party sellers, buying from Amazon's own stock or verified brands reduces (though doesn't eliminate) risk.
  • Check country of origin: Products manufactured outside the UK/EU may not meet the same safety standards, even if they appear legitimate.
  • When in doubt, verify independently: If a deal seems too good to be true, search the product name with "safety recall" or check the manufacturer's official website.
