White noise works by creating a consistent audio backdrop that masks sudden sounds—air conditioning units, neighborhood traffic, outdoor insects, and household activities that interrupt sleep. Studies show that consistent white noise can reduce sleep onset time by up to 50% and improve sleep quality significantly in infants.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is white noise safe for babies?

Yes, when used properly. White noise at safe volume levels (under 50 decibels) doesn't damage infant hearing. Baby Shusher is specifically engineered with safe volumes for newborns and includes timer functions to prevent overexposure. Position devices 3-4 feet from the crib and avoid earplugs or headphones for babies.

How loud should Baby Shusher be set during hot nights?

Set Baby Shusher to mask outdoor summer noise without exceeding 50 decibels—roughly the volume of rainfall or a quiet conversation. You should be able to hear it from across the room but not feel it's overwhelming. Most hot nights require moderate settings since air conditioning and open windows increase ambient noise.

Can white noise machines replace air conditioning?

No. White noise machines like Baby Shusher complement climate control but don't replace it. Maintaining 68-72°F is essential for safe infant sleep. Use Baby Shusher alongside proper room temperature regulation, not instead of it. The white noise masks the air conditioning unit itself, which is the primary benefit during summer.

How quickly will Baby Shusher help my baby sleep through hot nights?

Most babies respond within 3-5 nights of consistent use. Sleep associations build gradually, so patience is important. You may notice immediate calming effects, but sustained sleep improvements typically require one to two weeks of consistent application alongside other summer sleep strategies.
