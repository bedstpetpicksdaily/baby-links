Finding the right jogger stroller for a newborn who'll be traveling is a unique challenge—you need something sturdy enough for running or brisk walks, compact enough to fit in a car, and safe enough for a tiny infant who can't sit up yet. Most joggers are designed for older babies, which means new parents are often caught between the bulky all-terrain options and lightweight travel strollers that lack proper suspension. This guide cuts through the confusion to help you find a jogger that handles both your active lifestyle and your frequent trips without compromise.

What to Look For

  • Newborn-Friendly Recline and Support: Look for a jogger with a fully flat or near-flat recline position and excellent head/neck support, since newborns can't sit independently. A newborn insert or cocoon-style design that nestles your baby safely is essential for those first few months.
  • Compact Fold and Travel Weight: A jogger designed for travel should fold to fit standard car trunks and ideally weigh under 25 pounds. Check the folded dimensions carefully—some joggers claim portability but take up an entire trunk when folded.
  • Suspension System and Lock: Quality suspension handles bumpy terrain without jarring your newborn, while a sturdy hand brake or foot brake gives you control on inclines and during runs. A front wheel lock is crucial when you're jogging or walking on varied terrain.

  • Avoid Full-Sized All-Terrain Joggers: Models like the BOB Revolution Pro are incredible for jogging, but they're oversized for travel—they weigh 30+ pounds and have awkward folds. Save these for when your child is older and you've settled into a routine without frequent airport trips.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use a jogger stroller for a newborn?

    Most jogger strollers are not recommended for newborns under 6 months old because they lack proper head and neck support, and many don't recline fully flat. However, some premium jogger strollers offer newborn attachments or deep recline positions that allow safe use from birth. Always check the manufacturer's minimum age and weight requirements before purchasing.

    What is the best jogger stroller for traveling on planes?

    Look for lightweight jogger strollers under 25 pounds that fold compactly, such as the BOB Gear Revolution Flex or Baby Trend Expedition Jogger, as these meet most airline gate-check requirements. Check your specific airline's stroller dimensions and weight limits, and consider a stroller travel bag to protect it during transport. Compact joggers typically fold smaller than full-size options, making them ideal for travel.

    Are jogger strollers good for everyday use or just jogging?

    Jogger strollers are excellent for both jogging and everyday use because of their superior suspension, maneuverability, and durability compared to standard strollers. They handle various terrains well, making them great for outdoor activities, but their larger size and weight may be less convenient for car travel or tight spaces. If you plan to jog regularly plus everyday errands, a jogger stroller is a practical all-in-one choice.

    What features should I look for in a jogger stroller for a newborn?

    Prioritize a fully reclined or flat position (at least 170 degrees), a safety harness, good suspension for bumpy terrain, and compatibility with a newborn insert or car seat adapter. Look for adjustable handlebars for different heights, a large canopy for sun protection, and puncture-resistant tires if you'll be jogging. Additionally, ensure the stroller is lightweight enough to travel with but sturdy enough to handle daily use.
