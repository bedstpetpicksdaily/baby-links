Frequently Asked Questions

How long can my newborn actually stay in this seat before we need an upgrade?

The KeyFit 35 is rated for babies up to 35 pounds, which typically means ages newborn to around 2-3 years depending on your child's growth curve. Babies tracking in the 95th percentile might hit that limit by 18 months; average-sized babies usually stay longer. When they outgrow it, you'll transition to a forward-facing seat. Keep your receipts if you want flexibility.

Is the base necessary or just a convenience item?

It's both, but lean convenience. You can use the KeyFit 35 without the base—strap it with your vehicle's seat belt—but you'll do this install once and then struggle every single time you switch cars. The base costs extra but genuinely saves time if you're shuffling kids between two cars or frequent car swaps. If you have one vehicle and rarely switch, save the money.

Does it fit all vehicle seat types, or are there compatibility issues?

LATCH compatibility is solid across modern cars (2006+), but some vehicles have awkwardly positioned anchors that make base installation tight. Test fit before buying if possible. Also note: not all taxis or ride-share vehicles have LATCH, so if you're Uber-dependent, confirm the seat belt installation feels secure in your typical vehicles.
