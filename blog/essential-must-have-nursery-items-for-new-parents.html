    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are the absolute must-have items for a newborn nursery?

    The essentials include a safe crib with a firm mattress, fitted sheets, and a waterproof mattress protector, plus a dresser or storage for clothes and supplies. You'll also need blackout curtains to help with sleep, a white noise machine to mask household sounds, and a reliable thermometer to monitor room temperature (ideal range is 68-72°F). A changing table or dresser-top changing pad, a small nightlight for nighttime diaper changes, and a humidifier for dry climates round out the basics.

    Do I really need a bassinet or can I just use a crib?

    A bassinet is helpful for the first 3-6 months since it fits beside your bed for easier nighttime feeding and monitoring, but it's not essential if space or budget is limited. Many parents skip it and use a crib from day one, though you may find yourself climbing out of bed more frequently for nighttime care. If you plan to bedshare safely or use a co-sleeper, a bassinet becomes unnecessary.

    What nursery furniture do I actually need versus what's just nice to have?

    You absolutely need a safe crib and a place to store clothes and supplies—a dresser works perfectly for both. A changing table or changing pad on top of a dresser is practical but not essential if you're willing to change diapers on the floor or bed. Everything else—like a glider chair, nightstand, or decorative items—falls into the 'nice to have' category and depends on your space, budget, and personal preference.

    What safety items should I buy for the nursery?

    Install a reliable baby monitor (video monitors are worth the investment for peace of mind), ensure your crib meets current safety standards with no bumpers or loose bedding, and use a firm mattress with fitted sheets only. Add outlet covers or safety plugs, secure any furniture to walls to prevent tip-overs, keep the room at a safe temperature with a thermometer, and avoid cords from blinds or monitors that could be a strangulation hazard.
