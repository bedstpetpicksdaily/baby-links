What to Look For

  • Rear-facing weight and height limits for infants: Look for convertible seats that accommodate newborns from 5-8 pounds and extend rear-facing to at least 40 pounds. Rear-facing is safest for infants' developing spines and necks, so prioritize seats with generous rear-facing capacity rather than those that transition to forward-facing quickly.
  • Newborn-friendly harness adjustments and inserts: Ensure the seat includes an infant head insert or pillow designed specifically for newborns' head support and proper harness positioning. The harness should adjust easily without rethreading, with multiple position options to accommodate your growing infant comfortably and safely.
  • Easy installation with reliable base options: Choose a convertible seat with either a secure LATCH system or one that installs firmly with the seat belt, minimizing movement. A quality base option (if available) can make installation more straightforward and give you greater confidence that your infant is properly secured.
