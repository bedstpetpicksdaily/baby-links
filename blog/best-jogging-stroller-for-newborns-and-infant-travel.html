If you're a parent who loves staying active, finding the right jogging stroller for your newborn or infant can feel overwhelming. Unlike regular strollers, jogging strollers need to provide the stability and suspension your little one needs while keeping up with your fitness routine. The good news is that with the right knowledge, you can find a stroller that keeps both you and your baby safe, comfortable, and happy during your runs.

What to Look For

  • Proper suspension and shock absorption – Your newborn's developing spine and brain need protection from impact. Look for strollers with dual suspension systems and pneumatic (air-filled) tires that absorb jolts from uneven terrain, not just foam-filled wheels.
  • A fully reclinable seat for young infants – Newborns cannot sit upright safely. Your jogging stroller should have a seat that reclines completely flat or nearly flat so your baby can lie back properly, with adequate head and neck support until they're old enough to sit up on their own (typically 6+ months).
  • A secure 5-point harness and canopy protection – When you're jogging, stability matters. A five-point harness (not just a three-point) keeps your infant secure during movement and turns, while a full canopy protects delicate skin from UV rays and wind during outdoor runs.

What to Avoid

  • Fixed, upright seats – Never choose a jogging stroller with a seat that doesn't recline significantly or lie flat. Newborns lack the neck strength to support their heads in upright positions during bumpy runs. This isn't just uncomfortable; it's unsafe for developing spines.
  • Lightweight jogging strollers with minimal suspension – While lightweight strollers sound convenient, many ultra-light jogging models use basic suspension systems designed for older children. Your newborn needs priority; choose a stroller with robust suspension even if it weighs a bit more.
  • Models without proper harness systems – Avoid three-point harnesses or strollers with unclear safety ratings. Your infant's security during your runs depends on a quality five-point harness that keeps them centered and protected, especially on turns or varied terrain.
