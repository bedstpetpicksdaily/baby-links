Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Joie Kubbie Sleep compare to the Chicco Next2Me or Arm's Reach Co-Sleeper?

The Kubbie Sleep's main advantage is its compact profile and one-handed drop-side—it moves easier and feels less intrusive in smaller bedrooms. The Chicco Next2Me is slightly cheaper but has a more rigid frame and smaller bassinet area. The Arm's Reach is more spacious but bulkier and typically costs more. Choose the Kubbie if you value mobility; choose Chicco if budget is primary; choose Arm's Reach if you want maximum sleeping surface for your infant.

Is the mattress really that thin, and should I buy a replacement immediately?

Yes, the included mattress is noticeably thinner than standalone crib mattresses. Most experienced parents either upgrade to a firmer, better-ventilated option within the first month or plan that $80-120 additional expense into their budget. It's not a dealbreaker, but it's a realistic cost most first-time buyers don't anticipate.

Will the Kubbie Sleep fit a queen or king bed, and how's the height adjustment?

The height adjustment covers most standard bed heights (around 18-24 inches), but if you have a platform bed or ultra-high mattress, measure before purchasing. The adjustment itself is smooth and tool-free, but you'll need to do it while empty—it's not a quick mid-night tweak. Test the fit in-store or confirm return policies if you're uncertain about your bed height.
