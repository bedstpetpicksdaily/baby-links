    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use a jogging stroller for a newborn?

    Most jogging strollers are not recommended for newborns under 6 months old because their neck and spine aren't developed enough to handle the vibrations and jolts from running on uneven terrain. You should wait until your baby is at least 6 months old and can sit upright with good head control before using a jogging stroller, or look for models specifically designed with newborn-compatible bassinets.

    What's the difference between a jogging stroller and a regular stroller?

    Jogging strollers have a fixed front wheel (instead of a swivel wheel), air-filled tires for better shock absorption, a hand brake, and a more aerodynamic design built to handle running and rough terrain. Regular strollers prioritize maneuverability and everyday use, while jogging strollers are engineered specifically for speed and stability during athletic activities.

    Is it safe to jog while pushing a baby stroller?

    Yes, jogging with a baby stroller is safe when using a proper jogging stroller and following safety guidelines like keeping a firm grip, staying visible with reflective gear, and avoiding busy roads. However, avoid jogging during pregnancy or in the first 6 weeks postpartum, and consult your doctor before starting any exercise routine with your baby.

    How much does a good jogging stroller cost?

    Quality jogging strollers typically range from $300-$800 depending on brand, features, and durability. Budget-friendly options start around $200-$300, while premium models with advanced suspension and extra features can exceed $1,000, so consider your needs and running frequency when deciding on your budget.
