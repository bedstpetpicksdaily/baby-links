Choosing the right car seat for your newborn or infant is one of the most critical safety decisions you'll make as a parent. Your baby will spend significant time in a car seat during those early months, whether for trips home from the hospital, pediatrician visits, or everyday errands. Getting the installation right—and selecting a seat with features that truly matter for this stage—can mean the difference between maximum protection and compromised safety.

What to Look For

  • Rear-Facing Design with Extended Weight Limits: Newborns and infants must ride rear-facing, and you'll want a seat that accommodates them through at least 35-40 pounds. Rear-facing seats provide superior protection for their developing spines and necks during crashes, so prioritize models that keep your little one safely rear-facing for as long as possible rather than rushing to forward-facing transitions.
  • Easy-to-Use LATCH System with Clear Installation Guides: The Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children (LATCH) system should be intuitive, with clearly marked anchor points and minimal slack when properly installed. Look for seats with color-coded indicators, audible clicks, and simple belt routing that you can confidently install correctly every single time—installation mistakes are common, so a foolproof design matters tremendously.
  • Adjustable Harness and Recline Angles: Newborns need proper recline positioning to keep their airways open and reduce strain on their neck and spine during long drives. Choose a seat with multiple recline positions and a harness system that grows with your baby, allowing you to adjust without purchasing a new seat as they gain weight and length.
