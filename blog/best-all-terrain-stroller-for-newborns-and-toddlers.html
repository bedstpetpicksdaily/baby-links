What to Look For in an All-Terrain Stroller

  • Suspension System and Tire Quality: Look for strollers with independent wheel suspension and air-filled or all-terrain tires (at least 12 inches in diameter). This setup absorbs bumps, rocks, and roots while keeping your newborn or toddler comfortable and your arms from bearing the shock on long woodland walks or beach outings.
  • Recline and Support for Newborns: Since you're using this from the newborn stage, verify the seat reclines fully flat or nearly flat, and the harness system securely supports younger babies. Some all-terrain strollers are designed for toddlers only, so confirm it's truly suitable from birth or compatible with a newborn insert.
  • Weight Capacity and Maneuverability: All-terrain strollers are heavier than standard options, typically 25-35 pounds, but they should still have responsive steering and a tight turning radius. Check the weight capacity to ensure it accommodates your growing child comfortably and won't become unusable after your second year of parenting.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the best all terrain stroller for newborns?

    The best all terrain strollers for newborns feature a fully reclined seat, excellent suspension, and large air-filled tires to handle rough surfaces while keeping your infant comfortable. Look for models with a reversible seat so your newborn can face you, and ensure the stroller has a sturdy frame that can support the weight of an infant car seat if you plan to use it as a travel system.

    Can you use an all terrain stroller from birth?

    Yes, many all terrain strollers can be used from birth, but only if they have a fully flat or near-flat recline position suitable for newborns. Always check the manufacturer's specifications, as some models require an infant insert or bassinet attachment, while others can accommodate a newborn directly in the seat.

    Are all terrain strollers safe for newborns?

    All terrain strollers are safe for newborns when they have proper head and neck support through a fully reclined seat and a 5-point harness system. The key is choosing a model designed specifically to accommodate newborns and ensuring it meets safety standards like JPMA certification.

    What features should I look for in an all terrain stroller for toddlers?

    For toddlers, prioritize all terrain strollers with a durable, comfortable seat that reclines partially, large puncture-resistant tires for rough terrain, and good suspension to absorb bumps. Additionally, look for features like a spacious storage basket, adjustable handlebar height, and lightweight construction for easy maneuverability on trails and uneven ground.
