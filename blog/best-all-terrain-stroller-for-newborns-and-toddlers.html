What to Look For in an All-Terrain Stroller

  • Suspension System and Tire Quality: Look for strollers with independent wheel suspension and air-filled or all-terrain tires (at least 12 inches in diameter). This setup absorbs bumps, rocks, and roots while keeping your newborn or toddler comfortable and your arms from bearing the shock on long woodland walks or beach outings.
  • Recline and Support for Newborns: Since you're using this from the newborn stage, verify the seat reclines fully flat or nearly flat, and the harness system securely supports younger babies. Some all-terrain strollers are designed for toddlers only, so confirm it's truly suitable from birth or compatible with a newborn insert.
  • Weight Capacity and Maneuverability: All-terrain strollers are heavier than standard options, typically 25-35 pounds, but they should still have responsive steering and a tight turning radius. Check the weight capacity to ensure it accommodates your growing child comfortably and won't become unusable after your second year of parenting.
