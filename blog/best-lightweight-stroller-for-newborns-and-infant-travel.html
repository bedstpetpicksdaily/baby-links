What to Look For

  • Newborn-Friendly Recline and Support: Your stroller must fully recline (ideally flat) to safely accommodate newborns who can't sit upright. Look for adequate head and neck support, as newborns lack the muscle control to support themselves. Many lightweight strollers compromise here, so verification that your newborn can lie completely flat is essential—not semi-reclined, but truly horizontal for proper spinal alignment and breathing.
  • Weight and Portability for Real Travel Scenarios: A lightweight stroller should weigh under 15 pounds and fold compactly enough to fit in airplane overhead bins or car trunks without requiring a gate check. Consider your actual travel patterns: if you're flying frequently with a newborn, the ability to collapse the stroller one-handed while holding an infant matters significantly more than extra storage capacity.
  • Smooth Suspension and Shock Absorption: Newborns are sensitive to jarring movements, and lightweight strollers often skimp on suspension systems. Prioritize models with adequate wheel suspension and smooth-rolling wheels that handle sidewalk cracks and uneven surfaces without transferring every bump to your sleeping infant. Test this in-store by gently pushing and feeling how the stroller responds to rough terrain.
