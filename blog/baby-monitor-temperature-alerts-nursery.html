Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature should a baby's nursery be?

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping a baby's room between 68-72°F (20-22°C) for safe sleep. Most experts agree that anywhere from 65-75°F is acceptable, but temperatures above 75°F or below 65°F can increase the risk of sleep issues and SIDS.

How do baby monitor temperature alerts work?

Smart baby monitors with temperature sensors continuously track the nursery temperature and send alerts to your phone or device when it goes outside your preset safe range. You can typically set high and low temperature thresholds, and the monitor will notify you immediately so you can adjust the thermostat or add/remove blankets.

Do temperature alert baby monitors actually prevent SIDS?

While temperature alerts help you maintain a safe sleep environment, they don't directly prevent SIDS. However, keeping your nursery in the recommended temperature range is one important factor in reducing SIDS risk, along with firm sleeping surfaces and back sleeping positions.
