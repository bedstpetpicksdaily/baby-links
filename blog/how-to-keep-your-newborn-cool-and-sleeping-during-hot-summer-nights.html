Create the Ideal Sleep Temperature

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping your baby's room between 68-72°F for optimal sleep. During summer months, this means strategic use of air conditioning, fans, or portable cooling units. Position a fan to circulate air without blowing directly on your baby, and consider blackout curtains to reduce heat gain from sunlight. If you don't have air conditioning, open windows during cooler evening hours and close them during the day to trap cool air inside. A room thermometer helps you monitor temperature accurately throughout the night.

Dress Your Baby Appropriately for Warm Nights

Resist the urge to bundle your newborn in heavy blankets during summer. Instead, opt for lightweight, breathable sleepwear made from natural fabrics like cotton or bamboo. A single lightweight sleep sack rated for warmer temperatures is ideal—it keeps your baby safely contained without overheating. Avoid hats, mittens, and extra layers unless your pediatrician specifically recommends them. Many parents find that a onesie or light pajamas are sufficient on warm nights, allowing heat to dissipate while keeping their baby comfortable.

Optimize Your Crib Setup for Cooling

Your crib setup plays a major role in temperature regulation. Use fitted sheets made from breathable materials, and skip the bumper pads, pillows, and heavy blankets that trap heat. Consider a breathable crib mattress pad designed to improve airflow. Keep the crib away from direct sunlight and exterior walls that absorb and radiate heat. If your baby uses a sleep sack, choose a tog rating appropriate for warm weather—look for 0.5 or 1.0 tog ratings rather than heavier options. Proper ventilation around the crib allows air to circulate freely, preventing your baby from becoming too warm during the night.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe for newborns to sleep in air conditioning all night?

Yes, air conditioning is safe for newborns when the room temperature is maintained between 68-72°F. The key is avoiding extreme temperature fluctuations and ensuring your baby is dressed appropriately for the cooler air. Monitor your baby's temperature by feeling the back of their neck—it should feel warm, not hot or cold.

Can white noise machines help my baby sleep better in summer?

Absolutely. White noise machines like the Baby Shusher are particularly valuable during summer when outdoor noises increase and sunrise comes earlier. The consistent sound masks disruptive noises and creates a familiar, soothing environment that helps babies fall asleep faster and stay asleep longer.

What should my newborn wear to sleep on hot nights?

A lightweight sleep sack (0.5-1.0 tog rating) paired with a single layer of light pajamas or a onesie is ideal for hot nights. Avoid blankets, hats, and extra layers. Choose fabrics like cotton or bamboo that breathe well and wick moisture away from your baby's skin.

How can I tell if my baby is overheating at night?

Check the back of your baby's neck and upper back—if these areas feel sweaty or excessively warm to the touch, your baby may be overheating. Reduce layers or lower the room temperature slightly. Conversely, if your baby's hands and feet are cold, they may need an additional lightweight layer.

Are portable white noise machines worth the investment?

Yes, especially during summer. Portable devices like the Baby Shusher provide flexibility to use the same soothing sounds across different rooms. The investment pays dividends through improved sleep quality, reduced nighttime disruptions, and a more consistent sleep routine for your baby.
