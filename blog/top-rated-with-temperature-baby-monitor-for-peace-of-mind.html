Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature range should a baby monitor have?

Most pediatricians recommend keeping a baby's room between 68-72°F (20-22°C), and a good temperature monitor should display readings within this range and alert you if it goes above 75°F or below 65°F. Look for monitors with accuracy of at least ±1-2 degrees Fahrenheit to ensure reliable readings for your nursery.

Can you use a temperature baby monitor without WiFi?

Yes, many top-rated temperature monitors work on closed networks that don't require WiFi or internet connection, using secure parent-unit receivers instead. These are ideal if you want to avoid WiFi concerns, though WiFi-enabled models allow you to check temperatures remotely from anywhere using your smartphone.

Do temperature baby monitors work through walls?

Most non-WiFi temperature monitors have a range of 700-1000 feet in open space but typically 100-300 feet through walls and obstacles depending on the model. WiFi-enabled monitors work anywhere you have internet connectivity, making them more reliable for multi-story homes or when you're away from your house.

What's the difference between a temperature monitor and a video baby monitor with temperature?

A temperature-only monitor focuses solely on room temperature with audio alerts, while combo video monitors add camera capability so you can see and hear your baby plus monitor temperature. Combo monitors cost more but provide comprehensive nursery monitoring, whereas temperature-only monitors are budget-friendly if you already have a separate video camera.
