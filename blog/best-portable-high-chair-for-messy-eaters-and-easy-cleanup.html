Feeding time with a messy eater can feel like managing a small disaster zone, especially when you're traveling or eating out. What to Look For

  • Wipeable, non-porous materials everywhere: Look for high chairs with vinyl or silicone trays, polyurethane seat covers, and plastic frames—avoid fabric padding or canvas that traps food and stains. The entire chair should be cleanable with a single wipe-down, including crevices where crumbs hide.
  • Removable and dishwasher-safe trays: A detachable tray that fits in your dishwasher saves enormous amounts of time compared to hand-washing or scrubbing dried food from hard-to-reach spots. Make sure the tray is large enough to catch side spills and has raised edges.
  • Compact, lightweight design for transport: The best portable high chair weighs under 10 pounds and folds to fit in a car trunk or diaper bag. You'll only use it if it's genuinely easy to bring along, so weight and packability are non-negotiable for this situation.

What to Avoid

  • High chairs with fabric seat covers or padding: Even machine-washable fabric becomes a nightmare when your child has ground applesauce or cheese into every fiber. Once food dries in fabric, it's nearly impossible to fully clean, and you'll end up replacing the cover frequently.
