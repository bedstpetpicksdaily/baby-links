Frequently Asked Questions

Will the silicone degrade if I use an electric sterilizer?

Food-grade silicone holds up well to electric sterilization, but repeated extreme heat exposure (boiling water or extended high-heat cycles) will eventually cause degradation. Most parents find that alternating between sterilizer cycles and dishwasher cleaning extends bottle life considerably. Check Nanobébé's specific guidance for your sterilizer model, as some have lower heat settings that work better with silicone.

Are these bottles compatible with standard bottle nipples?

Nanobébé bottles come with their own silicone nipples that are sized to fit their specific rim design. You can sometimes fit standard nipples if they're the right size range, but fit isn't guaranteed and may be loose or uncomfortable for your baby. Stick with Nanobébé nipples designed for the bottle to avoid leaking or improper latch.

How long does the silicone actually last before it needs replacement?

With proper care (daily rinsing, avoiding prolonged sunlight exposure, not storing with caps sealed too tightly), most parents get 12-18 months of active use before silicone begins showing signs of wear or staining that affects appearance. If you're using bottles through multiple kids or storing them long-term between children, silicone degradation happens faster. Plan on replacement costs if you're thinking beyond a single child's feeding journey.
