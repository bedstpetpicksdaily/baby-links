The Limitations of AC Alone for Baby Sleep

Air conditioning cools the room, but it doesn't address the full spectrum of sleep challenges babies face in summer. A cold room alone won't stop your baby from waking to neighborhood noise, traffic sounds, or household activity. Additionally, many parents worry that running AC constantly increases energy bills and may expose babies to uneven temperature zones. Babies also tend to have difficulty self-soothing when startled by sudden sounds, which is where white noise becomes invaluable.

How White Noise Machines Create the Ideal Sleep Environment

When combined with a cool room temperature, white noise addresses both physical comfort and psychological security, leading to deeper, longer sleep sessions.

Yes, white noise is safe for babies when used appropriately. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping white noise devices at safe sound levels (below 85 decibels) to prevent hearing damage. Baby Shusher is designed with infant safety as the priority and operates at appropriate volumes.

Absolutely! Baby Shusher works excellently alongside AC. Use AC to maintain a cool temperature (65-70°F is ideal for babies), and use Baby Shusher to mask external noises. This combination creates the most effective summer sleep environment.
