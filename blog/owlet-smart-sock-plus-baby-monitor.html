Frequently Asked Questions

Can the Owlet Smart Sock Plus replace my video monitor?

No. It monitors vitals, not video. You still need to see your baby to know they're awake, crying, or in an unsafe sleep position. Most parents use both simultaneously. The Smart Sock Plus is a complement to video monitoring, not a replacement.

How often do you actually need to replace the sock?

Realistically, every 3-4 months depending on laundry frequency and how messy your baby is. The material degrades and the elastic stretches, compromising the fit that's essential for accurate readings. Budget $40-60 annually for replacements beyond the initial purchase.

Does it work for babies under 6 lbs or over 30 lbs?

The sizing range is 5-30 lbs, which covers roughly 2 weeks to 3 years for most babies. Below 5 lbs and the sock won't fit securely enough for reliable data. Above 30 lbs, the design doesn't accommodate. If you plan to use it across multiple children or have a smaller newborn, this is a real limitation.

What's the learning curve for setting it up?

Expect 15-20 minutes total. The app guides you through WiFi pairing and sock fitting. The trickiest part is finding the correct sock size for your baby—too tight restricts circulation, too loose doesn't read properly. The sizing chart in the manual is accurate, so follow it exactly.

How does this compare to the regular Owlet Smart Sock (non-Plus)?

The Plus model includes improved heart rate sensors and extended app history. For the price difference (usually $50-75 more), the Plus version is worth it if you want detailed trend data. The original model works fine for basic monitoring, but Plus users get more sophisticated notifications and better historical tracking.
