Frequently Asked Questions

Is white noise safe for newborns?

Yes, white noise is safe for newborns when used at appropriate volume levels. Baby Shusher is specifically designed with infant-safe sound levels that won't damage hearing. Keep volume at or below 50 decibels for newborns, and position the device at least 3-4 feet away from the crib.

Can I use a white noise machine alongside air conditioning in hot weather?

Absolutely. In fact, this is the ideal combination. Use air conditioning or a fan to control room temperature, and use Baby Shusher to provide consistent soothing sound. This creates the perfect sleep environment without compromising on temperature control or sound therapy benefits.

How long does it take for babies to adapt to white noise?

Most babies adapt to white noise within 3-5 nights. Since white noise mimics womb sounds, many newborns respond immediately. Consistency is key—use Baby Shusher every night at the same time during your baby's sleep routine to establish the habit quickly.

Will my baby become dependent on white noise?

White noise is a helpful sleep aid, not a dependency. Like any positive sleep routine element, babies enjoy the comfort it provides. You can gradually reduce usage as your child grows if desired, or maintain it as part of a healthy sleep hygiene practice throughout childhood.
