White Noise Machines for Baby Sleep: Baby Shusher vs. Marpac Dohm-UNO Compared

By Best Baby Picks Daily • July 20, 2026 • Contains affiliate links

White noise machines have become a staple in modern nurseries, but not all sleep aids work the same way. Two products dominate parent discussions: the Baby Shusher, which uses a scientifically-designed shushing sound, and the Marpac Dohm-UNO, a traditional white noise generator. Both claim to help babies fall asleep faster, but they take fundamentally different approaches to the problem.

This guide compares these two options head-to-head across safety, sound quality, usability, and actual parent outcomes. We've reviewed the evidence and specifications to help you choose the right tool for your family's sleep struggles.

Baby Shusher

The Baby Shusher is a compact, battery-operated device that produces a consistent shushing sound engineered to replicate a mother's natural shushing. It features adjustable volume controls and a 20-minute auto-shutoff timer. The device is designed to trigger the Moro reflex in newborns—a natural calming response—making it particularly effective for fussy infants. Its portable design allows easy transport between rooms, to daycare, or while traveling.

Best for: Parents seeking a portable, specialized solution for newborn fussiness; families who value the science behind mimicking womb sounds; travel and multi-room use.

Marpac Dohm-UNO

The Dohm-UNO is a traditional white noise machine that uses a fan-based mechanism to generate natural, unlooped white noise. It offers adjustable sound quality through a simple dial, with no batteries or digital components. The device operates quietly and reliably, making it a long-term investment that can grow with your child from infancy through toddlerhood and beyond.

Best for: Parents prioritizing durability and long-term value; families wanting non-digital, mechanical simplicity; households seeking genuine white noise over synthetic sounds.

Our Verdict

Both products are legitimate choices, but they serve different parental priorities. Choose the Baby Shusher if portability and the science of infant reflexes matter most to you. Its shushing sound is specifically engineered to calm newborns, the 20-minute timer removes guesswork from sleep routines, and it works in any room without outlet access. Parents consistently report faster sleep onset with this device. However, battery dependency is a real drawback for overnight use.

Choose the Marpac Dohm-UNO if you prioritize long-term durability and want a machine that will reliably serve your child through multiple years of development. The real choice comes down to lifestyle: portability and specialized infant acoustics (Baby Shusher) versus mechanical reliability and multi-year usability (Marpac Dohm-UNO).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is white noise safe for babies?

Yes, white noise is recognized by pediatricians as a safe, non-medicinal sleep aid. The key is volume control—machines should operate between 50-70 decibels to protect infant hearing. Both the Baby Shusher and Marpac Dohm-UNO offer adjustable volume to stay within safe ranges. Always verify that any machine meets CPSC standards and uses non-toxic materials.

What's the difference between white noise, pink noise, and brown noise?

White noise contains equal power across all frequencies (like static). Pink noise emphasizes lower frequencies (softer, more natural-sounding rainfall). Brown noise is even lower-frequency (resembling a deep rumble). The Baby Shusher uses a specialized shushing frequency, while the Marpac Dohm-UNO produces true white noise. Research suggests all three can be effective for infants; the best choice depends on your baby's response.

How long should I run a white noise machine for my baby?

The Baby Shusher auto-shutoff after 20 minutes, which covers the typical window for newborns to enter deep sleep. The Marpac Dohm-UNO has no timer, giving you flexibility to run it throughout the night or nap. Most pediatricians recommend using white noise consistently during sleep routines rather than sporadically, as babies respond better to predictable acoustic cues.

Can I use a white noise app on my phone instead?

Apps are cheaper upfront but introduce risks: phone notifications interrupt sleep, blue light exposure can disrupt circadian rhythms, and batteries drain faster. Dedicated machines like these two options eliminate those distractions and provide consistently clean sound without digital interference. They're also safer around infants than unsecured phones.

Which machine is better for travel?

The Baby Shusher is designed for portability with battery operation and compact size. The Marpac Dohm-UNO is heavier and requires an outlet, making it less ideal for frequent travel. If you move between multiple sleeping locations regularly, the Baby Shusher is the clear winner. If travel is occasional, the Dohm-UNO's durability may justify keeping it stationary in the primary nursery.
