What to Look For

  • Universal LATCH compatibility with flexible attachment options: Look for booster seats that work with both LATCH systems and vehicle seat belts, since older vehicles may lack LATCH anchors, while newer luxury SUVs often have limited spacing between anchor points. A seat with adjustable guide loops and multiple belt routing options gives you flexibility across different cars.
  • Compact width that fits three-across in sedans but doesn't shift in larger vehicles: Measure your vehicle's back seat width before purchasing—sedans often struggle to fit three booster seats, while large SUVs create gaps that let seats slide during turns. The best booster seats for multi-vehicle homes are slim enough for compact cars but have deep side wings or anti-slip bases for larger vehicles.
  • High-back or backless options with excellent side-impact protection: If you're moving the seat between vehicles frequently, a convertible high-back/backless option maximizes versatility. Prioritize seats with deep, reinforced side panels and properly positioned headrests, since these features matter more in vehicles with less structural support around the back seat.
