What to Look For

  • Full Recline and Newborn Support: Your jogging stroller must recline completely flat or near-flat for newborns under 6 months, as their spine and neck need proper alignment during bumpy runs. Look for models with excellent head and neck support cushioning, and verify that the recline doesn't compromise visibility or wheel performance.
  • Lightweight Yet Sturdy Frame: When traveling by plane or car, weight matters significantly—aim for jogging strollers under 25 pounds. However, this lighter frame must still handle road vibrations smoothly without transmitting shock to your newborn, making suspension quality more important than raw heftiness.
  • Travel-Friendly Collapse and Compatibility: Choose strollers that fold compactly and fit standard car trunks or airplane overhead bins. Additionally, confirm whether it's compatible with your car seat brand, as many premium jogging strollers accept major car seat brands via adapters—this is essential for seamless travel transitions from car to stroller without waking your newborn.
