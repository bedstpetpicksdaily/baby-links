The Best Baby Stroller for Newborns and Toddlers

The best baby stroller for newborns and toddlers is a convertible stroller that offers a fully flat bassinet mode for newborns and transitions to a forward-facing seat for older babies. Models like the UPPAbaby Vista, Bugaboo Cameleon, or Baby Jogger City Select are top choices because they grow with your child and eliminate the need to purchase multiple strollers.

The Short Answer

A convertible or modular stroller is your best investment because it accommodates both newborns and toddlers with proper support at each stage. These strollers feature a fully reclined bassinet position for newborns (meeting the safety requirement of completely flat sleeping surfaces), which converts to an upright toddler seat as your child grows. Quality convertible strollers typically last from birth through age 3-4, making them more cost-effective than buying separate infant and toddler strollers. Look for models with smooth suspension, durable frames, and reversible seats that face toward you or the world.

The Full Explanation

When shopping for a stroller that works for both newborns and toddlers, you need to understand the distinct requirements of each stage. Newborns require a completely flat sleeping surface—pediatricians and safety organizations recommend against inclined car seats or partially reclined stroller seats for extended periods. This is why bassinets are essential for the first 4-6 months. As your baby becomes a toddler (around 6 months and beyond), they'll want more interaction with their surroundings and will benefit from an upright, forward-facing position.

Convertible strollers solve this problem by offering both configurations in one unit. The best models feature:

  • Full recline capability: A seat that lies completely flat for newborn safety and comfort
  • Reversible seats: The ability to face the bassinet/seat toward you (parent-facing) or toward the world (forward-facing)
  • Durable construction: Lightweight yet sturdy frames that handle both gentle newborn rides and active toddler adventures
  • Smooth suspension: Quality shock absorption that protects your newborn's developing spine
  • Generous storage: Deep baskets to hold diaper bags, groceries, and toddler items
  • Easy maneuverability: One-handed steering and smooth turning in tight spaces like stores and crowded areas

Beyond the stroller itself, consider your lifestyle. If you're in an urban environment, you might prefer a more compact stroller. If you drive frequently and use a car seat, look for strollers compatible with major car seat brands, allowing you to create a travel system where your infant car seat clicks directly into the stroller frame.

What the Experts Say

Pediatricians and child safety experts emphasize that newborns should not spend extended periods in reclined car seats or semi-reclined stroller seats. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends using a fully flat surface for sleeping. Leading parenting experts note that investing in one quality convertible stroller saves money, reduces clutter, and provides better support across your child's early years compared to purchasing multiple strollers. Product reviewers consistently highlight that the best strollers for the newborn-to-toddler journey are those with smooth suspension, lightweight frames, and intuitive seat conversion mechanisms that parents can manage one-handed.

Frequently Asked Questions

What features should a newborn stroller have?

A newborn stroller should have a fully reclinable seat that lies completely flat, a sturdy frame, and good suspension to protect your baby's developing spine. Look for models with a large canopy for sun protection, adjustable handlebar height, and a spacious storage basket for diaper bags and essentials.

Can you use the same stroller for newborns and toddlers?

Yes, many convertible strollers accommodate both newborns and toddlers by offering a fully flat recline for infants and an upright position for older babies. These dual-purpose strollers save money and space, though you may need a separate infant car seat attachment or bassinet adapter for newborns under 6 months.

What is the best lightweight stroller for travel with a newborn?

The best lightweight stroller for newborns should weigh under 15 pounds while still offering a fully flat recline and good suspension—models like the Cybex Gazelle or UPPAbaby Minu are popular choices. However, ensure it's airline-approved if traveling frequently and check that the recline is truly flat rather than partially reclined.

Do I need a bassinet attachment or infant car seat for a newborn stroller?

You don't need both, but one is recommended since newborns shouldn't stay in a stroller seat for extended periods. A bassinet attachment or compatible infant car seat frame allows your newborn to lie flat and be easily moved between car and stroller without disturbing their sleep.
