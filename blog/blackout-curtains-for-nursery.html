Frequently Asked Questions

Are blackout curtains safe for nurseries?

Yes, when properly installed. Choose curtains with cordless or inaccessible cord systems to prevent entanglement hazards. Mount rods securely and ensure no dangling cords are within reach of your baby.

What percentage of light should nursery blackout curtains block?

Look for curtains that block 99-100% of light. This complete darkness promotes melatonin production and helps establish consistent sleep schedules for infants and toddlers.

How do blackout curtains improve baby sleep?

Darkness triggers melatonin production, your baby's natural sleep hormone. By eliminating light disruptions from windows, blackout curtains help regulate circadian rhythms and reduce sleep interruptions.

Can blackout curtains help with daytime naps?

Absolutely. Even during daylight hours, blackout curtains create the darkness needed for quality naps. This is especially helpful for maintaining nap schedules and ensuring your baby gets sufficient daytime rest.

What size blackout curtains do I need for a nursery?

Measure your window width and height, then add extra width for light coverage. Most nurseries need 84-96 inch length curtains. Choose width that extends 8-12 inches beyond the window frame on each side.
