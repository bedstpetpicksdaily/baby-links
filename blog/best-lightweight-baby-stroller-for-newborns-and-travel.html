Choosing the right stroller for a newborn who will travel frequently requires balancing portability with safety and comfort—two things that don't always come easily together. A lightweight stroller that's truly suitable for newborns is harder to find than you'd think, since many ultra-light options lack the recline and support newborns need. This guide walks you through exactly what matters when you're shopping for a stroller that won't weigh you down at the airport but won't compromise your baby's well-being either.

What to Look For

  • Full recline capability: Newborns can't sit upright, so your stroller must recline completely flat or nearly flat. Look for strollers that go to at least 170 degrees of recline—anything less forces your baby's head forward and isn't safe for napping during travel.
  • Weight under 15 pounds: For frequent travelers, aim for strollers that weigh 12-15 pounds maximum. This is light enough to carry through airports and lift in and out of car trunks without exhaustion, but still substantial enough to feel stable and safe pushing at normal speeds.
  • Compact fold size: The stroller should fold into a package roughly the size of a carry-on bag or smaller. Test the fold in person if possible—some "compact" strollers are deceivingly chunky, and you'll regret the purchase when you're trying to squeeze it into overhead bins or car spaces.
