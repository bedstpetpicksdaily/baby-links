    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the safest infant car seat for newborns?

    Rear-facing infant car seats are the safest option for newborns as they support the head, neck, and spine during crashes. Top-rated models like the Graco SnugRide 35 LiteX and Chicco KeyFit 30 consistently earn the highest safety ratings from organizations like NHTSA and IIHS. Look for seats with robust side-impact protection, a five-point harness system, and easy-to-use installation features.

    How long should a baby stay in an infant car seat?

    Most infant car seats are designed for babies from birth up to 30-35 pounds, typically lasting until 12-24 months depending on the model. Once your child reaches the weight or height limit specified by the manufacturer, you should transition to a convertible car seat that can be used rear-facing for longer. Never keep a child in an infant seat past the manufacturer's maximum weight or height limits for safety reasons.

    Does the safest car seat matter if installation is wrong?

    No—incorrect installation significantly reduces a car seat's effectiveness and can negate its safety features. Studies show that over 70% of car seats are installed incorrectly, which is why proper installation is just as critical as choosing a safe seat. Use the LATCH system (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) or the vehicle's seat belt, and consider getting your installation checked by a certified child passenger safety technician.

    What features should I look for in a safe newborn car seat?

    Choose a seat with a sturdy base, a five-point harness system, side-impact protection, and easy harness adjustment. Additional safety features like anti-rebound bars, crash-tested construction, and compatibility with your vehicle type are important. Ensure the seat has clear installation instructions and that it fits securely in your vehicle with minimal movement.
