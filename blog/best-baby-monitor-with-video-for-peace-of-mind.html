What to Look For

  • Crystal-Clear Night Vision with Infrared Technology – You need to see your baby's face and movements clearly in complete darkness without waking them with visible light. Look for monitors with infrared LEDs that provide black-and-white or thermal imaging so you can check on them instantly, whether it's 2 AM or during daytime naps.
  • Two-Way Talk-Back Audio – Being able to speak to your baby through the monitor helps soothe them remotely if they wake up fussy, and allows you to respond immediately without always running to the nursery. Clear, natural-sounding audio in both directions matters more than you might think when you're trying to comfort your child from another room.
  • Reliable Connection with Strong Range – Whether you choose WiFi or a closed digital connection, your monitor needs consistent signal strength throughout your home and ideally your yard. Battery life on the parent unit should last through the night, and connectivity should be secure so you can check in whether you're upstairs, downstairs, or away from home.
