The Short Answer

The absolute must-haves for a newborn nursery are a safe sleeping space (crib or bassinet), a quality mattress with several fitted sheets, a changing surface, and adequate storage for clothes and supplies. Beyond these basics, good lighting, blackout curtains for naps, and climate control make the space more functional and comfortable for both you and your baby. Everything you choose should prioritize safety and accessibility, as you'll be navigating this space multiple times throughout the day and night.

The Full Explanation

Safe Sleep Surface

The cornerstone of any nursery is a safe sleeping space. You have two main options: a full-sized crib or a smaller bassinet. Cribs offer more longevity (usable from newborn through toddler years), while bassinets take up less space and keep your baby closer during those early months. Whatever you choose, ensure it meets current safety standards, has a firm mattress, and fits snugly with no gaps where a baby could get trapped.

Mattress and Bedding

A firm, well-fitting mattress is non-negotiable for safe sleep. Pair it with at least 3-4 fitted sheets so you always have a clean one available. Avoid soft bedding, pillows, bumpers, and blankets in the sleep space, as these increase the risk of suffocation. Waterproof mattress protectors are essential for preventing damage from inevitable accidents.

Changing Station

A dedicated changing area saves your back and keeps supplies organized. This can be a standalone changing table, a dresser with a changing pad on top, or even a wall-mounted changing station. The key is having everything within arm's reach—diapers, wipes, creams, and a place to safely set your baby while you gather supplies.

Storage Solutions

Newborns generate an impressive amount of laundry and supplies. You'll need drawers or shelving for clothes, diapers, wipes, extra sheets, blankets, and toiletries. A dresser that doubles as a changing station maximizes space, while open shelving keeps frequently-used items accessible. Don't underestimate storage needs—most parents wish they had more.

Lighting

Proper lighting is crucial for nighttime diaper changes and feeding without fully waking yourself or your baby. Install a dimmer switch on overhead lights and add a soft nightlight or small lamp. Many parents appreciate warm-toned, adjustable lighting that won't startle a sleeping baby but provides enough visibility for safe care.

Temperature and Air Quality

Newborns can't regulate their temperature well, so maintain a comfortable room temperature (68-72°F is ideal). A white noise machine helps with sleep and masks household sounds. If you live in a dry climate, a humidifier prevents congestion and keeps skin comfortable.

Window Coverings

Blackout curtains or shades help establish healthy sleep patterns by blocking external light during naps and nighttime sleep. They also help regulate room temperature by blocking heat in summer and retaining warmth in winter.

What the Experts Say

The American Academy of Pediatrics emphasizes that the safest sleep environment is a bare crib, bassinet, play yard, or portable play yard that meets current safety standards
