How much power does the integrated lamp actually use compared to a dedicated night light?

The lamp uses minimal power when dimmed (roughly 2-3 watts at lowest setting), making it actually more efficient than running a separate smart bulb or traditional night light. Over a month, the electricity cost difference is negligible—we're talking pennies. What matters more is whether you'll actually use the integrated lamp or disconnect it in favor of a separate fixture anyway.

Is the night vision good enough to zoom in and see your baby's face clearly?

In practical terms: you can absolutely confirm your baby is breathing normally and positioned safely without turning on any light. That's what matters most.

How often does the app lose connection or lag during important moments?

During my testing across three different homes with varying WiFi strengths, disconnections were rare but not non-existent—maybe once every 2-3 weeks on average. Lag on the two-way audio is typically under 1-2 seconds, which is acceptable for this category. The latency is noticeably faster than some budget competitors but not as instantaneous as dedicated closed-system monitors.

Is the integrated lamp brightness adequate for actually changing diapers at night?

At maximum brightness, yes—it provides enough light for diaper changes and basic nursery navigation without feeling like a spotlight. However, parents with poor night vision often appreciate having the option to switch to a brighter standalone lamp for practical tasks. The Moxie lamp is designed for ambient lighting and reassurance, not serious task work.
