What to Look For

Not all temperature sensors are created equal. Look for sleepsuits using medical-grade thermistors that measure skin temperature directly (not ambient room temperature). Accuracy within ±0.2°C matters—a delayed or inaccurate reading defeats the purpose. Check that the sensor is positioned on the torso where it captures your baby's core temperature, and verify that the manufacturer has third-party testing data.

2. Real-Time Alerts and Customizable Thresholds

The best sleepsuits let you set temperature ranges appropriate for your baby's age and your room conditions. Alerts should notify you immediately via app notification, sound, or vibration if temperatures exceed safe parameters. Your sleepsuit must meet CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) standards and be free of strangulation hazards.
