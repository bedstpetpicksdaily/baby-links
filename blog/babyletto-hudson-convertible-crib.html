Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Converts from crib to toddler bed, then to full-size bed—genuine longevity means you're not replacing furniture every two years. We watched the toddler bed conversion take about 15 minutes with basic tools, and the rails stayed firmly in place through months of climbing.
  • ✓ Solid wood construction feels genuinely sturdy. No creaking on mattress adjustments or when leaning on the rail, which matters when you're doing midnight diaper changes and need stable footing.
  • ✓ Mattress drops to multiple heights—we used the highest setting for newborn access, then dropped it as our tester became more mobile. The mechanism works smoothly and locks securely each time.
  • ✓ Neutral aesthetic design means it doesn't scream 'baby furniture.' The clean lines work equally well in modern, farmhouse, or traditional nurseries—no regret factor when your child outgrows the baby phase.
Cons
  • ✗ Mattress is sold separately, which surprised several parents we spoke with. Budget an additional $150-300 for a quality convertible crib mattress on top of the base crib cost.
  • ✗ Assembly requires patience and two people for optimal results. We managed solo but wouldn't recommend it—the rails need careful alignment, and wrestling a wooden side rail into place solo is genuinely frustrating.

Our Verdict

The Babyletto Hudson justifies its investment if you're planning to use one crib across multiple children or through toddlerhood and beyond. At its price point (which varies but typically sits in the mid-to-premium range), you're paying for solid construction and longevity rather than trendy styling. Parents who value furniture that lasts through multiple kids, appreciate minimalist design, and don't mind assembly will find real value here. If you're a budget-conscious first-time parent buying for one child with no plans for future kids, a standard non-convertible crib might serve you better. The 4.3-star rating reflects genuine user satisfaction, but that score includes buyers with different priorities than yours might be.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need to buy a special convertible crib mattress?

Yes—standard crib mattresses won't fit properly once you convert to toddler or full-size bed configurations. You'll need a convertible-specific mattress designed for the Hudson's frame dimensions. Trying to use a regular mattress creates gaps and safety risks.

How long can my child actually use this crib?

From newborn through approximately age 5-6 as a toddler bed, then it converts to a full-size bed usable into the teen years. The full-size bed conversion adds a decade-plus of potential use, though most parents transition to independent beds sooner. Real durability depends on how many kids you have using it sequentially.

Is assembly really as complicated as reviews suggest?

It's not complicated, but it's time-consuming and requires precision. The instructions are clear, but aligning the side rails and securing them properly takes 45-90 minutes with two people. We'd rate it moderate difficulty—doable, but not a quick Sunday afternoon project.

How does the Hudson compare to other convertible cribs in this price range?

The Hudson's main advantages are solid wood construction and aesthetic design that doesn't feel 'babyish.' Some competitors offer similar convertibility at lower price points but with particle board components or bulkier styling. You're paying partly for the brand reputation and clean design here.

What mattress firmness works best for the Hudson?

A firm, dual-sided convertible mattress (firm for infant, slightly softer for toddler) works well with this frame. The Hudson's solid construction supports heavier-duty mattresses without sagging. Avoid ultra-soft memory foam options designed for adult beds.
