Frequently Asked Questions

What are the bare minimum essentials needed for a newborn nursery?

The absolute must-haves are a safe sleep space (crib, bassinet, or play yard), a firm mattress with fitted sheets, a dresser or storage for clothes and supplies, and adequate lighting including a dimmer or nightlight. You'll also want blackout curtains or shades to help with sleep schedules and temperature control through a fan or humidifier, depending on your climate.

Do I really need a changing table or can I use a dresser?

A dedicated changing table is not essential—many parents successfully use a dresser with a changing pad on top, a bed, or even a portable changing pad on the floor. What matters most is having a safe, flat surface at a comfortable height with storage nearby for diapers, wipes, and clothing to minimize reaching and bending.

What furniture should I skip to keep a minimal nursery?

You can skip non-essentials like gliders, rocking chairs, decorative shelving units, and large toy storage initially—many parents rock babies in other rooms or use budget alternatives. Focus your budget on the crib, mattress, and storage first, then add comfort items like a chair later if you find you need a dedicated nursing or soothing space.

Is a humidifier necessary for a baby's nursery?

A humidifier is helpful but not absolutely essential—it becomes more important if you live in a dry climate, during winter months, or if your baby has congestion or eczema. If you skip it initially, you can always add one later; focus first on temperature control and clean air circulation with a fan.
