Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a travel system stroller and a regular stroller?

A travel system stroller includes a compatible infant car seat that clicks directly into the stroller frame, eliminating the need to buy them separately and making transitions between car and stroller seamless. Regular strollers require you to purchase a car seat independently and don't have the click-in compatibility feature. Travel systems are ideal for newborns, while regular strollers work better once your child outgrows the infant seat (around 12-15 months).

Do I really need a video baby monitor or is audio enough?

Audio monitors are sufficient if you're in a small home or apartment and can hear your baby easily, but video monitors provide peace of mind by letting you check on your baby without entering the room, which is especially helpful during sleep training. Video monitors with night vision and temperature sensors are worth the investment if you have a larger home, multiple floors, or want to monitor naps throughout the day without disturbing your baby.

What are the must-have nursery essentials for a newborn?

The absolute essentials are a safe sleep space (crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets CPSC standards), a firm mattress with fitted sheets, a dresser for storage, blackout curtains for better sleep, and a white noise machine to mask household sounds. You'll also need a changing table or dresser-top changing pad, proper lighting (a dimmable lamp is crucial for nighttime changes), and adequate storage for diapers, wipes, and clothes within arm's reach of the changing area.

What's the best bottle and feeding system for breastfed babies switching to bottles?

Anti-colic bottles with slow-flow nipples (like Tommee Tippee or Dr. Brown's) are recommended for breastfed babies because they mimic natural breastfeeding and reduce nipple confusion and gas. Look for bottles with nipples labeled 0+ months, and introduce bottles around 4-6 weeks after breastfeeding is established; having multiple bottles (8-10) prevents constant washing if you're exclusively pumping or combination feeding.
