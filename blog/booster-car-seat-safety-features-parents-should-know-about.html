As your child grows out of their forward-facing car seat, booster seats become the next critical safety layer—and choosing the right one can mean the difference between proper protection and dangerous gaps in coverage. Booster seats are designed for children who are too tall or heavy for their harness-equipped car seats but not yet ready to use the vehicle's adult seat belt alone. Understanding the specific safety features that matter most will help you make a decision that keeps your growing child secure for years to come.

What to Look For

  • High-Back vs. Backless Design with Proper Support: High-back boosters offer superior head and neck protection, especially important for side-impact crashes and vehicles without headrests. If you choose a backless booster, ensure your vehicle's seat has a proper headrest and that your child meets the minimum height requirements (usually 4'9"). High-back options provide that safety net regardless of your vehicle's seating configuration.
  • Belt Positioning Guides and Lap/Shoulder Belt Fit: The most critical safety feature is how well the booster positions your vehicle's seat belt across your child's body. Look for clearly marked belt guides that ensure the lap belt sits low across the hips (not the stomach) and the shoulder belt crosses the chest and shoulder (not the neck). Poor belt positioning negates all other safety features.
  • Side-Impact Protection and Material Quality: Boosters with reinforced sides, thicker foam padding, and energy-absorbing materials provide better protection in side-impact collisions, which are increasingly common. Check for boosters with proven crash-test performance from organizations like the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS), and verify that the materials maintain their integrity over time and through regular use.
