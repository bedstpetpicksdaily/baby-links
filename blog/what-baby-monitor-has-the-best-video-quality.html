Frequently Asked Questions

What resolution do I need for a baby monitor to see clearly?

Look for monitors with at least 1080p (Full HD) resolution for clear, detailed video. 4K monitors offer sharper images, but 1080p is generally sufficient for most parents to see their baby's face and movements clearly from across the room.

Is night vision important in a baby monitor?

Yes, night vision is essential since you'll be monitoring your baby in dark rooms. Infrared night vision lets you see without a light disturbing your baby's sleep, and it should provide clear black-and-white footage from at least 10-15 feet away.

What's the difference between WiFi and non-WiFi baby monitors for video quality?

WiFi monitors let you view on your phone from anywhere but video quality depends on your internet connection and can lag. Non-WiFi monitors with closed systems often have more stable, consistent video quality without compression, though you're limited to viewing on a dedicated parent unit.

Do baby monitors with zoom and pan features affect video quality?

Zoom and pan features don't necessarily reduce quality, but digital zoom can make the image grainier at maximum magnification. Look for monitors with optical zoom rather than digital zoom, and ensure the base resolution is high enough (1080p minimum) so that zoomed-in video remains clear.
