The Full Explanation

Video quality in baby monitors is measured by resolution, frame rate, and night vision capability. The best monitors on the market today offer 1080p resolution (compared to older 720p models) and at least 30 frames per second for smooth, clear video. Night vision should reach at least 15-20 feet with good clarity, allowing you to see your baby's facial expressions and movements even in complete darkness.

Range refers to how far the monitor can transmit signal while maintaining video quality. Most closed-system monitors (those that don't connect to WiFi) offer 700-900 feet of range, which covers most single and multi-story homes, as well as backyards and garages. WiFi-enabled monitors can theoretically reach anywhere with internet access, but video quality may vary depending on connection strength.

What the Experts Say

Pediatricians and child safety experts emphasize that video quality matters most when you need to monitor your baby's breathing patterns, check on illness symptoms, or identify potential hazards in the crib.
