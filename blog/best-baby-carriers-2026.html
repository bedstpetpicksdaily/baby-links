Baby Carrier Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • Ergonomic Support: Choose a carrier with proper lumbar support and weight distribution to prevent back pain during extended use. Look for padded shoulder straps and waist belts that can accommodate different body types.
  • Versatility & Longevity: Consider carriers that grow with your baby from newborn to toddler, offering multiple carrying positions (front, hip, and back). This maximizes your investment and eliminates the need to purchase multiple carriers.
  • Breathability & Comfort: Look for carriers made with breathable, moisture-wicking fabrics that prevent overheating in warm weather. Ensure the carrier has an infant insert or is sized appropriately for your baby's size and age.

Frequently Asked Questions

What age can babies use carriers?

Most carriers are suitable for babies from newborn (with proper inserts) up to 2-3 years old, depending on the model and weight limits. Always check the manufacturer's guidelines, as some carriers require inserts for newborns under 12 pounds, while others are designed to accommodate them from birth.

Are baby carriers safe?

Yes, baby carriers are safe when used correctly and chosen from reputable brands that meet safety standards. Always ensure your baby's airway is clear, their chin is off their chest, and you follow the manufacturer's instructions for proper positioning and tightness.
