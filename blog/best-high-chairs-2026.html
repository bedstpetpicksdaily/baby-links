High Chair Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • Safety Features: Ensure the high chair has a secure 5-point harness system, a sturdy base that won't tip easily, and meets current safety standards. Look for models that have been tested and certified by safety organizations.
  • Ease of Cleaning: Choose a high chair with a removable, dishwasher-safe tray and seat cushion, as babies are messy eaters. Avoid models with crevices and hard-to-reach areas where food can accumulate and harbor bacteria.
  • Space and Portability: Consider your kitchen size and whether you need a compact, foldable model or if a full-size chair works for your space. Lightweight options are ideal if you plan to move the chair between rooms or travel with it.

High Chair FAQ

What age can a baby start using a high chair?

Most babies can begin using a high chair around 6 months of age when they start eating solid foods and can sit up with minimal support. However, always check the manufacturer's recommendations, as some chairs have specific weight and height requirements.

How long will my child use a high chair?

Most children use high chairs until around 3-4 years of age, though this varies depending on the child and the chair's weight capacity. Many convertible models transition to booster seats, extending their usability and providing better value over time.
