The Best Way to Feed a Newborn Baby

The best way to feed a newborn is through either breastfeeding or formula feeding, depending on what works best for your family's circumstances and the baby's health needs. Both methods can provide complete nutrition when done correctly, and the most important factor is that your baby is fed consistently and gains weight appropriately.

The Short Answer

Whether you choose breastfeeding or formula feeding, consistency and responsiveness to your baby's hunger cues are what matter most. Newborns typically need to eat every 2-3 hours, and you should look for signs like rooting, hand-to-mouth movements, and crying as hunger indicators. The key is finding a feeding method that is sustainable for your family while ensuring your baby receives adequate nutrition and grows at a healthy rate.

The Full Explanation

Breastfeeding is recommended by health organizations like the WHO and American Academy of Pediatrics as the ideal first choice when possible. Breast milk provides optimal nutrition with antibodies that protect against infections, and it changes composition as your baby grows. However, breastfeeding requires significant time commitment, can be challenging initially, and isn't possible for all mothers due to medical, personal, or circumstantial reasons.

Formula feeding is a complete and nutritious alternative that offers flexibility and allows other caregivers to participate in feeding. Modern infant formulas are carefully regulated and designed to closely mimic breast milk's nutritional profile. Formula feeding eliminates concerns about maternal diet, medications, or supply issues, making it the right choice for many families.

Combination feeding (breast milk and formula) is another viable option that allows mothers to maintain some breastfeeding benefits while introducing bottle feeding for convenience and flexibility. Many families find this approach helps with returning to work, managing supply issues, or simply sharing feeding responsibilities more equitably.

Feeding frequency and amounts vary by method. Breastfed newborns typically nurse 8-12 times per day, while formula-fed babies usually take 6-8 bottles daily. Signs of adequate feeding include consistent wet diapers, regular bowel movements, and steady weight gain after the initial newborn weight loss.

What the Experts Say

Pediatricians emphasize that fed is best—meaning the feeding method matters far less than ensuring your baby gets adequate nutrition and you're practicing safe feeding hygiene. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends exclusive breastfeeding for about the first 6 months if possible, but recognizes that formula feeding is a completely acceptable and safe alternative. Lactation consultants and pediatric nutritionists stress the importance of proper latch (for breastfeeding), correct bottle preparation and sterilization (for formula), and watching for your baby's individual hunger and fullness cues rather than following rigid schedules.
