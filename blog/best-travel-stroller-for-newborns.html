What to Look For

  • Full recline capability: Newborns need to lie completely flat for proper spinal support and safe sleeping during travel. Look for strollers that recline 180 degrees or close to it, allowing your baby to sleep naturally while you're on the go.
  • Lightweight yet sturdy construction: Travel strollers should weigh under 12 pounds for easy maneuvering through airports and train stations, but not at the expense of stability. Check that the frame feels solid and the wheels lock securely in place.
  • Compatibility with infant car seats: The ability to attach your infant car seat directly to the stroller base transforms it into a travel system, letting you move your sleeping newborn without disturbing them. This feature is essential for minimizing wake-ups during transit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use a travel stroller for a newborn?

Yes, but only if it reclines fully flat or nearly flat, since newborns need to lie completely horizontal for proper spine support. Standard travel strollers with limited recline aren't suitable for infants under 3-4 months old, so check the recline angle specifications before purchasing.

What's the difference between a travel stroller and a regular stroller?

Travel strollers are designed to be lightweight, compact, and fold small for easy airport gate-checking and carrying, while regular strollers prioritize comfort and features over portability. Travel strollers typically weigh 10-15 lbs compared to 25+ lbs for standard models, but may sacrifice storage space and sun coverage.

How much should I spend on a travel stroller for a newborn?

Quality newborn-friendly travel strollers range from $200-$600, with budget options around $150-$250 and premium brands like UPPAbaby and Cybex starting at $400+. The sweet spot for value is $250-$400, where you'll find strollers with full recline, good wheel suspension, and durability for multiple children.

Do I need a separate bassinet or car seat attachment for my travel stroller?

Most modern travel strollers have full flat recline seats suitable for newborns, so a separate bassinet isn't necessary. However, many parents appreciate strollers compatible with their existing infant car seats for convenience, allowing you to snap the seat directly onto the stroller frame without waking the baby.
