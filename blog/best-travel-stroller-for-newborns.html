Traveling with a newborn can feel overwhelming, but the right stroller makes all the difference. A travel stroller designed for newborns needs to balance portability with the safety and comfort features your littlest passenger requires. Finding one that checks all these boxes means you can explore the world without leaving your peace of mind at home.

What to Look For

  • Full recline capability: Newborns need to lie completely flat for proper spinal support and safe sleeping during travel. Look for strollers that recline 180 degrees or close to it, allowing your baby to sleep naturally while you're on the go.
  • Lightweight yet sturdy construction: Travel strollers should weigh under 12 pounds for easy maneuvering through airports and train stations, but not at the expense of stability. Check that the frame feels solid and the wheels lock securely in place.
  • Compatibility with infant car seats: The ability to attach your infant car seat directly to the stroller base transforms it into a travel system, letting you move your sleeping newborn without disturbing them. This feature is essential for minimizing wake-ups during transit.

What to Avoid

  • Partial reclines: Many travel strollers recline only partway to save weight. This doesn't work for newborns, who need fully flat sleeping positions for safety and spinal development. An inadequate recline can also make your newborn uncomfortable during extended travel time.
  • Strollers without canopy protection: Newborn skin is extremely delicate and sun-sensitive. Avoid compact travel strollers with minimal or no canopy coverage—your baby's fragile skin deserves robust UV protection during travel.
  • Incompatible car seat systems: Some lightweight strollers won't accept infant car seats at all, forcing you to carry your baby separately or use a travel system from two different brands. This defeats the purpose of simplified travel and increases the risk of dropping an exhausted newborn at baggage claim.
