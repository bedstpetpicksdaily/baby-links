What to Look For

  • Universal Compatibility: Not all car seats click into all strollers. Look for systems labeled "travel systems" that are specifically engineered to work together, or verify that your car seat's brand matches your stroller's frame (like Graco, Chicco, or Uppababy systems). This eliminates the guesswork and ensures a secure, one-click connection.
  • Lightweight and Compact Design: When you're traveling, every pound matters. Choose strollers that weigh under 20 pounds and fold into a compact footprint—ideally something that fits in a car trunk or airplane overhead bin. A bulky stroller defeats the purpose of having a portable travel system.
  • Smooth One-Handed Folding: With a newborn in one arm and a car seat in the other, you need a stroller that folds with minimal effort. Look for mechanisms that collapse with a single motion or lever, and ideally stay folded without requiring straps or multiple steps to secure.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What car seat brands are compatible with the most strollers?

    Graco and Chicco car seats have the widest compatibility across stroller brands, as many manufacturers design their strollers to work with these popular seats. UPPAbaby, Baby Jogger, and Bugaboo also offer excellent compatibility with multiple car seat brands through their universal adapters. Check your specific stroller model's compatibility list before purchasing, as even within brands there can be variations.

    Can you use any car seat with any stroller?

    No, not all car seats work with all strollers due to different attachment mechanisms and base designs. Most strollers are compatible with specific car seat brands or require special adapters, which are sometimes sold separately. Always verify compatibility before buying by checking the stroller manufacturer's documentation or contacting customer service.

    What is the best stroller for frequent travel with a newborn?

    Lightweight, compact strollers like the Baby Jogger City Tour 2, UPPAbaby Minu, or Maclaren Quest are ideal for travel since they fold small enough for airplane overhead bins and are easy to carry. Look for models that accept your car seat directly or have a lightweight car seat option to minimize what you're carrying through airports and hotels.

    Do I need a travel system or can I buy stroller and car seat separately?

    You can buy separately, which gives you more flexibility to choose the best individual products rather than settling for a bundled package that may be heavier or less ideal for your needs. However, travel systems guarantee compatibility and often cost less than buying items separately, plus they're convenient for parents who want everything to work together seamlessly from day one.
