Frequently Asked Questions

Are white noise machines safe for babies?

Yes, when used properly. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping sound machines at or below 50 decibels, positioned at least 7 feet from your baby's crib. Most quality machines designed for infants meet these safety standards.

At what age can I use a sound machine with my baby?

Sound machines are safe for newborns from birth onward. Many parents use them from the hospital stay forward, as they can help ease the transition from the womb's consistent background noise.

Should I leave the sound machine on all night?

Most pediatricians recommend using a timer to turn the machine off after baby falls asleep, typically 30-60 minutes. This prevents dependency and allows your baby to learn to self-soothe without constant noise.

What sounds are best for baby sleep?

White noise, brown noise, and gentle rain or ocean sounds are most effective. Avoid jarring nature sounds or music. Consistent, steady tones work best for promoting deep sleep in infants.
