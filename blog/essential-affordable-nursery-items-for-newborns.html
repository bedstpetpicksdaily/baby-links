What to Look For

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most essential items needed for a newborn nursery on a budget?

The absolute essentials are a safe sleeping space (crib or bassinet), a firm mattress with fitted sheets, blankets for warmth, diapers and wipes, and basic clothing like onesies and sleep sacks. You can skip expensive furniture like changing tables by using a dresser with a changing pad on top, and buy generic diapers and clothing from budget retailers to save significantly.

Do I really need a white noise machine, humidifier, and blackout curtains for a nursery?

White noise machines and blackout curtains are helpful but not essential—you can use a fan for noise and regular curtains or even blankets for darkness. A humidifier is only necessary if your baby has congestion or you live in a very dry climate, so evaluate your specific needs before purchasing.

What nursery items are worth spending more money on versus buying cheap?

Invest in a quality crib mattress and sheets since your baby spends 16+ hours sleeping there daily, and poor support can affect sleep and development. A safe crib frame is also worth the investment, but you can save on décor, storage bins, and non-essential items like nightlights or fancy bedding sets.

Can I skip buying new and use secondhand nursery items to save money?

Yes, you can buy secondhand cribs, dressers, and clothing safely, but always purchase a new mattress for hygiene and safety reasons. Check all secondhand items for recalls, missing parts, and safety certifications, especially for products like cribs and car seats that have strict safety standards.
