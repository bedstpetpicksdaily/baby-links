What Parents And Caregivers Should Know

  • Shorter names are winning: Expect names like Mia, Leo, Ella, and Noah to remain popular while shorter variations of longer classics gain traction. These names are easy to spell, pronounce, and personalize on products.
  • Nature-inspired names are growing: From Willow to River to Sage, botanical and natural element names appeal to parents seeking connection to the environment and a sense of calm, earthy aesthetics in nursery design.
  • Vintage and retro names are making a comeback: Classic names like Olive, Henry, and Eleanor reflect a nostalgic trend where parents value timeless appeal over trendy novelty.
  • Gender-neutral names continue to rise: Names like River, Morgan, and Casey are increasingly chosen across all genders, allowing for more flexible and inclusive product selections.
  • Personalized products are more popular than ever: With name trends shifting, parents are investing in custom items—from monogrammed blankets to engraved furniture—to make their nursery feel unique.
