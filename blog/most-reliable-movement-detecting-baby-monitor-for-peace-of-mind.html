Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between movement detecting and breathing monitors?

Movement detecting monitors use video or motion sensors to track your baby's general movements and activity, while breathing monitors use specialized pads or sensors under the mattress to detect your baby's chest movements and breathing patterns specifically. Breathing monitors are more sensitive to cessation of breathing, whereas movement monitors are better for general sleep tracking and seeing what your baby is doing. Most pediatricians recommend movement monitors for standard use, while breathing monitors are typically reserved for high-risk situations.

Do movement detecting baby monitors work through blankets and clothing?

Video-based movement monitors work through blankets and clothing since they rely on visual detection, but sensor-based motion monitors may have reduced accuracy if your baby is heavily bundled or covered. For the most reliable detection, it's best to avoid heavy blankets and opt for sleep sacks or appropriate room temperature instead. Check product reviews to see how parents report performance in real bundled-baby scenarios.

What's the best movement detecting baby monitor that doesn't require WiFi?

Closed-system monitors like the Nanit Pro Plus and Owlet Smart Sock 3 offer reliable movement detection with optional WiFi, giving you local monitoring without internet dependency for your core features. Non-WiFi monitors typically have stronger privacy protections and work during internet outages, though they limit you to monitoring only within your home's range. Research your specific model's offline capabilities, as some features like alerts may still require WiFi connectivity.

How accurate are baby monitors at detecting movement and potential issues?

Quality movement detecting monitors are generally 85-95% accurate at tracking normal movement and sleep positions, but they cannot diagnose medical conditions or replace a pediatrician's care. False alarms do occur, especially with certain sleeping positions, heavy blankets, or sensor malfunctions, so it's important to have realistic expectations. Always pair a monitor with safe sleep practices and consult your pediatrician if you have concerns about your baby's breathing or health.
