What to Look For

  • Easy-to-clean surfaces and removable trays: During the early days of solid food introduction, messes are inevitable. Prioritize high chairs and accessories with smooth, non-porous surfaces and trays that detach completely for thorough washing. Look for chairs with adjustable recline positions, footrests, and safety straps that keep your baby upright and engaged without slouching. Proper positioning helps with swallowing and reduces choking risks as they learn to eat.
  • Compatible feeding accessories: Choose high chairs that work with various bowl styles, spoon holders, and cup attachments. Since you'll be experimenting with different foods and feeding methods, having a system that accommodates multiple accessories without requiring additional purchases saves money and frustration.
