Create the Ideal Cool Sleep Environment

The foundation of summer baby sleep success starts with room temperature. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping your baby's room between 68-72°F for optimal sleep. Use air conditioning, fans, or open windows during cooler evening hours to maintain this temperature. Blackout curtains also help by blocking heat-generating sunlight during the day, naturally cooling the room by several degrees.

Clothing and bedding choices matter tremendously in summer. Dress your baby in lightweight, breathable cotton clothing—consider a simple onesie or sleep sack made from moisture-wicking fabric. Replace heavy blankets with a lightweight cotton sheet or breathable sleep sack appropriate for warmer temperatures. These adjustments prevent overheating while maintaining safety standards.

Maintain Hydration and Comfort During Sleep

Keeping your baby hydrated throughout the day supports better nighttime sleep. For exclusively breastfed babies, more frequent nursing may be necessary during hot weather. For bottle-fed infants, offer additional water between feedings (after six months). During night sleep, keep a bottle of water accessible for quick hydration if your baby wakes.

Consider using a cool compress or damp cloth on your forehead before the bedtime routine to signal body temperature regulation. Bath time with lukewarm (not cold) water 30-60 minutes before bed also helps babies cool down naturally and prepares them physically and psychologically for sleep.
