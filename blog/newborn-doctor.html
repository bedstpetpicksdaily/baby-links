What to Look For

Board Certification and Credentials

Ensure your newborn doctor is board-certified by the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) or American Board of Pediatrics (ABP). Look for additional specializations in neonatology if your baby has specific health considerations. Verify credentials through the ABMS website or your state's medical board.

Availability and Responsiveness

Check whether the practice offers same-day sick visits, after-hours phone support, and accessible communication channels like patient portals or text messaging. During your baby's first months, responsive healthcare access can be the difference between peace of mind and unnecessary emergency room visits.

Hospital Affiliation and Delivery Partnership

Confirm that your newborn doctor has privileges at the hospital where you're delivering and has established protocols for newborn screening, jaundice management, and postpartum follow-up. A seamless handoff from obstetric to pediatric care prevents gaps in your baby's early healthcare.

Philosophy Alignment and Communication Style

Schedule a prenatal consultation to assess whether the doctor's approach to feeding, sleep, vaccinations, and developmental milestones aligns with your family's values. You want a provider who listens, answers questions thoroughly, and supports your parenting decisions within evidence-based guidelines.
