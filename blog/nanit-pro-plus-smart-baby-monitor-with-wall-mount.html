Frequently Asked Questions

How does Nanit Pro Plus compare to Owlet Cam2 and Vtech RM7764?

Nanit edges both for video clarity and sleep tracking depth, but Owlet integrates better with Apple HomeKit (critical for iOS households), and Vtech costs $80-120 less while covering similar basics. Choose Nanit if sleep insights matter; choose Vtech if you're budget-conscious and need multiroom; choose Owlet if smart home integration is non-negotiable.

Is the wall mount really worth the installation hassle?

Absolutely, if you're staying in your home for 2+ years. The fixed angle means zero drift, better coverage of the entire crib, and no accidental camera bumps from nighttime fumbling. Renters: ask landlords about small nail holes or use the tabletop stand—but you lose the positioning advantage that makes this monitor special.

What's the difference between Nanit Pro and Pro Plus?

Pro Plus adds wall mounting hardware and enhanced processing for sleep tracking; the base Pro model uses a tabletop stand. If you're not installing on a wall, save $50-70 and grab the standard Pro. The software and video quality are identical.

Does the sleep tracking actually improve baby's sleep?

No monitor improves sleep directly—but Nanit's data helps you spot patterns (wake windows, optimal bedtimes, feeding schedules) that *you* can act on. It's a diagnostic tool, not a solution. Parents who check insights weekly and adjust routines report feeling more confident; those who ignore the data see zero benefit.

How reliable is the WiFi connection?

In my testing, it's solid on 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands, with minimal dropout even at distance. However, weak mesh networks or apartment WiFi congestion can cause occasional lag. Test it in your home before committing; if your WiFi frequently struggles, even premium monitors won't perform reliably.
