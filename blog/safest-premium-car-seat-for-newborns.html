    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the safest car seat for newborns?

    Rear-facing infant car seats and travel systems are considered safest for newborns because they support the head, neck, and spine during crashes. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) recommends keeping infants rear-facing until age 2 or until they reach the seat's height and weight limits. Look for seats with excellent crash test ratings from NHTSA and IIHS, as well as features like side-impact protection and energy-absorbing foam.

    How long can a newborn stay in a car seat?

    Most car seats are designed for continuous use of 1-2 hours at a time, though some pediatricians recommend limiting it to 30-45 minutes for newborns to prevent breathing issues and positional asphyxia. For longer trips, plan breaks every 2 hours to remove your baby from the seat. Always check your specific car seat's manual, as recommendations vary by manufacturer.

    Is a bucket seat or convertible car seat better for newborns?

    Bucket (infant) seats are more convenient for newborns since they're portable, click into stroller bases, and can be easily moved between vehicles—making them ideal for first-time parents. Convertible seats can be used from birth through toddlerhood in rear-facing mode, saving money long-term, but they're heavier and permanently installed. Many parents choose a bucket seat for the first year, then switch to a convertible seat for extended rear-facing use.

    What safety features should I look for in a newborn car seat?

    Priority features include a five-point harness system, side-impact protection, energy-absorbing foam, an easy-to-install base, and a recline indicator for proper angle. Check that the seat has passed rigorous crash testing and look for certifications like JPMA (Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association). Additionally, choose a seat with a secure installation method—either LATCH connectors or a properly anchored base—to ensure minimal movement in a crash.
