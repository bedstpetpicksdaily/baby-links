Frequently Asked Questions

Which Target baby wipes are being recalled?

Target recalled specific batches of their Up & Up brand baby wipes due to potential bacterial contamination. You can identify recalled products by checking the lot numbers on the package against Target's official recall list on their website or the FDA announcement, which includes specific date codes and UPC numbers.

Are all Target baby wipes unsafe right now?

No, only specific batches of Up & Up brand baby wipes are affected by the recall, not all Target baby wipes or all Up & Up products. Other baby wipe brands sold at Target and newer production batches of Up & Up remain safe to use.

What should I do if I bought recalled Target baby wipes?

Stop using the wipes immediately and check if your package matches the recalled lot numbers. You can return the product to Target for a full refund without a receipt, or contact Target customer service for a prepaid return shipping label if you prefer not to visit a store.

How do I know if my baby was affected by the recalled wipes?

If your baby used the recalled wipes, watch for signs of infection such as rash, redness, or unusual skin irritation that doesn't improve within a few days. Contact your pediatrician immediately if your child develops any concerning symptoms, as they can determine if medical treatment is needed.
