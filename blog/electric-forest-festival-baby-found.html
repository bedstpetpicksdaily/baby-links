Frequently Asked Questions

What is the age limit for babies at Electric Forest Festival?

Electric Forest allows babies of all ages to attend the festival with a parent or guardian. However, infants under 2 years old typically do not require a ticket, though you'll need to register them at will-call or during check-in. It's recommended to contact the festival directly to confirm current age policies and any registration requirements.

Is Electric Forest Festival safe for babies and young children?

While Electric Forest is family-friendly during daytime hours, the late-night electronic music sets and crowded areas make it challenging for infants and very young children. The festival does offer a designated family camping area and family-friendly programming during afternoon hours, making it more suitable for babies if you plan strategically.

What facilities does Electric Forest provide for babies?

Electric Forest has limited baby-specific facilities, so you'll need to bring essentials like diapers, wipes, formula, and bottles. The festival does have medical tents and first aid stations available throughout the grounds, and some camping areas have water access for cleaning supplies and bottle washing.

Do I need a ticket for my baby at Electric Forest?

Babies under 2 years old typically do not require a festival ticket, though policies vary by year. Toddlers aged 2 and up generally need their own ticket, so check the official Electric Forest website or contact their customer service for the current year's specific age and ticketing requirements.
