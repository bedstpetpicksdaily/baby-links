What Clothing Do I Need for a Newborn?

Newborns need basic essentials including bodysuits, sleepers, socks, mittens, hats, swaddles, and a light jacket or sweater for warmth. You'll want multiple sets since babies go through clothes quickly due to diaper blowouts, spit-ups, and frequent changes.

The Short Answer

Every newborn needs a foundation of simple, practical clothing that prioritizes comfort and easy diaper access. Focus on bodysuits and sleepers as your core items, supplemented with socks, mittens, and seasonal layers. Aim for 7-10 bodysuits, 5-7 sleepers, multiple pairs of socks, and a few lightweight cardigans or jackets. Avoid complicated clothing with excessive buttons or snaps—babies need quick, fuss-free dressing for frequent changes.

The Full Explanation

Essential Newborn Clothing Categories

  • Bodysuits and Onesies: These are the foundation of a newborn's wardrobe. Choose snap or button closures for easy diaper changes. Short-sleeved versions work year-round with layering, while long-sleeved options provide extra warmth. Most parents need 7-10 bodysuits in newborn and 0-3 month sizes.
  • Sleepers and Sleepwear: One-piece sleepers with snap closures from neck to toe make diaper changes and dressing easier than separate pants and shirts. Aim for 5-7 sleepers in various weights depending on your climate. Look for options with mittens attached to prevent babies from scratching their faces.
  • Socks and Booties: Newborns lose heat rapidly through their extremities. Stock 8-10 pairs of soft socks and several pairs of booties. Many parents prefer booties that stay on better than traditional socks.
  • Mittens: Newborn mittens prevent babies from scratching their delicate facial skin. Look for mittens with gentle closures that won't restrict circulation. 3-4 pairs should be sufficient, and many sleepers come with built-in mittens.
  • Hats and Head Coverings: Babies regulate temperature through their heads, making hats essential immediately after birth. Keep 2-3 lightweight hats in the hospital bag and at home for those first weeks.
  • Swaddles and Wraps: Muslin swaddles and lightweight wraps serve multiple purposes—swaddling for comfort, covering for nursing, and sun protection. 3-4 swaddles in various sizes are useful additions.
  • Layers and Jackets: Lightweight cardigans, jackets, or sweaters allow temperature adjustment without overheating. Choose 2-3 lightweight options that work with your climate and season.
  • Seasonal Considerations: In winter, invest in thicker sleepers and warm jackets. Summer babies need breathable fabrics and light layers. Avoid heavy coats for car seats—use lightweight blankets instead for safety.

Sizing Guide

Most newborns wear newborn (NB) size for the first 2-4 weeks, then transition to 0-3 months. However, some babies skip newborn sizes entirely. Rather than buying exclusively in one size, split your purchases between newborn and 0-3 month sizes. Buy one or two items of each size first to see what fits your baby before investing heavily.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many onesies and bodysuits does a newborn need?

You'll need 8-10 onesies in newborn and 0-3 month sizes, since newborns go through multiple outfit changes daily from spit-up and diaper leaks. Buy a mix of short and long-sleeve options depending on your climate, and consider having extras on hand for the first few weeks when laundry piles up quickly.

What size clothes should I buy for a newborn?

Start with newborn size (0-3 months) for your hospital bag and first weeks home, but buy most of your initial wardrobe in 0-3 month and 3-6 month sizes since newborns grow rapidly. Many babies skip newborn size entirely or only wear it for 1-2 weeks, so avoid buying too much in that size unless your family tends toward larger babies.

Does a newborn need mittens, hats, and socks?

Yes—mittens prevent newborns from scratching their faces, hats help regulate body temperature (babies lose heat through their heads), and socks keep tiny feet warm since they can't regulate temperature well. Pack 3-4 of each in your hospital bag, plus several pairs of socks for home since babies kick them off constantly.

What type of newborn pajamas are best?

Look for snap or zip-up footie pajamas rather than button-up styles, as they're faster for nighttime diaper changes and dressing. Avoid pajamas with drawstrings for safety, and choose breathable cotton material to reduce overheating and SIDS risk—aim for 4-6 pairs in your newborn and 0-3 month sizes.
