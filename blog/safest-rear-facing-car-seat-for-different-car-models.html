What to Look For

  • Vehicle-Specific Compatibility: Check the car seat's manual for your exact vehicle make, model, and year. Some seats fit better in sedans with shallow back seats, while others are designed for SUVs or vehicles with deeper seating. Measure your car's back seat depth and compare it to the car seat's installation footprint to ensure proper recline angles (typically 30-45 degrees for rear-facing seats).
  • Multiple Installation Options: Look for seats offering both LATCH and seat belt installation, as not all vehicles have equally accessible LATCH anchors. Some cars place LATCH connectors too close together or too far apart, making certain car seats difficult or impossible to install securely. A seat with flexible installation methods gives you backup options if one method doesn't work well in your vehicle.
  • Extended Rear-Facing Weight Limits: Choose a seat that accommodates children up to at least 40-50 pounds while rear-facing. Vehicles with more spacious back seats can accommodate higher-weight-limit seats, allowing children to rear-face longer (the safest position for developing spines and necks).
