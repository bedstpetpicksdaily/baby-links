Choosing the right travel stroller for newborns and toddlers is one of the most important decisions you'll make as a parent heading out into the world. A stroller that works well for travel needs to be lightweight enough to carry through airports and fold into tight car trunks, yet still provide the safety and comfort your little one needs during those crucial early years. The good news is that the right travel stroller doesn't mean compromising on quality or functionality—it just means being strategic about which features matter most.

What to Look For

  • True Lightweight Design (Under 15 lbs): When you're traveling with a newborn or toddler, you're already managing car seats, diaper bags, and luggage. A stroller heavier than 15 pounds becomes a burden rather than a helper. Look for aluminum frames and minimal fabric that still maintain structural integrity. Lighter strollers fold faster, fit in overhead compartments on some airlines, and won't exhaust you when carrying them up stairs or through crowded terminals.
  • One-Hand Fold and Compact Dimensions: Travel strollers need to fold quickly and store easily in car trunks, hotel closets, and airplane overhead bins. A one-hand fold mechanism is essential when you're juggling a baby, diaper bag, and luggage. Measure the folded dimensions against your car's trunk space and your typical airline carry-on requirements before purchasing.
  • Recline Position for Newborns: Since you may use this stroller from birth through toddlerhood, ensure it has a full or near-full recline position safe for newborns. Many lightweight travel strollers sacrifice recline, which is problematic if you're planning to use it from day one. The recline allows newborns to lie flat for proper spine support and makes napping easier during travel.
