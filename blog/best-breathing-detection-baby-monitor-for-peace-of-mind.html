Frequently Asked Questions

Do breathing detection baby monitors actually work?

Breathing detection monitors use under-mattress sensors or chest clips to track your baby's breathing patterns and can alert you if breathing stops or drops below a certain threshold. While they provide additional monitoring beyond standard video monitors, they're not medical-grade devices and shouldn't replace medical advice—some pediatricians note that SIDS risk is already significantly reduced through safe sleep practices like back sleeping and firm surfaces.

What's the difference between breathing detection and movement detection monitors?

Breathing detection monitors use sensitive sensors to measure actual chest movements and respiratory rate, while movement detection monitors only track general motion in the crib. Breathing detection is more specific to respiratory activity, making it theoretically more precise for detecting breathing abnormalities, though both types have their limitations in real-world use.

Are breathing monitors safe to use with babies?

Under-mattress breathing sensors are considered safe when used correctly on a firm, flat sleep surface, though some pediatricians advise against adding extra items to the crib. Chest clip monitors should only be used on older babies (typically 6+ months) and come with strangulation risk warnings, so always follow manufacturer guidelines based on your baby's age and weight.

How much do breathing detection baby monitors cost?

Breathing detection monitors typically range from $100-$400 depending on the technology and features—under-mattress sensor systems are generally in the $150-$300 range, while clip-on monitors are usually $100-$200. Premium models with video, temperature sensing, and smartphone connectivity will cost more than basic breathing-only monitors.
