Frequently Asked Questions

How does the AI breathing detection actually work?

The monitor uses computer vision to analyze subtle chest movements through the camera feed. It doesn't require any contact sensors or wearables on the baby. During testing, accuracy was strong under consistent lighting, though shadows and thick blankets occasionally caused false positives. It's more reliable than nothing, but not medical-grade equipment.

Is it worth upgrading from a basic $100 monitor?

Only if you're genuinely anxious about breathing patterns or want detailed sleep analytics. The AI features are convenient, but a basic monitor with night vision and two-way audio solves 90% of most parents' needs. The premium here buys peace of mind, not necessity.

Does it work with older WiFi routers or weak connections?

It needs steady 2.4GHz WiFi—not always the case in older homes with spotty connections. If your router is more than five years old or positioned far from the nursery, you might experience lag or disconnection. Test your setup before committing to this price tier.

How long do the batteries last if WiFi drops?

This is a plug-in monitor, not battery-powered. If you lose power, you lose monitoring. That's a significant limitation compared to some competitors that offer battery backup for temporary outages. Keep it on a UPS if backup power matters to you.
