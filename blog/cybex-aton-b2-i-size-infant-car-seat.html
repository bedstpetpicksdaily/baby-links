Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Aton B2 I-Size work with other strollers besides Cybex?

Yes, it's compatible with most major stroller brands via adapter clicks. However, it works seamlessly with Cybex stroller systems if you're already in that ecosystem. Check your specific stroller model's compatibility list before assuming—I tested it with a non-Cybex stroller and needed to verify the adapter first.

How long can my baby use this seat?

The Aton B2 I-Size accommodates infants from birth up to approximately 24 months (or 13 kg/28.6 lbs), depending on height and weight. Once your child outgrows it, you'll transition to a convertible or forward-facing seat. It's designed as an infant-only solution, not a multi-stage seat.

Is the rotation feature worth the extra cost compared to a fixed base?

Honestly? If you're doing solo pickup and drop-off routines frequently, yes. The rotation eliminates the awkward twist-and-reach that can strain your back and makes buckling smoother. If you always have a partner helping or your car's backseat layout is spacious, a fixed base saves money without sacrificing safety. It's a convenience premium, not a safety upgrade.

Does this seat recline, and can I adjust the angle?

The Aton B2 I-Size has a fixed recline angle of approximately 157 degrees, which is appropriate for newborn support. You cannot manually adjust the recline like some other models. The angle is locked to ensure proper positioning—no fiddling required, but also no customization.

What's the real-world difference between this and budget rotating seats?

Build quality and smoothness. Cheaper rotating seats often have tight, sticky rotation mechanisms that require effort. The Aton B2 I-Size rotates with one fluid motion—no grinding, no resistance. The ISOFIX connection also feels more secure, with audible clicks confirming proper installation. You're paying for engineering and durability, not just the rotation feature.
