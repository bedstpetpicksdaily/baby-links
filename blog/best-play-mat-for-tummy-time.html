Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a good tummy time play mat?

A good tummy time mat should have cushioning to protect your baby's head and joints, a non-slip bottom to prevent sliding on hard floors, and be easy to clean. Look for mats with engaging visuals, textures, or detachable toys that encourage your baby to lift their head and strengthen neck muscles.

How thick should a tummy time mat be?

A tummy time mat should be at least 0.5 inches thick to provide adequate cushioning for your baby's comfort and development. Mats between 0.75 to 1 inch are ideal as they offer good support without being too soft, which can make it harder for babies to build strength.

Are foam play mats safe for newborns?

Yes, foam play mats are safe for newborns when they meet safety standards like CPSIA certification and are made from non-toxic materials. Always supervise tummy time and choose mats labeled as free from harmful chemicals like phthalates and BPA.

What's the difference between a play mat and a tummy time mat?

Tummy time mats are specifically designed with extra cushioning and are typically smaller to focus on prone positioning for newborns and young infants. Play mats are larger, more versatile, and often used for older babies to crawl and explore, with less emphasis on neck-strengthening support.
