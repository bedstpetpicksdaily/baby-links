Traditional Sound Machines: Pros and Limitations

Sound machines have been around for years and offer variety—think thunderstorms, ocean waves, fan noise, and lullabies. This versatility appeals to many parents who want options, and some babies do respond well to these ambient sounds. However, traditional sound machines often require you to cycle through multiple sounds to find what works for your baby. They also tend to be bulkier and may include unnecessary features that drive up the cost. Additionally, the generic white noise produced by standard machines lacks the specific frequency optimization that makes shushing so effective for newborn sleep.
