    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use a convertible car seat for a newborn from birth?

    Yes, most convertible car seats are approved for use from birth (starting at 4-5 lbs), but you must use them in rear-facing mode for newborns and infants. Always check the manufacturer's specifications, as some convertible seats have minimum weight requirements or require an infant insert for proper newborn positioning.

    How long does a convertible car seat last?

    A convertible car seat typically lasts from birth through age 8-10 (up to 65-80 lbs depending on the model) because it converts from rear-facing to forward-facing and eventually to a booster seat position. This means one seat can replace 2-3 separate car seats as your child grows, making it a cost-effective long-term investment.

    What's the difference between a convertible car seat and an infant car seat?

    Infant car seats are designed only for newborns and babies up to 30-35 lbs and are typically used with a stroller frame, while convertible seats stay in your vehicle and grow with your child up to 10+ years old. Convertible seats are more budget-friendly overall, but infant seats offer more portability and can be easier for frequent travel between vehicles.

    Do convertible car seats recline enough for newborns?

    Most convertible car seats have multiple recline positions to meet the proper angle requirement for newborns (typically 30-45 degrees when rear-facing), which supports their head and neck development. Many also include an infant insert or head support pillow to ensure a snug fit and proper positioning for smaller newborns.
