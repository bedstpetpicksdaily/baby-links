Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between infrared and thermal night vision on baby monitors?

Infrared night vision uses LED lights to illuminate the room and capture clear black-and-white images, while thermal vision detects heat signatures and works through walls and objects. Infrared is better for seeing your baby's facial expressions and movements, whereas thermal is useful for detecting if your baby is too hot or cold, but shows less detail.

How far can a baby monitor's night vision see?

Most quality baby monitors with night vision can see 8-15 feet clearly in complete darkness, though some premium models reach up to 20+ feet. The actual range depends on the monitor's IR LED power and camera lens quality, so check the manufacturer's specifications for your room size.

Does night vision on baby monitors hurt babies' eyes?

No, infrared night vision is completely safe for babies because infrared light is invisible to the human eye and produces no heat or radiation. The IR LEDs on monitors emit very low levels of light specifically designed to be gentle and non-disruptive to sleep.

Can I use a baby monitor with night vision during the day?

Yes, baby monitors automatically switch between day and night modes using a light sensor, so the night vision only activates when the room is dark. During the day, it operates like a regular camera with standard color video, so you don't need to manually switch anything.
