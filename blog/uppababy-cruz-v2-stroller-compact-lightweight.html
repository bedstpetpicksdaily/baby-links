Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Cruz V2 compare to the Cybex Eos or Nuna Mixx Next?

The Cruz V2 is lighter and folds more compactly than the Eos, making it superior for travel and car storage. Versus the Mixx Next, you're trading some recline functionality for a significantly simpler fold mechanism—the Mixx Next is more feature-dense but heavier. If portability is your primary concern, the Cruz V2 wins. If you need maximum recline for younger infants, the Mixx Next justifies its weight.

Can you use this with a newborn, or do I need an infant seat?

Technically yes with the seat alone due to semi-recline, but practically speaking, a newborn needs the UPPAbaby Mesa or compatible infant car seat for true flat positioning. The Cruz V2 shines as a second stroller once your baby is 6+ months. Buying this as your first stroller requires accepting that newborn naps happen elsewhere until your child is ready for the seat recline.

For parents doing frequent car transfers or overhead storage, this weight difference is the entire value proposition—don't underestimate it.
