Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best wireless baby monitor with no WiFi required?

Closed digital monitors like the Infant Optics DXR-8 and Motorola Halo+ offer secure, private connections without internet dependency, making them ideal if you prioritize privacy and don't want to worry about WiFi outages. These models use FHSS technology to create a private connection between the parent unit and camera, so hackers cannot access the feed. However, they only work within a limited range (typically 700-900 feet), so they're best for in-home monitoring.

Can you use a baby monitor if your WiFi goes down?

WiFi-enabled monitors require internet to view remotely on your phone, but most have a local connection option that works when both the camera and parent unit are on the same home network without internet. If you want monitoring during WiFi outages, choose a closed digital monitor with a dedicated parent unit, as these don't rely on WiFi at all. For WiFi monitors, check if the brand offers a secondary app or local viewing feature as a backup.

What is the difference between WiFi and non-WiFi baby monitors?

WiFi monitors connect to your home internet and let you view your baby on your smartphone from anywhere (even away from home), while non-WiFi monitors use a closed digital signal between a dedicated parent unit and camera with limited range (usually one house). WiFi monitors offer more flexibility and remote access but carry minor privacy risks if hacked, whereas non-WiFi monitors are more secure and private but only work within range. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize remote access or maximum security and simplicity.

How far can a wireless baby monitor work?

WiFi-enabled monitors work anywhere you have internet access (home, work, on vacation), while closed digital monitors typically work 700-900 feet in open space but may be reduced to 100-300 feet through walls and interference. The actual range depends on the brand and obstacles in your home, so it's important to test it in your specific layout. If you have a large home or multiple floors, check the manufacturer's range specifications and consider a WiFi model for reliable long-distance monitoring.
