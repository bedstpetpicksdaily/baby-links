Why Air Conditioning Alone Isn't Enough for Baby Sleep

Air conditioning cools the room, but it doesn't create a consistent sleep environment. Babies are sensitive to sudden temperature drops, which can actually jolt them awake. Additionally, AC units produce their own noises—compressors humming, air vents rushing—that create unpredictable auditory stimulation. These inconsistent sounds disrupt REM sleep cycles rather than supporting them.

More importantly, AC doesn't address the psychological comfort your baby needs. Newborns and infants thrive in predictable, controlled environments. A room that's simply cool isn't the same as a room with white noise, which provides consistent auditory masking and triggers the calming reflex that helps babies drift off naturally.

Is white noise safe for babies?

Yes, when used properly. White noise machines designed for babies, like Baby Shusher, use safe sound frequencies and adjustable volume levels. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping volumes under 50 decibels for infant safety.

Can I use white noise instead of air conditioning in summer?

White noise addresses sleep quality, but air conditioning controls temperature. In hot weather, use both together—AC to maintain a cool room (68-72°F) and white noise to optimize sleep cycles and mask disruptive sounds.

How quickly do babies respond to white noise machines?
