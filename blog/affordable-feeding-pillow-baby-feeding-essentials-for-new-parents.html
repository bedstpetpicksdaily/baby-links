    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is a feeding pillow and do I really need one?

    A feeding pillow is a specially designed cushion that supports your baby during bottle or breastfeeding, reducing strain on your arms, neck, and back. While not mandatory, most parents find them essential for comfort during frequent feedings, especially in the first months when you're nursing 8-12 times daily.

    What's the difference between a nursing pillow and a regular pillow?

    Nursing pillows are specifically contoured to wrap around your torso and elevate your baby to the correct feeding height, while regular pillows don't provide proper support and can create unsafe positioning. Feeding pillows are also firmer to maintain shape and often have safety features like non-slip bottoms.

    Can I use a feeding pillow for both breastfeeding and bottle feeding?

    Yes, most quality feeding pillows work for both breastfeeding and bottle feeding, making them versatile investments. They also double as support pillows for tummy time and learning to sit, extending their usefulness beyond the newborn stage.

    How much should I spend on a feeding pillow?

    Affordable feeding pillows range from $25-$60 and offer the same core benefits as premium options—proper height, support, and comfort during feedings. Focus on features like removable, washable covers and firm support rather than brand name, as mid-range options typically offer the best value for new parents.
