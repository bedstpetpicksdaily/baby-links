    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the best range for a baby monitor?

    Most parents need a range of at least 700-900 feet in open space, though this decreases significantly through walls and obstacles. For larger homes, multi-story houses, or yard monitoring, look for monitors advertising 1000+ feet of range or those using WiFi for unlimited range. WiFi monitors offer the best long-range capability since they work anywhere you have internet access.

    Is WiFi or closed digital baby monitor better for long range?

    WiFi monitors offer superior long-range capability and let you check on your baby from anywhere, even away from home, but require internet and have potential privacy concerns. Closed digital monitors are more secure and don't need WiFi, but their range is typically limited to 700-1000 feet and only work within your home's range. For true long-range peace of mind, WiFi monitors are the better choice.

    Do long range baby monitors need WiFi?

    No, closed digital (FHSS) monitors work without WiFi and offer decent range up to 900 feet, but WiFi monitors are required if you want to access the feed outside your home or from different buildings. If you primarily need range within your home, a closed digital monitor works fine, but WiFi gives you flexibility and true peace of mind when you're away.

    What features matter most in a long range baby monitor?

    Beyond range, prioritize two-way talk-back, night vision, temperature monitoring, and pan/tilt capabilities so you can see your entire nursery. Battery life on the parent unit is also crucial for long-range monitoring, as you'll be carrying it further distances. Look for monitors with low-light indicators and reliable connection stability, especially if you're monitoring across multiple floors or outdoor distances.
