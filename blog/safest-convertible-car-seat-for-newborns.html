What to Look For

  • Newborn-Specific Support: Look for convertible seats with excellent head and neck support for tiny babies, including adjustable headrests and deep side-impact protection. Many convertibles include removable newborn inserts or adjustable harness systems that accommodate smaller frames from day one without requiring additional purchases.
  • Easy Installation and Proper Fit: Choose a seat with both LATCH connectors and a strong, straightforward seat belt installation for your specific vehicle. Test the installation in your car before purchasing—the seat should move less than an inch side-to-side when properly installed, as incorrect installation is a leading cause of car seat failure.
  • High Safety Ratings and Crash Test Performance: Prioritize seats that exceed federal safety standards with excellent ratings from the NHTSA and independent crash tests. Look for certifications indicating superior side-impact protection and forward-facing stability, since you'll be using this seat for many years of driving.
