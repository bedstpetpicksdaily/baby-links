Is a 4.7 out of 5 stars rating trustworthy for products?

A 4.7-star rating is generally very trustworthy, especially if it's based on hundreds or thousands of reviews. This rating suggests the product works well for most users while acknowledging minor issues some customers experienced, which is more realistic than a perfect 5-star rating.

What should I look for in the reviews behind a 4.7 star rating?

Check the 1-star and 2-star reviews to understand common complaints—these reveal real issues like durability, sizing, or quality problems. Also verify the review count is substantial (100+ reviews) and look for verified purchase badges to ensure ratings come from actual buyers.

Why are products with 4.7 stars better than 5-star products?

A 4.7-star rating is often more reliable than 5 stars because it indicates authentic customer feedback including legitimate concerns. Products with perfect 5-star ratings may have fewer reviews, fake reviews, or be too new to show real-world issues that emerge over time.

Can I return a product if it's rated 4.7 stars but I'm unhappy with it?
