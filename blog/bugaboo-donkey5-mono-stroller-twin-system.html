Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use the Donkey5 Mono with just one seat, or do you have to buy the twin configuration?

The Donkey5 Mono comes with a single seat as standard. You purchase the additional seat separately if and when you need it—usually $300-400. This staged approach actually makes financial sense; you're not paying for capacity you won't use immediately. Many parents wait until their second child is 6-12 months old before adding the second seat.

How does it compare to the Uppababy Vista for families with twins or growing families?

The Vista is fully twin-configured from day one and typically cheaper upfront. The Donkey5 Mono strategy is better if you want to start minimal and expand later—you save money initially and have a genuinely smaller footprint as a single-seat stroller. The Vista wins if you need immediate twin capacity. Both are durable; it's about whether you want modularity or ready-made twin functionality.

Is the Bugaboo Donkey5 Mono good for newborns, or should we wait until the baby is older?

Newborns are perfectly fine in the flat recline position—that's actually one of its strengths. Pair it with a compatible bassinet or carrycot if you prefer, but the seat itself handles newborns safely. You could theoretically use this from birth as your only stroller, though many parents transition to the flat carrycot position in early months.
