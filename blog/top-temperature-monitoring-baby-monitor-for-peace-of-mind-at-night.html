    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the normal temperature range for a baby during sleep?

    A baby's normal body temperature is typically between 97°F and 100.4°F (36.1°C to 38°C) when measured rectally. During sleep, a baby's temperature may drop slightly, so a reading of 96-98°F is often normal depending on the room temperature and bedding used. However, any temperature above 100.4°F warrants contacting your pediatrician.

    How accurate are temperature monitoring baby monitors?

    Most quality temperature monitoring baby monitors are accurate within ±0.5°F to ±1°F, with contact sensors being more accurate than non-contact infrared options. Look for monitors that have been clinically tested and FDA-cleared for accuracy, as budget models may have larger variances. Reading the specifications and user reviews can help you identify which models deliver reliable readings.

    Can a baby monitor detect SIDS or sudden temperature changes?

    Temperature monitoring baby monitors can alert you to room temperature changes or body temperature fluctuations, but they cannot diagnose or prevent SIDS. These monitors are best used as an additional safety tool alongside safe sleep practices like firm mattresses, back sleeping, and keeping soft objects away from the crib. Always follow AAP guidelines and consult your pediatrician about SIDS prevention.

    Do I need WiFi for a temperature monitoring baby monitor?

    Most modern temperature monitoring baby monitors offer WiFi connectivity for remote viewing on your smartphone, though some models also work with a closed-system parent unit that doesn't require WiFi. If you want to check temperature alerts while away from home, WiFi-enabled monitors are more convenient, but closed-system monitors offer better privacy and don't depend on internet connectivity. Consider your needs—local monitoring only versus remote access—when choosing.
