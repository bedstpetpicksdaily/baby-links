What to Look For

  • LATCH System and Five-Point Harness: Ensure the car seat has both a reliable Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children (LATCH) connection and a five-point harness system that distributes crash forces evenly across your baby's shoulders, hips, and between the legs. For newborns specifically, a five-point harness is non-negotiable as it keeps tiny bodies secure without restricting their delicate development.
  • Newborn-Specific Support and Padding: Look for car seats with removable newborn inserts and properly contoured support that cradle your infant's head and neck. Lightweight seats shouldn't skimp on cushioning—the padding should be plush enough to support their underdeveloped spine without being so thick that it prevents a snug harness fit.
  • Weight and Portability Without Safety Sacrifices: Choose a car seat weighing between 7-10 pounds that still maintains superior crash test ratings and stability. Check that it has a comfortable handle for carrying, a wide base for stability when placed on surfaces, and works with multiple stroller frames if you plan to use a travel system.
