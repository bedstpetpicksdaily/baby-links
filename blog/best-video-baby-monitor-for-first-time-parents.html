Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a video baby monitor or is audio enough?

A video monitor gives you peace of mind by letting you see your baby's position and check for issues like rolled blankets without entering the room, which reduces sleep disruptions. Audio-only monitors are cheaper and work fine if you're in a small space, but video is worth the investment for safety and avoiding unnecessary room checks.

What's the difference between WiFi and closed-network baby monitors?

WiFi monitors connect to your internet and let you view your baby from anywhere using your phone, while closed-network monitors only work within range of the base station (typically 700-900 feet). WiFi monitors are more convenient for checking in from work or another room, but closed-network monitors are more secure and don't depend on internet reliability.

How much should I spend on a baby monitor as a first-time parent?

You can find solid monitors between $100-200 that include video, night vision, and temperature monitoring—this range covers most first-time parents' needs without unnecessary features. Budget options under $100 exist but often have lag or connectivity issues, while premium monitors over $300 add features like two-way talk and movement detection that aren't essential.

What features actually matter in a baby monitor for newborns?

Prioritize clear night vision, reliable connection, room temperature monitoring, and a good viewing angle so you can see your entire crib. Two-way audio is helpful for soothing without entering the room, and a zoom function lets you check on your baby without getting up, but movement detection and sleep tracking are less critical for newborns.
