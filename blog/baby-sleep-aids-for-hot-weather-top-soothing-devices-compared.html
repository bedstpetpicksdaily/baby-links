Why Hot Weather Disrupts Baby Sleep

Heat affects babies differently than adults. Infants cannot regulate their body temperature as effectively, making them more susceptible to overheating and sleep disruption. When babies become too warm, they wake frequently, cry more often, and struggle to enter deep sleep cycles. Additionally, the discomfort from heat-related restlessness can create negative sleep associations, making bedtime increasingly difficult for parents.

Creating a cool, soothing sleep environment requires more than just air conditioning. White noise devices help mask external sounds that become more prominent in summer—like neighborhood activity, open windows, and air conditioning units. These masking sounds create a consistent audio environment that helps babies sleep through ambient noise and minor disturbances.
