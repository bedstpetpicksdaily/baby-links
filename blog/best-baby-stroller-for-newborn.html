Choosing your first stroller is one of the most important baby gear decisions you'll make, especially when you're bringing a newborn home from the hospital. Your newborn needs a stroller that provides proper support, safety, and flexibility for those delicate early months. The right choice can make daily outings manageable and give you confidence in your new parenting journey.

What to Look For

  • Full Recline and Flat Sleep Position: Newborns cannot sit upright and need to lie completely flat for proper spinal support and safe sleeping. Look for strollers that recline all the way to a bassinet-like position, not just a partial recline. This is essential for naps during outings and ensures your newborn isn't left in a compromising position.
  • Reversible or Parent-Facing Seat: A newborn benefits from being close to you and seeing your face, which aids bonding and lets you monitor their breathing and comfort easily. Choose a stroller where the seat can face toward you or be fully reversible, so you're not relying solely on a mirror to keep tabs on your little one.
  • Sturdy Frame and Smooth Suspension: Newborns are sensitive to jolts and bumps, so you need a stroller with excellent suspension and a robust frame that handles various terrain smoothly. A lightweight stroller might seem convenient, but prioritize stability and shock absorption over portability during the newborn phase.
