Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • Safety Ratings: Verify the car seat meets NHTSA standards and check independent crash test ratings from organizations like Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS).
  • Installation Method: Choose between LATCH, seat belt installation, or both. Consider your vehicle's compatibility and which method feels most intuitive for your lifestyle.
  • Longevity & Growth: Convertible and all-in-one seats offer better value by transitioning from rear-facing to forward-facing to booster modes, accommodating your child from birth through elementary school.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do car seats last?

Most car seats have a lifespan of 6-10 years from the manufacturing date due to material degradation and evolving safety standards. All-in-one and convertible seats provide better value since they're used throughout multiple stages of childhood within this timeframe.

Can I use a used car seat?

It's generally not recommended to use a used car seat unless you know its complete history, as previous crashes may have compromised structural integrity. Always purchase new from authorized retailers to ensure you have a full warranty and safety documentation.
