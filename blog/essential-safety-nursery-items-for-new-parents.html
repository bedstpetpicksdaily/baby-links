Creating a safe nursery is one of the most important investments you'll make as a new parent. The nursery is where your baby spends crucial sleeping hours, and having the right safety items in place gives you peace of mind while protecting your little one from potential hazards. This guide will help you navigate the essential safety products that every nursery needs.

What to Look For

  • Firm, Flat Sleep Surfaces: Look for cribs, bassinets, or play yards that meet current CPSC standards and feature a firm, flat mattress with fitted sheets. Avoid anything soft like pillows, bumpers, or blankets that increase suffocation risks.
  • Reliable Monitoring Technology: Choose baby monitors with secure connections, clear video/audio quality, and consistent range throughout your home. Look for monitors with room temperature alerts and two-way communication so you can respond quickly if needed.
  • Proper Furniture Anchoring and Spacing: Select nursery furniture that can be securely anchored to walls to prevent tipping, and ensure crib slats are no more than 2 3/8 inches apart to prevent head entrapment.

What to Avoid

  • Soft Bedding and Pillows: Skip crib bumpers, pillow bumpers, comforters, pillows, and stuffed animals in the sleep space. While they may look cozy, they significantly increase suffocation and SIDS risks. Stick with fitted sheets on a firm mattress—that's all your baby needs.
  • Unstable or Non-Anchored Furniture: Avoid dressers, shelving units, or changing tables that aren't secured to the wall. Babies become curious climbers, and unsecured furniture can tip over, causing serious injuries. This is a non-negotiable safety measure.
  • Budget Monitor Brands Without Security Features: Don't choose baby monitors based solely on price if they lack secure connections or have poor reviews about connectivity issues. A monitor that frequently loses signal defeats its purpose and creates anxiety rather than peace of mind.
