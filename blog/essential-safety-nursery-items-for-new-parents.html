What to Look For

  • Firm, Flat Sleep Surfaces: Look for cribs, bassinets, or play yards that meet current CPSC standards and feature a firm, flat mattress with fitted sheets. Avoid anything soft like pillows, bumpers, or blankets that increase suffocation risks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What safety items do I need for a newborn nursery?

Essential safety items include a firm crib mattress, fitted sheets, a baby monitor with video capability, blackout curtains, a white noise machine, and outlet covers. You'll also want a humidifier, a room thermometer to maintain 68-72°F, and a nightlight for safe nighttime navigation. Avoid pillows, bumpers, blankets, and stuffed animals in the crib as these increase SIDS risk.

Do I need a video baby monitor or is audio enough?

Video monitors are highly recommended as they allow you to check on your baby without opening the door, reducing sleep disruptions and letting you monitor breathing patterns and sleep position. Audio-only monitors are more affordable but limit your ability to visually assess your baby's condition, making video monitors worth the investment for peace of mind.

What type of crib mattress is safest for newborns?

A firm, flat crib mattress that meets CPSC standards is safest—avoid soft or memory foam mattresses as they increase suffocation risk. The mattress should fit snugly in the crib with no gaps, and you should use only fitted sheets designed specifically for your crib size to prevent entanglement hazards.

How can I prevent falls and injuries in a nursery?

Secure furniture like dressers and bookshelves to the wall with anti-tip brackets, use safety gates if needed, and keep the floor clear of cords and small objects. Install outlet covers or tamper-resistant outlets, keep the room clutter-free, and ensure good lighting to safely navigate during nighttime care.
