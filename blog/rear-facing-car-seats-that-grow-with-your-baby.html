Frequently Asked Questions

How long can a baby stay in a rear facing car seat?

Most convertible rear-facing car seats accommodate children from birth up to 40-50 pounds, which typically means your child can stay rear-facing until age 3-4 years old. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping children rear-facing as long as possible, ideally until they reach the maximum height or weight limit of their specific seat.

What's the difference between infant and convertible car seats?

Infant car seats are designed for newborns up to about 35 pounds and must be replaced once your child outgrows them, while convertible seats work rear-facing from birth and then convert to forward-facing as your child grows, eliminating the need to buy multiple seats. Convertible seats are more cost-effective long-term since they literally grow with your baby.

Can I use a convertible car seat rear facing in the front seat?

No, it's not recommended to place any rear-facing car seat in the front passenger seat due to airbag deployment risks. Rear-facing seats should always be installed in the back seat, preferably in the center position for maximum safety.

Are convertible car seats that grow with your baby worth the extra cost?

Yes, convertible seats typically cost $200-400 but eliminate the need to buy an infant seat and then a separate booster seat later, saving you $300-500 overall. You'll also reduce waste and have a simpler transition process as your child grows, making them a practical investment for most families.
