Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-have items for a small nursery?

The essentials for a small nursery include a crib or bassinet, a changing table or dresser with a changing pad on top, a safe place to store diapers and clothes, and adequate lighting. Consider multi-functional furniture like a crib with built-in drawers or a dresser that doubles as a changing station to maximize limited space. You'll also want blackout curtains and a white noise machine to help your baby sleep.

Do I need a separate changing table for a small room?

A dedicated changing table isn't necessary for small spaces—a changing pad on top of a dresser, crib, or even a closet shelf works just as well and saves valuable floor space. Make sure whatever surface you choose is at a comfortable height (around 30-36 inches) and has guardrails or is wide enough to prevent falls. Keep all diaper supplies within arm's reach in a caddy or drawer below the changing surface.

What's the best crib option for a tiny nursery?

Mini cribs or portable play yards take up about 40% less space than standard full-size cribs while still meeting safety standards. If you prefer a full-size crib, position it against the wall and choose one with under-crib storage or drawers to utilize vertical space. Measure your room dimensions before purchasing to ensure you have at least 2-3 feet of clearance around the crib for safe access.

What storage solutions work best for small nursery rooms?

Vertical storage is key in small spaces—use wall-mounted shelves, over-the-door organizers, and tall narrow dressers to store clothes, diapers, and supplies without consuming floor space. Closet systems with double rods and shelf dividers can maximize your existing closet space, and clear storage bins help you see contents at a glance while stacking efficiently. Consider a wall-mounted hamper and pegboard for frequently used items to keep surfaces clutter-free.
