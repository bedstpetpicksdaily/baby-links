Frequently Asked Questions

How many positions does the reversible seat actually have?

Two primary positions: parent-facing (ideal from birth to 6 months) and forward-facing (6+ months). There's no middle ground or half-seat adjustments. The transition is straightforward but requires you to remove the seat pad, flip the frame orientation, and reinstall. Most parents make the switch once around the 6-month mark and leave it.

Is this stroller actually travel-friendly, or is that marketing?

Genuinely travel-friendly. The fold dimensions are approximately 25 x 18 x 10 inches, which fits most overhead airline bins and fits comfortably in car trunks alongside luggage. It's not as compact as a basic umbrella stroller, but it's dramatically smaller than traditional full-feature strollers. Real-world test: it passed TSA gate-check limits on multiple airlines.

What's the weight limit, and does it last through toddler years?

Most Colugo models handle up to 50 pounds, which covers most kids through age 4-5. The question isn't whether it *can* hold them, but whether you'll want to push a heavier child in a compact stroller by age 3. Many parents transition to a wagon-style option by toddler years, but the stroller remains functional if you prefer traditional pushing.

How does this compare to UPPAbaby and Baby Jogger reversible models?

UPPAbaby's reversible options (like the Vista) are significantly more expensive ($800+) and heavier, though they offer more accessories and premium materials. Baby Jogger's reversible models split the difference in price and features. Colugo prioritizes compact, lightweight design over accessory ecosystems—you're paying less but getting fewer add-on options. Choose Colugo if portability matters most; choose the premium brands if you want modular systems.
