Finding the right stroller for your newborn and beyond is one of the most important baby gear decisions you'll make—after all, you'll be pushing it daily for the next 2-3 years. What to Look For

  • Lie-flat capability for newborns: Your newborn needs a completely flat sleeping surface for safe napping on the go. A stroller that reclines only partially won't work—look for one that goes fully horizontal, allowing proper airway alignment and spine support for those early months.
  • Reversible or parent-facing seat: Newborns benefit from being close to you, and a reversible seat lets you face your baby while they sleep or during those early bonding months. As they grow and become curious about the world, you can flip the seat to face forward without buying a new stroller.
  • Compatibility with infant car seats: Most parents need their stroller to work with their car seat for seamless transitions. A stroller that accepts your car seat directly means you won't wake your sleeping newborn during transfers—this is a game-changer for real-world parenting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use a travel stroller for a newborn?

Most travel strollers are designed for babies 6 months and older, but some models feature a fully reclined seat or bassinet attachment that's safe for newborns from birth. Always check the manufacturer's specifications to ensure the stroller reclines flat and provides proper head and neck support for a newborn's development.

What's the weight difference between a travel stroller and a regular stroller?

Travel strollers typically weigh 10-15 lbs compared to 25-35 lbs for full-size strollers, making them significantly lighter and easier to carry through airports and fold into overhead bins. This reduced weight doesn't sacrifice durability for most models, which are built to withstand regular use while remaining portable.

How long can a baby use a travel stroller?

Most travel strollers accommodate children up to 40-50 lbs, which typically covers ages newborn (with proper recline/bassinet) through 3-4 years old. Some compact models have lower weight limits of 35-40 lbs, so verify the specifications if you plan to use it beyond the toddler stage.

Is a travel stroller safe for sleeping babies?

Travel strollers with fully reclined seats are safe for sleep if they meet safety standards and provide adequate head and neck support, though a flat bassinet attachment is preferable for newborns. Always ensure the stroller is on a flat surface and never leave your baby unattended, as stroller safety depends on proper setup and supervision.
