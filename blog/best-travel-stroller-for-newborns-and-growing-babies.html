Finding the right stroller for your newborn and beyond is one of the most important baby gear decisions you'll make—after all, you'll be pushing it daily for the next 2-3 years. What to Look For

  • Lie-flat capability for newborns: Your newborn needs a completely flat sleeping surface for safe napping on the go. A stroller that reclines only partially won't work—look for one that goes fully horizontal, allowing proper airway alignment and spine support for those early months.
  • Reversible or parent-facing seat: Newborns benefit from being close to you, and a reversible seat lets you face your baby while they sleep or during those early bonding months. As they grow and become curious about the world, you can flip the seat to face forward without buying a new stroller.
  • Compatibility with infant car seats: Most parents need their stroller to work with their car seat for seamless transitions. A stroller that accepts your car seat directly means you won't wake your sleeping newborn during transfers—this is a game-changer for real-world parenting.

What to Avoid

  • Lightweight travel strollers for your primary stroller: Those ultra-compact strollers are tempting, but they typically don't offer a safe flat recline for newborns and lack the durability and features you'll need long-term. Save lightweight strollers for backup use with older babies.
