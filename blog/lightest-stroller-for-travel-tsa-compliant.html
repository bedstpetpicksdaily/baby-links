Frequently Asked Questions

What is the weight limit for strollers on airplanes?

The TSA doesn't have a specific weight limit for strollers, but airlines typically allow one stroller to be checked for free if it weighs under 40-50 lbs depending on the carrier. Most travel strollers weigh between 6-15 lbs, so they easily fall within airline requirements. Check your specific airline's policy before traveling since some have stricter guidelines.

Can I bring a stroller through TSA security?

Yes, you can bring a stroller through TSA security checkpoints—they're treated like any other item and must go through X-ray screening. You'll need to fold it up and place it on the conveyor belt, then it will be scanned. Some parents choose to gate-check their stroller instead to avoid the hassle of folding/unfolding at security.

What's the lightest stroller that's actually durable for travel?

Lightweight travel strollers typically weigh 6-10 lbs and include models like the GB Pockit (9.3 lbs), Babyzen YOYO2 (13.8 lbs), and UPPAbaby Minu (10.8 lbs). These brands prioritize durability with quality materials while keeping weight minimal, making them ideal for frequent travelers. Look for strollers with aluminum frames and reinforced fabrics to ensure they withstand repeated folding and airport handling.

Do I need a special stroller for air travel or will my regular stroller work?

You don't need a special stroller, but a lightweight, compact travel stroller is much more practical than a full-size one for flying—regular strollers are often 20+ lbs and take up significant luggage space. If you plan to travel frequently, investing in a dedicated travel stroller saves you stress and baggage fees, though a regular stroller will technically still pass TSA requirements.
