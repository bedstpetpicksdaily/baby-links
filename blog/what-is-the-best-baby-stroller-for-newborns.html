What Is the Best Baby Stroller for Newborns?

The best baby stroller for newborns is an infant travel system that combines a rear-facing infant car seat with a stroller frame, allowing you to safely transition your newborn from car to stroller without disturbing their sleep. Look for models with a fully reclined seat, excellent suspension, and a protective canopy to shield delicate newborn skin from the elements.

The Short Answer

For newborns, you need a stroller that offers a completely flat or near-flat recline position since babies can't sit upright. An infant travel system is ideal because it integrates your car seat with a stroller frame, eliminating the need to move a sleeping baby. Key features should include superior suspension for smooth rides, a large canopy for sun protection, and adequate storage for diaper bags and essentials. Prioritize safety certifications and sturdy construction over trendy designs.

The Full Explanation

When selecting a stroller for newborns, understanding the unique needs of infants is essential. Newborns have underdeveloped neck and back muscles, so they require full support and cannot sit upright. This immediately narrows your options to strollers with completely flat or nearly flat recline positions.

Why Infant Travel Systems Excel: An infant travel system combines three products into one cohesive solution—an infant car seat, stroller frame, and base. This design eliminates the need to purchase these items separately and, more importantly, allows you to keep your newborn in their car seat while moving between vehicle and stroller. This is crucial because removing a sleeping newborn from their car seat can disrupt their rest and leave them unsupported.

Suspension and Ride Quality: Newborns are sensitive to jarring movements. Look for strollers with multi-point suspension systems that absorb bumps and vibrations from uneven sidewalks and streets. High-quality suspension creates a smoother ride, which is better for your baby's comfort and development.

Protection from Elements: A newborn's skin is sensitive and vulnerable to UV rays. Choose a stroller with a large, extendable canopy that provides UPF protection. The canopy should cover most of the stroller's seating area without obstructing airflow, which is important for preventing overheating.

Weight and Maneuverability: While safety and comfort are paramount, you'll be pushing this stroller frequently. Consider the weight and ease of folding. Lightweight models are easier to transport and maneuver, but they shouldn't compromise on stability and support.

Storage Capacity: Parents of newborns carry substantial gear—diapers, wipes, bottles, extra clothing, and blankets. Adequate under-seat storage prevents you from having to make trips back to your car and keeps essentials within reach.

What the Experts Say

Pediatricians and child safety experts consistently recommend that newborns remain in a rear-facing car seat, even in strollers. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) emphasizes that proper car seat positioning is critical for newborn safety during vehicle transport. Stroller experts note that the transition from car to stroller should be seamless, which is why travel systems are preferred for the newborn phase. Additionally, certified stroller reviewers recommend testing suspension systems in-store by pushing the stroller over different surfaces to feel the ride quality firsthand. Experts also stress that a stroller purchase should prioritize the newborn phase (0-6 months) even if you plan to use the stroller longer, since your baby will have different needs as they grow.
