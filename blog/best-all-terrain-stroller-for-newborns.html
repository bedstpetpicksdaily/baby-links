Finding the right stroller for your newborn can feel overwhelming, especially when you want something that handles more than just smooth city sidewalks. If you're an active parent who loves getting outdoors—whether that's hiking trails, beach walks, or rough park terrain—an all-terrain stroller designed specifically for newborns offers the perfect blend of rugged capability and the support your tiny one needs during those early months.

What to Look For

  • Fully Reclinable Seat with Newborn Support: Newborns need to lie nearly flat, and their developing spine requires proper support during bumpy rides. Look for strollers that recline to at least 165 degrees (ideally fully flat) and come with quality padding or an infant insert designed specifically for newborns. This ensures your baby stays safe and comfortable even on uneven terrain where the stroller might rock or tilt.
  • Suspension System and Large, Pneumatic Tires: All-terrain strollers need genuine suspension that absorbs shock from rocks, roots, and ruts, not just air-filled tires. Check for independent wheel suspension on at least three wheels, and make sure the tires are pneumatic (air-filled) rather than foam-filled—they provide significantly better shock absorption and traction on unpredictable surfaces.
  • Weight Capacity and Stability on Inclines: Even though your newborn is small, you're adding diaper bags, gear, and possibly a second child later. Choose a stroller with a weight capacity of at least 50 pounds and test how it handles on hills during shopping. A stroller that feels stable when you're pushing uphill will inspire confidence on backcountry trails.
