Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Paxfix work with non-Maxi-Cosi car seats?

No. This base is designed exclusively for Maxi-Cosi car seats that have ISOFIX connectors. Using it with other brands defeats its purpose and creates safety issues. If you have a different brand of car seat, you need the ISOFIX base designed for that seat.

Is ISOFIX installation actually safer than seat-belt installation?

When installed correctly, both methods are equally safe and meet the same safety standards. ISOFIX isn't inherently safer—it's just less likely to be installed incorrectly because there's less room for human error. The real advantage is consistency and peace of mind, not superior crash protection.

How much does this base actually cost compared to using just the car seat with a seat belt?

Prices vary, but expect to pay $80–150 more for the ISOFIX base compared to seat-belt-only installation of the same Maxi-Cosi model. That's real money. Evaluate whether the convenience justifies that premium for your specific situation—frequent travelers might say yes, single-car families might say no.

Will this base fit in my vehicle's ISOFIX points?

Most modern vehicles with ISOFIX points (manufactured 2010+) will accommodate this base, but confirmation is essential. Check your vehicle's manual for ISOFIX location and spacing, or contact Maxi-Cosi support with your vehicle make, model, and year. A few vehicles have unconventional ISOFIX placement that causes fitment issues.

Can multiple bases fit in the same car?

Yes, you can install bases in multiple ISOFIX points in the same vehicle if your car has them (most have at least two rear points). Some vehicles have center ISOFIX points, though those are less common. This is actually a major selling point if you have multiple Maxi-Cosi car seats for different-aged children.
