Frequently Asked Questions

Is white noise safe for babies?

Yes, white noise is safe for babies when used correctly. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping the volume at or below 50 decibels (similar to a quiet office) to protect your baby's hearing. Avoid placing the machine directly next to the crib and never use it continuously throughout the night without breaks.

What's the difference between white noise, pink noise, and brown noise for babies?

White noise contains all sound frequencies at equal volume and mimics TV static, while pink noise is softer and sounds like gentle rain or wind. Brown noise is deeper and more soothing, similar to thunder or a low rumble. Most parents find pink or brown noise more effective for baby sleep since they're less harsh than white noise.

How long should I run a white noise machine for my baby?

Most sleep experts recommend running white noise only during sleep time (naps and nighttime), turning it on just before bed and off when your baby wakes. Limit usage to 8-10 hours per day to prevent your baby from becoming dependent on it or experiencing hearing issues. You can gradually reduce use as your baby gets older.

What features should I look for in a baby white noise machine?

Look for machines with adjustable volume controls, multiple sound options (white/pink/brown noise), and a timer function to auto-shutoff. Additional helpful features include portability, battery backup for travel, and soft nightlights, though avoid models with blue light that can disrupt sleep. Make sure it's designed for baby safety with no loose parts or cords.
