Frequently Asked Questions

How does ClickTight actually compare to regular LATCH installation?

ClickTight eliminates the most common LATCH misuse: over-tightening or under-tightening the connectors without feedback. With ClickTight, you get tactile and audible confirmation that the seat is locked in correctly. Studies show installation errors drop significantly with this system. The tradeoff is that ClickTight only works with the vehicle's seat belt system (not auxiliary LATCH), which is fine since belt installation is actually more stable when done correctly—and ClickTight ensures it is.

Is the Boulevard ClickTight worth upgrading from a basic infant seat?

Only if you plan to keep one car seat through age 6-7. If you're the type to buy an infant seat, then upgrade to a toddler seat, then a booster, you'll spend more total money than buying the Boulevard now. But if you want single-seat simplicity and your infant can use it from day one (with proper positioning), the math works. Just know that rear-facing newborns require the seat reclined at an angle—Britax includes a newborn insert, but it's another thing to manage in those early months.

How does this handle side-impact crashes versus other seats at this price?

The Boulevard's padded side wings are among the deepest in the convertible category at this price point. They extend high enough to cradle the head in a lateral impact. Britax also uses a different foam density in those wings compared to budget competitors. Real-world crash data is limited (seats aren't tested in side impacts as rigorously as frontal), but engineering-wise, Britax is doing more here than most brands at $250-400. You're genuinely paying for that protection, not marketing.
