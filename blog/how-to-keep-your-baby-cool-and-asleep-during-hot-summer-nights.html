Create the Ideal Temperature Environment

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping your baby's room between 68-72°F for optimal sleep. During summer months, achieving this temperature requires strategic planning. Use air conditioning during peak heat hours, or invest in a quality fan to improve air circulation. Keep blackout curtains closed during the day to prevent heat buildup, and open windows during cooler evening hours to naturally ventilate the room.

For additional comfort, dress your baby in lightweight, breathable sleepwear made from organic cotton or bamboo materials. Avoid heavy blankets and opt for a thin cotton sheet or sleep sack designed for warmer climates. Keeping your baby's head uncovered helps with natural heat dissipation, which is essential for safe sleep.

Optimize Bedding and Sleep Setup

Choose moisture-wicking crib sheets that help pull sweat away from your baby's skin. Waterproof mattress protectors with cooling gel technology can also help regulate temperature. Avoid plastic mattress covers, as these trap heat and moisture. Keep the crib minimal—only include fitted sheets and your baby, removing pillows, bumpers, and excess blankets that contribute to heat retention.

Consider elevating the crib slightly or positioning it away from direct sunlight. A portable air purifier with cooling technology can help maintain consistent room temperature while providing white noise that supports sleep—similar to how the Baby Shusher combines sound therapy with a sleep-friendly environment.

What is the safest room temperature for a sleeping baby in summer?

The AAP recommends 68-72°F as ideal for infant sleep. During summer, aim for the coolest temperature your AC can safely maintain. Avoid temperatures below 65°F, which can be too cold. Use a room thermometer to monitor consistently.

Is it safe to use a fan in my baby's room during summer?

Fans can help circulate air and cool the room, but position them away from the crib to avoid direct drafts. Never point a fan directly at your baby. Combine fan usage with white noise solutions like Baby Shusher for optimal sleep conditions.

What should my baby wear to sleep during hot summer nights?
