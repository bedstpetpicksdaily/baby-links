    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long can a baby stay in a forward facing car seat?

    Most forward-facing convertible car seats accommodate children from 20-40 lbs and up to age 4-5, though some premium models extend to 65 lbs or older. The exact duration depends on your child's height and weight relative to the seat's specific limits, so always check the manufacturer's guidelines. Your child can transition out once they exceed the maximum weight or height, or when they're ready for a booster seat.

    What's the difference between convertible and forward facing only car seats?

    Convertible car seats start rear-facing from birth and transition to forward-facing, giving you 8+ years of use, while forward-facing only seats work from around 20 lbs until they outgrow them. Convertible seats are more economical long-term since you won't need to buy multiple seats, but forward-facing only seats are lighter and more portable. Choose based on your budget and whether you want one seat to grow with your child from infancy.

    When can I turn my baby to forward facing in their car seat?

    The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping children rear-facing until age 2 or until they reach the car seat's maximum rear-facing weight limit, whichever comes first. Some seats allow you to flip to forward-facing as early as 1 year old if your child meets the minimum weight requirement (usually 20 lbs), but experts suggest waiting as long as possible for safety. Check your specific seat's manual for its earliest forward-facing eligibility.

    Do forward facing car seats that grow with baby actually save money?

    Yes, convertible car seats that grow from rear to forward-facing typically cost $200-400 upfront but eliminate the need for separate infant and booster seats, saving $300-500+ over time. However, if you plan to have multiple children, the initial investment becomes even more worthwhile since you can reuse the same seat. Compare the total cost of buying three separate seats (infant, convertible, and booster) versus one quality convertible to see your potential savings.
