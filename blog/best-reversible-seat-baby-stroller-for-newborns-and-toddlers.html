    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is a reversible seat stroller and why do I need one?

    A reversible seat stroller has a seat that rotates or flips to face either toward you or away from you. This is beneficial because it allows your newborn to face you for bonding and monitoring, then switch to face outward as they grow and want to explore their surroundings.

    At what age can babies use a reversible stroller?

    Most reversible strollers are safe for newborns from birth, though some require a separate bassinet or infant car seat attachment for the first few months. Once your baby can hold their head up steadily (around 4-6 months), they can typically use the forward-facing or parent-facing seat.

    How do I switch a reversible stroller seat from parent-facing to outward-facing?

    The method depends on the model, but most reversible strollers use either quick-release buttons on the seat frame or require you to unbuckle and manually rotate or flip the seat unit. Always check your specific stroller's manual, as switching mechanisms vary—some take seconds while others require more effort.

    Are reversible strollers good for newborns and toddlers, or should I buy two strollers?

    A quality reversible stroller can work for both newborns and toddlers, making it a cost-effective single purchase that grows with your child. However, you'll need to ensure it accommodates a newborn properly (either with a bassinet or car seat) and can handle the weight of a toddler (typically up to 50 lbs).
