What to Look For

  • Compact fold with one-handed capability: When you're juggling a car seat, diaper bag, and boarding pass, you need a stroller that collapses smoothly without requiring both hands or complex multi-step procedures. Look for models that fold to cabin-size dimensions (under 22 inches long typically) so they fit overhead bins or under airplane seats, and can be managed while holding your baby.
  • Newborn-friendly configurations from day one: Convertible strollers should accept your infant car seat or include a reversible bassinet seat so your newborn can lie completely flat.   • Lightweight but sturdy construction: Travel strollers typically weigh 15-25 pounds, but you'll be lifting this repeatedly at airports, hotels, and car trunks. The frame should feel solid enough to handle uneven terrain and curbs without feeling flimsy, especially when your growing toddler is onboard.
