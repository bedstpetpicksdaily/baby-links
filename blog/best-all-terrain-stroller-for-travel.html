What to Look For

  • Large, air-filled tires with excellent suspension: Look for strollers with wheels at least 10 inches in diameter that can handle gravel, grass, sand, and uneven surfaces without jolting your baby. Air-filled tires provide cushioning that solid wheels simply cannot match, and good suspension absorbs shocks from rocky or root-laden paths.
  • Compact fold and reasonable weight: While all-terrain strollers are naturally heavier than umbrella strollers, you need one that still fits in a car trunk or overhead luggage compartment. Aim for models that weigh under 30 pounds and fold to a manageable size—you'll be hauling this through airports and into rental cars.
  • Reversible seat and quality sun protection: When traveling, your stroller becomes a multi-purpose tool. A reversible seat lets you face your baby toward you on crowded streets or face them outward for sightseeing. Deep canopies and peek-a-boo windows are essential for protecting your baby from variable weather and sun exposure across different climates.
