Choosing a car seat for your newborn is one of the most important safety decisions you'll make as a parent. What to Look For

  • NHTSA 5-Star Crash Test Ratings: Prioritize seats that have earned top marks in both frontal and side-impact crash tests. These ratings reflect real-world collision scenarios and show how well a seat protects your newborn during accidents. Don't rely on marketing claims alone—verify the actual test data on the NHTSA website.
  • Superior Harness Systems: Look for seats with adjustable, multi-position harnesses that keep newborns snugly secured without being uncomfortable. The harness should be easy to adjust as your baby grows, and it should work seamlessly with the seat's shell design to distribute crash forces evenly across your child's body.
  • Installation Certainty: Choose a seat with clear LATCH connectors and/or a secure belt path that makes correct installation straightforward. Many crashes are survivable, but improper installation significantly reduces protection. Seats with load legs or stability feet provide additional support and make proper positioning easier to achieve.
