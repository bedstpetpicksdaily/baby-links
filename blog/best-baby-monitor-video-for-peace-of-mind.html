    What features should I look for in a baby monitor video?

    Look for high-definition video (at least 1080p), night vision with infrared or low-light capability, and a wide viewing angle (at least 110 degrees) to see your entire nursery. Additional features like two-way audio, temperature monitoring, and movement/sound alerts provide extra peace of mind and help you respond quickly to your baby's needs.

    Is a WiFi baby monitor safe and secure?

    WiFi monitors can be secure if you choose a reputable brand with encrypted connections and strong privacy policies. Look for monitors with two-factor authentication, regular security updates, and avoid storing footage on unsecured cloud services; some parents prefer closed-network monitors that don't require internet for local viewing.

    How far can a baby monitor video camera transmit?

    WiFi-enabled monitors work anywhere you have internet access, even outside your home, while closed-network monitors typically work within 700-900 feet of the base station depending on walls and interference. If you plan to use the monitor across multiple rooms or outside frequently, a WiFi monitor is more convenient, though closed-network options offer faster, more reliable connections within your home.

    Do I need a video baby monitor or is audio-only enough?

    Video monitors provide significantly more peace of mind since you can instantly see if your baby is safe, comfortable, or rolling over, rather than just hearing sounds that could be misleading. For most parents, video is worth the investment, especially for newborns and restless sleepers, though some use audio-only monitors as a budget-friendly backup option.
