What to Look For

  • Newborn-Safe Suspension and Support: Look for joggers with excellent shock absorption and a fully reclinable seat that lies completely flat or near-flat. Newborns can't sit upright safely for extended periods, so you need a stroller that accommodates their developmental needs while you run. Check that the seat offers proper head and neck support, and verify it's rated for infants from birth (not 6 months old).
  • Double or Expandable Configuration: Since you're managing both a newborn and toddler, choose a jogging stroller with either a built-in second seat, an attachable platform, or the ability to add a sibling seat. This eliminates the need to purchase two separate strollers and keeps both children secure in one unit. Make sure the weight capacity is adequate for your combined children's weights.
  • Lightweight Yet Durable Frame: Jogging strollers are heavier than standard strollers, but when you're pushing two kids while running, every pound matters. Seek models made with aluminum or reinforced plastic frames that balance durability with maneuverability. Test the stroller's responsiveness on turns—you'll need smooth handling to stay safe while jogging with your family.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use a jogging stroller for a newborn?

    Most jogging strollers aren't suitable for newborns under 6 months old because their necks lack the strength to handle the bouncing and jolting movements. However, some premium models like the BOB Revolution Flex allow you to attach an infant car seat or bassinet attachment for younger babies, making them versatile from birth.

    What's the difference between a jogging stroller and a regular stroller?

    Jogging strollers feature three wheels (typically a fixed front wheel), superior suspension systems, and a hand brake for better control during running. Regular strollers prioritize maneuverability and compact storage, while jogging strollers are built for stability and performance on uneven terrain at higher speeds.

    How much should I spend on a jogging stroller?

    Quality jogging strollers range from $300-$700, with budget options around $200-300 offering basic features and premium brands like BOB and Thule costing $500+. Investing in a mid-range to higher-end model ($400-600) typically ensures better suspension, durability, and features that will last through multiple children.

    Is a jogging stroller safe for daily use and walking?

    Yes, jogging strollers are safe for daily walks and errands, though their three-wheel design makes them slightly less maneuverable in tight spaces like grocery stores. They're more durable and stable than standard strollers, making them excellent all-purpose options if you plan to both jog and run daily errands.
