    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do movement detecting baby monitors work if my baby stops breathing?

    Movement-detecting monitors alert you when your baby hasn't moved for a set period (typically 15-20 seconds), which can indicate no breathing. However, they're not a substitute for medical-grade apnea monitors and may produce false alarms from vibrations or pet movement. For babies at high risk of SIDS or with breathing concerns, consult your pediatrician about clinical-grade alternatives.

    What's the difference between movement detecting and video baby monitors?

    Movement-detecting monitors use sensor pads under the mattress to track motion and alert you if movement stops, while video monitors let you see your baby in real-time via camera. Many parents use both together for the most comprehensive monitoring—video monitors provide peace of mind through visibility, while movement sensors catch cessations in breathing or movement.

    Are movement detecting baby monitors safe for my newborn?

    Yes, modern movement-detecting monitors with under-mattress sensors are considered safe when placed correctly under a firm mattress—they don't contact your baby directly. The sensor pad should never go under pillows, blankets, or bumpers, as these can muffle the signal. Always follow manufacturer guidelines and consult your pediatrician if your baby has specific health concerns.

    How accurate are movement detecting baby monitors?

    Most quality movement monitors are 95%+ accurate at detecting motion when properly installed, but they can have false positives from pet movement or vibrations near the crib. False negatives are rarer but possible if the sensor pad shifts or isn't making proper contact with the mattress. Check reviews for specific models and ensure you calibrate sensitivity settings according to your baby's sleep patterns.
