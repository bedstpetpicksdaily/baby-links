    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long can you use a convertible car seat from infant to toddler?

    Most convertible car seats can be used from birth (with an infant insert) until your child reaches 40-65 pounds, typically lasting from newborn through age 4-6 years. This extended usability makes them cost-effective since you won't need to purchase multiple seats as your child grows. Always check your specific model's weight and height limits, as these vary by brand.

    Can you use a convertible car seat rear-facing for infants?

    Yes, convertible car seats are designed to be used rear-facing from birth and typically remain rear-facing until your child reaches the seat's maximum rear-facing weight limit (usually 40-50 pounds). Rear-facing is the safest position for infants and young toddlers because it better protects their head, neck, and spine in a crash. Once they outgrow rear-facing, you can turn the seat forward-facing.

    What's the difference between convertible and all-in-one car seats?

    Convertible car seats handle infant-to-toddler stages and typically go up to about 65 pounds, while all-in-one seats often extend to booster mode and can accommodate children up to 120 pounds. All-in-one seats offer longer usability but are generally bulkier and more expensive, whereas convertible seats are more affordable and easier to install in most vehicles. Choose based on your budget and whether you want a single seat for your child's entire car seat journey.

    Do convertible car seats work with infant car seat bases?

    No, convertible car seats are not compatible with infant car seat bases because they're designed as standalone seats, not snap-in carriers. However, many convertible seats have their own built-in bases or compatibility with vehicle LATCH systems for secure installation. If you want the convenience of a snap-in carrier for frequent transitions, you'd need a separate infant car seat in addition to a convertible seat.
