Nursery Essentials You Need Before Baby Arrives

You'll need a safe crib with a firm mattress, fitted sheets, and a waterproof mattress protector, plus adequate storage, proper lighting, and climate control to create a functional nursery. The essentials focus on safety, comfort, and practicality rather than decorative items.

The Short Answer

Before your baby arrives, prioritize the big-ticket items: a crib, mattress, bedding, and dresser for storage. Add a safe place to change diapers, adequate lighting for nighttime feedings, and temperature control to keep your baby comfortable. You don't need expensive decorations or excess furniture—focus on functionality and safety instead.

The Full Explanation

Sleeping Setup

A safe crib is your foundation. Choose one that meets current safety standards and fits your space. You'll need at least 2-3 fitted crib sheets, a firm mattress (memory foam isn't recommended for infants), and a waterproof mattress protector to handle inevitable accidents. A white noise machine helps establish sleep routines and masks household sounds.

Diaper Changing Station

You can use a dedicated changing table, a dresser with a changing pad on top, or even a bed with a portable changing pad. Stock it with diapers, wipes, diaper cream, and a small trash bin for quick disposal. Keep frequently used items within arm's reach so you're never far from supplies during changes.

Storage and Organization

A dresser or closet system keeps clothing, blankets, and supplies organized and accessible. Invest in drawer dividers and labeled bins to maximize space. Open shelving or a small bookcase works for blankets, swaddles, and sleep sacks.

Lighting Solutions

Install a dimmer switch or use a soft nightlight for nighttime care. A dedicated nursery lamp with adjustable brightness helps during diaper changes without stimulating your baby. Blackout curtains support healthy sleep patterns by blocking outside light.

Climate Control

A room thermometer ensures the nursery stays between 68-72°F. A humidifier helps during cold months, while a fan provides air circulation and white noise. Avoid placing the crib near windows, vents, or radiators where temperature fluctuates.

Safety Essentials

Install a baby monitor with video capability to watch your infant remotely. Ensure all furniture is securely anchored to walls to prevent tipping. Remove pillows, bumpers, blankets, and stuffed animals from the crib—keep it bare for safe sleep.

What the Experts Say

The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) recommends room-sharing without bed-sharing for at least the first six months, ideally the first year. This means your baby sleeps in their own crib in your room, then transitions to their nursery. Safety experts emphasize that an empty crib is a safe crib—avoid all soft objects and loose bedding that pose suffocation risks. The focus should be on creating a calm, organized space that supports your baby's sleep and makes nighttime care easier for parents.
