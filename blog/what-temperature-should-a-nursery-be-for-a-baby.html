What Temperature Should a Nursery Be for a Baby?

The ideal nursery temperature for a baby is between 68-72°F (20-22°C), with 70°F (21°C) being the optimal target. This temperature range helps prevent overheating, reduces the risk of Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS), and keeps your baby comfortable throughout the day and night.

The Short Answer

Keeping your baby's nursery between 68-72°F is the goldstandard recommended by pediatricians and the American Academy of Pediatrics. Babies struggle to regulate their body temperature, so a slightly cool room combined with appropriate bedding and sleepwear prevents both overheating and chilling. The key is consistency—maintaining a stable temperature throughout the day helps your baby sleep better and stay safer. Avoid extreme temperatures in either direction, as both excessive heat and cold can disrupt sleep and pose health risks.

The Full Explanation

Understanding why temperature matters for your baby is essential for creating a safe sleep environment. Unlike adults, newborns and infants cannot efficiently regulate their body temperature. They're particularly vulnerable to overheating, which is a known risk factor for SIDS. A room that's too warm can cause your baby to overheat, even if they don't seem sweaty or uncomfortable.

The 68-72°F range is ideal because it's cool enough to prevent overheating but warm enough that your baby won't lose body heat too rapidly. This sweet spot works best when combined with proper bedding—a fitted sheet, a sleep sack or wearable blanket, and age-appropriate sleepwear. Never use loose blankets, pillows, or bumpers in the crib, as these increase both overheating risk and suffocation hazards.

Seasonal changes require adjustment. In summer, your air conditioning should maintain the lower end of the range (around 68-70°F). In winter, keep the nursery at 70-72°F with a humidifier to combat dry air from heating systems. Humidity levels should stay between 40-60% to prevent respiratory irritation and maintain comfort.

Room temperature also affects sleep quality. Babies fall asleep more easily in slightly cool environments and experience deeper, more restorative sleep. A room that's too warm can cause restlessness, frequent waking, and discomfort. Conversely, a room that's too cold may cause your baby to cry or wake frequently as their body works to generate warmth.

What the Experts Say

The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) emphasizes that maintaining a moderate room temperature is a critical component of safe sleep practices. Their research indicates that overheating during sleep is associated with increased SIDS risk, making temperature control one of the most important factors parents can control. Pediatricians consistently recommend the 68-72°F range as the safest and most comfortable option for infants of all ages, from newborns through toddlers.

Sleep specialists note that temperature preferences can vary slightly from baby to baby. Some infants sleep better at 70°F, while others prefer 68°F. The key is finding your baby's optimal temperature and maintaining consistency. Many parents discover their baby's preference through observation—a baby who sleeps restlessly or wakes frequently may be too warm or too cold.
