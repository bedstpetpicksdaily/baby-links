Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ISOFIX base really necessary, or can I just use the seat belt?

Honestly, no—it's not necessary. Modern car seats install securely with the vehicle's seat belt. The ISOFIX base is a convenience upgrade. You get faster installation, one-click attachment, and no re-installation hassle if you swap the base between vehicles. If you're moving your car seat to grandma's car twice a week, the base saves time and eliminates installation anxiety. If you're using one seat in one car, seat belt installation is free and reliable.

What's the actual price difference between buying the seat with a base versus without?

The Paxfix base typically runs $150–$220 depending on sales and current month (June deals often exist around summer travel season). A compatible Maxi-Cosi infant or convertible seat ranges from $200–$400. So you're looking at roughly 40–50% additional cost for the base. Ask yourself: how many vehicles will this base live in? If it's two or more, the math justifies it. If it's one vehicle for three years, probably not.

Does this work with all Maxi-Cosi car seats?

Not all. The Paxfix is compatible with Maxi-Cosi's ISOFIX-ready car seats (primarily infant carriers and convertible models), but not their budget or older models. Check the compatibility chart on the product page before buying. If you're already committed to Maxi-Cosi, this makes sense. If you're flexible with brands, universal ISOFIX bases exist but come with their own limitations.
