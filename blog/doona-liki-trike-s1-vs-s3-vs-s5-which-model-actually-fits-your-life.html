Doona Liki Trike S1

The entry point in the Doona line, the S1 is positioned as the budget-conscious option that doesn't sacrifice the core compact-fold promise. Weighs approximately 4.5kg (10 lbs) and folds to a size that fits most car trunks without removing seats or using a roof rack. Folding takes under 30 seconds once you learn the sequence, though there is a learning curve. Includes parent handle, adjustable seat spanning roughly 18 months to 4 years, and fits the budget-aware buyer willing to compromise on some features for lower price.

Best for: Budget-conscious parents, second-child users, or those wanting to test the Doona concept without the S3/S5 investment. Works if you're comparing against $120-140 budget tricycles and need a genuine step up without the premium price.

Doona Liki Trike S3

The middle-tier "sweet spot" model that Doona emphasizes for real-space living. Folds to roughly 24x16x10 inches—approximately umbrella-stroller size—fitting under stroller baskets and closet shelves. Aluminum frame at 6.7 lbs is lighter than S1, with parent-controlled steering, 3-point harness, and removable push handle. The key differentiator: S3 functions as a genuine three-mode system (parent-controlled trike, guided tricycle, independent riding) without tools or complicated assembly. Reversible seat and extended UV-protective canopy included. Age range spans roughly 10 months through age 3, starting earlier than S1.

Best for: Apartment dwellers, frequent travelers, or families needing genuine versatility across developmental stages. Best choice if you're transitioning from an infant carrier and want one tool to handle multiple toddler phases without buying separate gear.

Doona Liki Trike S5

The premium model tested for real-world durability through farmers markets, beach trips, and cross-state summer travel. Folds to umbrella-stroller size, comparable to S3, but distinguished by enhanced comfort features: genuinely comfortable seat with actual backrest support (not flat plastic), oversized air-filled tires providing pneumatic suspension over sidewalk cracks and rough pavement, responsive steering geometry that handles obstacle navigation, and noticeably solid build quality with no rattle or creaking after six weeks of frequent use. Frame durability withstood minor curb collision without bending or loosening. However, parent handle doesn't disconnect cleanly, leaving exposed connection points.

Best for: Parents prioritizing comfort and durability over price, frequent outdoor users, or those planning extended use across multiple children. Best if you're planning 45-minute+ rides and your toddler complains about soreness on other tricycles, or if you need maximum reliability for travel.

Frequently Asked Questions

How small does the Doona actually fold compared to a regular stroller?

S3 and S5 fold to roughly 24x16x10 inches (approximately umbrella-stroller size), fitting under stroller baskets and standard closet shelves. S1 is similarly compact but slightly bulkier. All three fit in most car trunks without removing front passenger seats—the main advantage over full-size tricycles that require roof racks or dedicated floor space.

What's the real age range? Can my 18-month-old use it?

S1 starts at roughly 18 months (with parent handle). S3 starts at 10 months (parent-controlled mode), making it better for younger toddlers transitioning from infant carriers. S5 specifications weren't explicitly detailed but appears similar to S3. All extend to age 3+, though comfort narrows for larger children due to the compact seat design.

How long does it actually take to fold after you learn the sequence?

Under 30 seconds once learned, though the first 5-10 folds feel awkward. S1 owners report the mechanism can get sticky in humid conditions over time, requiring occasional maintenance. S3 and S5 haven't reported this issue as commonly, possibly due to better materials.

Will my toddler in summer clothes fit comfortably?

The narrow seat (necessary for compact folding) means bulky summer clothing creates snugness. Standard toddler clothing fits fine. If your child runs large or you're in hot climates where puffy clothes are typical, try the S5, which has a more comfortable backrest and seat design for longer rides.

Is the parent handle essential, or can I skip it?

The handle is practically essential for 18-36 month olds who lack pedaling power. S1 and S3 include it; S5 includes it but doesn't disconnect cleanly. Budget tricycles often skip the handle and charge $80-120 extra, making Doona's inclusion valuable. Skip it only if your child is already independently pedaling.

How does this compare to other 'compact' tricycles like XJD?

XJD and similar budget brands ($120-140) fold but are bulkier, heavier, and lack parent steering control. Doona costs 2.5x more but includes responsive steering, better harness security, lighter weight (no back strain), and genuine three-mode versatility (S3 only). Whether that justifies the price depends on your space constraints and budget—Doona solves actual logistics problems, not just marketed portability.
