Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature range should a baby monitor have?

Most pediatricians recommend keeping a baby's room between 68-72°F (20-22°C), so look for monitors that display temperatures in this range with accuracy of at least ±1°F. The best temperature baby monitors will alert you if the room gets too hot (above 75°F) or too cold (below 65°F) to help prevent SIDS and discomfort.

How accurate are baby monitor temperature readings?

High-quality baby monitors are typically accurate within ±1-2°F, which is precise enough for monitoring your baby's comfort. However, placement matters—keep the monitor away from direct sunlight, heating vents, and air conditioners, as these can cause false readings.

Do I need a baby monitor with temperature and humidity?

While temperature is the priority, humidity levels between 40-60% also affect your baby's sleep quality and respiratory health. Many parents find a combo monitor helpful for preventing dry skin and congestion, though a separate humidifier with humidity tracking can work just as well.

Can a baby temperature monitor prevent SIDS?

While a temperature monitor can't prevent SIDS directly, maintaining an optimal room temperature (68-72°F) is one of the evidence-based risk reduction strategies recommended by pediatricians. A good monitor gives you peace of mind by alerting you immediately if the room becomes dangerously hot or cold.
