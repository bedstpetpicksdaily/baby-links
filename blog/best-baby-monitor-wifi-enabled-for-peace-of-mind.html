Frequently Asked Questions

Are WiFi baby monitors secure and can hackers access my baby's camera?

Quality WiFi baby monitors use encryption and secure servers to protect your feed, but security depends on choosing reputable brands and using strong passwords. Always select monitors from established companies with good security track records, enable two-factor authentication when available, and keep firmware updated to minimize hacking risks.

What's the difference between WiFi and non-WiFi baby monitors?

WiFi monitors let you view your baby from anywhere using your smartphone, while non-WiFi monitors typically require a base station and only work within range (usually 700-900 feet). WiFi monitors offer more flexibility for working parents or traveling, but non-WiFi options have no subscription fees and don't depend on internet reliability.

Do WiFi baby monitors work without WiFi or need internet all the time?

Most WiFi baby monitors require an internet connection to send live video to your phone, so they won't work during internet outages. However, many models can still record locally to a microSD card or function on local network backup, allowing limited use without WiFi.

How much does a good WiFi baby monitor cost?

Quality WiFi baby monitors range from $50-$300 depending on features like pan-and-tilt, night vision, two-way audio, and temperature monitoring. Budget options ($50-$100) cover basic streaming, while mid-range ($100-$200) adds better video quality and smart features, and premium monitors ($200+) include advanced analytics and AI-powered alerts.
