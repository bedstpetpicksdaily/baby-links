    Frequently Asked Questions

    Are silicone baby bottles safe for newborns?

    Yes, silicone baby bottles are safe for newborns when made from food-grade, BPA-free silicone. They're softer than glass or plastic, reducing injury risk if dropped, and don't leach chemicals when heated. Always choose bottles from reputable brands that meet safety standards like FDA or EU regulations.

    Do silicone baby bottles work with all breast pumps?

    Most silicone bottles are compatible with standard breast pump flanges, but compatibility depends on the bottle's rim size and shape. Check your specific pump's documentation or the bottle manufacturer's compatibility list before purchasing to ensure a proper seal.

    How long do silicone baby bottles last?

    Quality silicone bottles typically last 12-18 months of regular use before showing signs of wear like discoloration or degradation. They're more durable than disposable bottles but less long-lasting than glass, and should be replaced sooner if you notice cracks, leaks, or material breakdown.

    Can you sterilize silicone baby bottles in a bottle sterilizer?

    Yes, silicone bottles can be sterilized using electric steam sterilizers, though you should check your bottle's temperature rating first. Most silicone bottles are dishwasher-safe and can also be boiled, but avoid extremely high heat which may degrade the material over time.
