What to Look For

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the safest car seat for newborns under $200?

The Cosco Scenera Next and Graco SnugRide SnugLock 35 are top-rated affordable options under $200 that meet all federal safety standards. Both have excellent crash test ratings and rear-facing designs recommended for newborns up to 35 pounds. Look for models with side-impact protection and easy installation features when comparing budget options.

Is a cheaper car seat as safe as an expensive one?

Yes, all car seats sold in the US must meet the same federal safety standards (FMVSS 213), so price doesn't determine safety in crashes. The main differences between budget and premium seats are extra padding, detachable bases, and convenience features—not crash protection. Focus on proper installation and usage rather than brand cost when prioritizing safety.

How do I know if a budget car seat is reliable?

Check NHTSA crash test ratings, read reviews from parents and safety organizations like Safe Kids, and verify the seat has a 5-point harness system. Confirm the manufacturer is established, offers good customer service, and provides clear installation instructions. Avoid used seats from unknown sources or those involved in recalls.

What features matter most in an affordable newborn car seat?

Prioritize a rear-facing design, secure harness system, compatibility with your vehicle, and easy installation—these directly impact safety. Additional features like removable covers, adjustable bases, and lightweight frames are nice conveniences but not essential for protection. Make sure the seat's weight limit accommodates your newborn and will grow with them to at least 35 pounds.
