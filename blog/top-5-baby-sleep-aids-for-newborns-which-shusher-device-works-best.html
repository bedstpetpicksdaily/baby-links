2. Traditional White Noise Machines

White noise machines have been around for years and remain a popular choice for parents seeking ambient sound solutions. These devices produce consistent background noise that masks household sounds and creates a calming environment. Most white noise machines offer multiple sound options—from rain and ocean waves to fan sounds—giving parents variety in their sleep strategy.

While effective for many families, traditional white noise machines can be bulkier and may lack the targeted "shushing" sound that newborns respond to most favorably. However, they're often more affordable and offer longer battery life than specialized shusher devices.

3. Swaddling Wraps and Sound Combinations

Some parents find success combining white noise or shushing devices with swaddling wraps. Swaddling mimics the womb environment and can be paired with the Baby Shusher or similar devices for enhanced soothing effects. This multi-sensory approach addresses both physical comfort and auditory cues that newborns find reassuring.

4. Pacifiers and Comfort Items

While not strictly "sleep aids," pacifiers work wonderfully alongside sound devices. The combination of shushing sounds from a device like the Baby Shusher plus a pacifier can create a comprehensive soothing strategy that addresses multiple comfort needs simultaneously.

5. Weighted Sleep Sacks

Weighted sleep sacks provide gentle pressure that some babies find comforting. When combined with white noise or shushing sounds, they create a multi-layered approach to infant sleep. These work best for babies over a certain weight and should always follow safe sleep guidelines.
