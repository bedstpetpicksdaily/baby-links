What to Look For

  • LATCH system with clear indicators: Look for car seats that feature color-coded LATCH connectors and tactile feedback when properly connected. This visual and physical confirmation helps you know immediately whether the installation is secure, without having to consult the manual repeatedly. Newborns are particularly vulnerable to impact, so foolproof LATCH installation is essential.
  • Minimal base complexity: The simpler the base, the easier the installation. Avoid seats with overly complicated recline mechanisms or adjustment systems that require multiple steps. Many new parents struggle with bases that have numerous dials and levers—you want something that clicks into place and stays there.
  • Clear, comprehensive labeling: Every attachment point, strap adjustment, and installation step should be clearly marked directly on the seat itself. While manuals are helpful, on-seat labeling means you can troubleshoot installation issues without constantly flipping through pages. This is especially valuable when your pediatrician or nurse wants to verify your installation is correct.
