What to Look For

  • Crystal-clear night vision with minimal false alerts: Peace of mind depends on being able to see exactly what's happening in dim lighting. Look for infrared technology that provides true grayscale night vision (not green or pink-tinted) so you can actually discern your baby's facial expressions and confirm they're sleeping peacefully, not just getting a murky silhouette that keeps you guessing.
  • Two-way audio with sound and movement detection: The ability to hear your baby clearly and talk to them soothes both of you instantly. Movement detection alerts that distinguish between normal sleep movements and potential concerns (like positioning issues) help you respond appropriately without jolting awake at every little sound your baby makes.
  • Reliable remote access and long battery life on the parent unit: True peace of mind means monitoring from anywhere in your home—or checking in from another room while keeping both hands free for household tasks. A parent unit that lasts an entire night without dying at 3 AM and maintains a stable connection prevents anxiety from technical failures rather than actual baby concerns.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What features should I look for in a video baby monitor?

    Look for high-resolution video (at least 720p), night vision capability, two-way audio so you can talk to your baby, and a reliable WiFi connection with secure encryption. Temperature monitoring is also valuable since overheating is a SIDS risk factor. Pan and zoom features help you keep tabs on different areas of the crib without moving the camera.

    Is a WiFi baby monitor safe and secure?

    WiFi monitors are secure if you choose reputable brands that use encrypted connections and require strong passwords. Avoid older or budget models with poor security records, as hackers have been known to access unsecured baby monitors. Always update your monitor's firmware regularly and use a strong, unique password.

    How far away can I view my baby monitor?

    WiFi monitors work from anywhere with an internet connection, so you can check in from another room, your backyard, or even while you're away from home. Closed-system monitors (without WiFi) typically have a range of 700-900 feet depending on obstacles and interference in your home.

    Do I need a baby monitor with a parent unit or is an app enough?

    An app-only monitor is convenient and works anywhere with WiFi, but a dedicated parent unit is more reliable since it doesn't depend on your phone battery or WiFi strength. Many parents prefer having both options—an app for when they're away and a dedicated unit for everyday use at home.
