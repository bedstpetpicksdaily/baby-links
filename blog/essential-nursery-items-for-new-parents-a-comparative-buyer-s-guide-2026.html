Setting up a nursery feels overwhelming because you're genuinely choosing between different approaches: a convertible crib that grows with your child, a space-efficient multi-function solution, or traditional separate pieces. Each approach has real trade-offs in cost, durability, and long-term flexibility.

This guide compares the actual products parents mention—the DaVinci Kalani convertible crib, the Graco Pack 'n Play system, and the core furniture categories—so you can match your nursery priorities (budget, space, durability, or flexibility) to the option that actually fits your situation.

DaVinci Kalani 4-in-1 Convertible Crib with Organic Mattress & Bedding Set

The Kalani converts from full-size crib to toddler bed, daybed, and full-size bed. It comes with non-toxic wood construction and an included organic mattress, eliminating separate purchases. The stationary design (no wheels) provides stability, and adjustable mattress heights accommodate newborn through early childhood stages.

Best for: Parents planning to use one crib long-term (birth through childhood) and willing to invest upfront for durability and fewer future purchases.

Graco Pack 'n Play with Changing Station and Bassinet

This combines a bassinet for newborns, an integrated changing station with built-in storage, a portable play yard, and toddler play space in one compact unit. It's certified by the Consumer Product Safety Commission and eliminates the need to buy three separate items. The changing table saves you from purchasing a standalone dresser.

Best for: New parents working with limited nursery space or budget constraints who need a multi-function solution that serves as both sleeping and changing infrastructure.

Traditional Separate Pieces: Crib + Dresser/Changing Table Combination

A dedicated crib (firm mattress, proper slat spacing under 2⅜ inches) paired with a dresser serving as a changing table with storage. This allows you to choose each piece independently and often find budget-friendly options. Includes separate items for window treatments (blackout curtains), lighting (nightlight or dimmable lamp), and climate control (thermometer, fan, or humidifier).

Best for: Parents with adequate nursery space who want flexibility to select individual pieces based on aesthetic preference and room layout.

Mini Crib or Bassinet (Early Months) + Full-Size Crib (Long-Term)

Bassinets keep newborns close during the first 4-6 months, then transition to a full-size crib as your baby grows. This two-step approach allows you to keep your baby in your room initially and move to the nursery later. Mini cribs are more portable for apartment living or homes without dedicated nurseries.

Best for: Parents who prioritize having their newborn sleep nearby initially and don't need a one-piece solution lasting from birth through toddlerhood.

Our Verdict

The right choice depends on three concrete factors: timeline (are you keeping this nursery for one child or five?), space (dedicated nursery or shared bedroom?), and upfront budget.

Choose the DaVinci Kalani if you're planning 10+ years of use, have adequate space, and want a single piece that eliminates future bed purchases. The organic mattress and multi-stage conversion justify the premium cost only with that long-term perspective.

Choose the Graco Pack 'n Play if your nursery is small, your budget is tight, or you need flexibility to keep your newborn in your room for the first months. You'll spend less overall and can upgrade to a full-size crib later without feeling wasteful.

Choose separate pieces if you have space, enjoy customizing individual furniture items, or want to mix budget and premium options (e.g., an affordable IKEA dresser with a high-quality mattress). This approach lets you spread costs across time rather than requiring a large upfront investment.

Across all options, your actual essentials remain consistent: a firm, properly-spaced crib or safe sleep surface; blackout curtains for sleep regulation; a nightlight for middle-of-the-night safety; storage for supplies; and temperature/climate monitoring between 68-72°F. The product you choose is the delivery method for these functional requirements, not the requirement itself.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I actually need a dedicated changing table?

No. A dresser with a changing pad on top works identically and provides dual-purpose storage. The key is having a height-appropriate surface with easy access to diapers, wipes, and creams. This prevents back strain during the hundreds of diaper changes you'll do in the first year.

Are blackout curtains really necessary?

Yes—they make a measurable difference in sleep quality. Blackout curtains regulate your baby's circadian rhythm by blocking external light during daytime naps and early summer mornings. This helps establish consistent sleep patterns, which matters for both your baby and your own rest.

What's the ideal nursery temperature?

Between 68-72°F (20-22°C). Monitor temperature with a reliable room thermometer and adjust using a fan, humidifier, or space heater as needed. Temperature regulation is essential for both comfort and safe sleep—overheating increases SIDS risk.

Do I need a baby monitor if the nursery is nearby?

A monitor with two-way audio and reliable connectivity gives you peace of mind and lets you respond to your baby without waking them fully. If your nursery is directly adjacent to your bedroom and you hear well, you might skip it, but most parents find the ability to listen (and sometimes speak back) helpful during sleep-deprived early months.

Is the included mattress in the DaVinci actually good quality?

Yes—the organic mattress inclusion eliminates a major purchase and decision. However, you'll still want several fitted sheets, a waterproof mattress protector, and potentially a second mattress for the crib-to-toddler-bed stage if you plan extended use. The included mattress is functional but not luxurious.

Can I use the Graco Pack 'n Play as my only sleep surface for the first year?

Yes, it meets safety standards and works for that timeline. However, it's less sturdy than a full-size crib for long-term daily use. Many parents use it for 4-6 months in their bedroom, then transition to a full-size crib in the nursery. This two-step approach is common and reduces the need to purchase the most expensive option upfront.
