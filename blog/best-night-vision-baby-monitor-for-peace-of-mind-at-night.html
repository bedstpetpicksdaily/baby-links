    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the best night vision range for a baby monitor?

    Most parents need a night vision range of at least 10-15 feet to see their baby clearly in a standard-sized nursery. Premium models offer 20+ feet of range, which is useful for larger rooms or if you want to monitor from further away, but anything less than 8 feet may leave blind spots in your room's corners.

    Is infrared or thermal night vision better for baby monitors?

    Infrared night vision is the standard for baby monitors and provides clear black-and-white footage without disturbing your baby with visible light. Thermal imaging is more expensive and unnecessary for monitoring a sleeping infant, as infrared performs just as well for typical nursery setups.

    Do night vision baby monitors emit harmful radiation?

    No, infrared night vision in baby monitors is safe and emits no harmful radiation—it simply uses invisible infrared light to capture video, similar to security cameras. The FDA has not identified any safety concerns with standard baby monitor night vision technology.

    Can I use a night vision baby monitor without WiFi?

    Yes, many closed-system baby monitors work without WiFi using a secure closed connection between the camera and parent unit, which actually offers better privacy and no hacking risk. WiFi-enabled monitors offer remote viewing from your phone but require a strong internet connection and have potential security vulnerabilities.
