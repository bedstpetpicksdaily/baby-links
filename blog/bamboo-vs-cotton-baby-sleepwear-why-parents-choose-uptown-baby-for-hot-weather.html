Bamboo vs Cotton: The Key Differences

While both bamboo and cotton are natural fibers, they have distinct properties that affect how they perform in warm weather. Cotton is breathable and soft, making it a classic choice for baby clothing. However, bamboo fabric—particularly viscose bamboo—offers superior moisture-wicking capabilities and temperature regulation. Bamboo fabric can absorb up to three times more moisture than cotton, making it ideal for babies who sleep hot or sweat during the night. Additionally, bamboo has natural thermoregulatory properties that help maintain optimal skin temperature, whether your baby is too warm or too cool.

Is bamboo fabric safe for newborns and sensitive skin?

How much cooler is bamboo sleepwear compared to cotton?

Bamboo fabric can regulate body temperature more effectively than cotton due to its superior moisture-wicking properties. Babies sleeping in bamboo often experience less sweating and feel cooler throughout the night, especially in hot and humid climates.
