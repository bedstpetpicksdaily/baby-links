What to Look For

High-Waisted Design With Gentle Compression

The waistband should sit comfortably above your hips without digging in—you need coverage that doesn't aggravate your healing core. Look for shorts with a wide, soft waistband (at least 2-3 inches) that provides light compression without feeling restrictive. Avoid anything with elastic that creates a tight band directly over your incision or sensitive abdominal areas. The compression should feel like a gentle hug, not a squeeze.

Breathable, Moisture-Wicking Fabric

Cotton blends alone won't cut it in summer heat. Search for fabrics that specifically mention moisture-wicking properties—nylon-spandex blends, bamboo fabrics, or specialized postpartum recovery materials that pull sweat away from your skin. The fabric should feel cool to the touch and allow air circulation. Check reviews mentioning how the shorts perform in heat; if multiple reviewers praise how they "don't stick to your skin," that's your signal this is the right material.

Most postpartum shorts come in 5-7 inch inseams.
