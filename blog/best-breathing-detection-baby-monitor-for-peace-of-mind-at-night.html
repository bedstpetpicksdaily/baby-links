    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do breathing detection monitors actually detect SIDS?

    Breathing detection monitors can alert you if your baby stops breathing or has prolonged pauses, but they cannot prevent SIDS. They work best as a notification tool rather than prevention—the real SIDS reduction comes from safe sleep practices like back sleeping and firm surfaces. Use them as a supplement to, not a replacement for, safe sleep guidelines.

    What's the difference between chest clip monitors and under-mattress pads?

    Chest clip monitors attach directly to your baby's clothing and track breathing and heart rate, while under-mattress pads detect movement and breathing through the crib mattress without anything on the baby. Chest clips are more accurate but can be uncomfortable for some babies, whereas mattress pads are less intrusive but may have more false alarms.

    How accurate are baby breathing monitors and do they have false alarms?

    Most reputable breathing monitors have 95%+ accuracy rates when properly fitted or positioned, though false alarms can occur due to loose clothing, improper sensor placement, or normal sleep pattern variations. Reading reviews specific to your model and adjusting sensitivity settings can help reduce unnecessary alerts.

    Is a breathing monitor worth it if my baby is healthy?

    Healthy babies don't medically need breathing monitors, as the risk of SIDS is low with proper sleep practices. However, many parents find the peace of mind valuable—especially if they have anxiety, a family history of SIDS, or a premature baby who's still developing respiratory stability.
