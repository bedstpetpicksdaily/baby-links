What to Look For

  • Car Seat Compatibility and Easy Installation: Look for a travel system where the car seat clicks securely into the stroller frame with one hand and minimal fuss. The connection should feel solid and intuitive, especially when you're tired or managing multiple children. Verify that the system works with your vehicle type and that the car seat is FAA-approved for air travel if you plan to fly.
  • Lightweight and Compact Design: For travel purposes, weight matters significantly. Choose a stroller that folds compactly for car trunks and airport baggage claims, ideally under 25 pounds for the frame alone. Look for models with one-handed fold mechanisms and a compact folded footprint—this feature alone will save you frustration at the airport or when navigating tight spaces.
  • Versatile Seating and Maneuverability: Your travel system should grow with your baby, offering a bassinet or fully reclined newborn position, then converting to a forward-facing toddler seat. The frame should have responsive steering and smooth-rolling wheels that handle various terrains from airport terminals to sidewalks, without being unnecessarily bulky.
