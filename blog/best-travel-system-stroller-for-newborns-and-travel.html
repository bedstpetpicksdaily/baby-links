If you're planning to travel with a newborn, a quality travel system stroller is one of the most important investments you'll make in your first year of parenthood. A travel system that seamlessly connects your car seat to a stroller frame eliminates the need to wake a sleeping baby and keeps them secure from car to destination. For families who fly, road trip, or frequently move between locations, choosing the right travel system can mean the difference between smooth outings and stressful mornings.

What to Look For

  • Car Seat Compatibility and Easy Installation: Look for a travel system where the car seat clicks securely into the stroller frame with one hand and minimal fuss. The connection should feel solid and intuitive, especially when you're tired or managing multiple children. Verify that the system works with your vehicle type and that the car seat is FAA-approved for air travel if you plan to fly.
  • Lightweight and Compact Design: For travel purposes, weight matters significantly. Choose a stroller that folds compactly for car trunks and airport baggage claims, ideally under 25 pounds for the frame alone. Look for models with one-handed fold mechanisms and a compact folded footprint—this feature alone will save you frustration at the airport or when navigating tight spaces.
  • Versatile Seating and Maneuverability: Your travel system should grow with your baby, offering a bassinet or fully reclined newborn position, then converting to a forward-facing toddler seat. The frame should have responsive steering and smooth-rolling wheels that handle various terrains from airport terminals to sidewalks, without being unnecessarily bulky.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use a travel system stroller from birth or do you need a bassinet?

    Most travel system strollers can be used from birth when paired with their matching infant car seat, which serves as a newborn bed during the first months. Some parents prefer adding a separate bassinet attachment for flat sleeping during longer stroller use, but the car seat alone is sufficient for newborns during typical outings. Always check the manufacturer's guidelines for minimum weight and age requirements specific to your model.

    What is the lightest travel system stroller for newborns?

    Lightweight travel systems typically weigh between 20-30 pounds total, with models like the Doona, GB Pockit+, and Cybex Aton strollers being among the most compact and travel-friendly options. The trade-off is that lighter strollers may have fewer storage features or less recline capability compared to full-size models. Consider your lifestyle and whether you'll frequently lift, fold, or transport the stroller before prioritizing weight alone.

    How long can a newborn stay in a travel system stroller?

    Newborns should not spend more than 1.5-2 hours continuously in a car seat or attached stroller seat due to neck and spine development concerns. Most travel system strollers are designed for short outings rather than extended use, making frequent position changes important for your baby's health. Once your child reaches 6+ months, they can spend longer periods in a stroller seat, though taking breaks is still recommended during long trips.

    Is a travel system stroller worth it for a newborn or should I buy separately?

    A travel system stroller can save money upfront and provides convenience by keeping your baby in the same car seat when moving between car and stroller without waking them. However, if you plan to use the stroller for several years, buying a high-quality stroller separately with a compatible car seat often provides better quality and flexibility. Consider your budget, lifestyle (frequent travelers vs. local use), and whether you want the convenience factor before deciding.
