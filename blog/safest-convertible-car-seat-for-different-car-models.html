What to Look For

  • Vehicle-Specific Installation Compatibility: Check the car seat's manual against your vehicle's owner's manual to confirm the LATCH system will work in your car's rear seats. Some convertible seats have upper tether connectors and lower anchors positioned in ways that work better with certain vehicle architectures—sedans versus SUVs, for example. Verify that the seat won't sit at an unsafe angle or interfere with your vehicle's seat structure before purchasing.
  • Multiple Recline Positions and Angle Indicators: Different cars have different seat angles and headrest heights. Look for convertible seats with multiple recline positions and clear angle indicators so you can achieve the proper installation angle (typically 45 degrees for rear-facing, flatter for forward-facing) regardless of your vehicle's seat design. This flexibility ensures safe positioning across different models you might own or ride in.
  • Compact Design for Your Vehicle's Space: Measure your vehicle's rear seat width and the space between seats before selecting a model. Some convertible seats are wider or deeper and may not fit safely in compact cars, while others work beautifully in everything from sedans to minivans. Prioritize seats with streamlined bases that leave comfortable room for passengers on either side.
