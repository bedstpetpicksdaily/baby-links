Choosing a car seat for your newborn or infant is one of the most critical safety decisions you'll make as a parent. Unlike other baby gear that prioritizes comfort or convenience, a car seat is your child's primary protection during vehicle travel—the leading cause of unintentional injury for infants and young children. Getting this decision right from day one sets the foundation for safer travels throughout your baby's early years.

What to Look For

  • Rear-facing capability with extended weight limits: Rear-facing seats provide the best protection for your infant's developing neck and spine during crashes. Prioritize seats that accommodate rear-facing from newborn weight (typically 4-5 lbs) up to at least 40 lbs, giving you years of use before transitioning forward-facing.
  • Five-point harness system with easy adjustments: Newborns and infants need a secure harness that keeps them snugly in place without gaps. Look for seats with harnesses that adjust smoothly without removing your baby—you'll be doing this frequently as your infant grows, and complicated systems lead to improper installation or fit.
  • Lower anchor system (LATCH) and clear installation guides: Since improper installation is the #1 problem with car seats, choose a model with intuitive LATCH connectors and detailed, easy-to-follow instructions. Some seats include level indicators that help you achieve the correct recline angle—essential for newborns' developing airway.
