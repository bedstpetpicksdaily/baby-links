Is the Clek Foonf actually safer than budget car seats, or just more expensive?

Real-world crash test data from insurance institutes shows premium seats perform better in high-speed and angled impacts. The question is whether that 5-10% additional protection justifies 100% higher cost. Insurance data suggests it matters statistically; your actual risk depends on your driving environment.

How long will a Foonf actually last if I use it for multiple kids?

Realistically, 7-10 years across two children if you're not abusing it. Parent reviews consistently mention using their Foonf from newborn through first grade, then passing it to a second child. The steel frame and quality harness mechanism hold up better than budget plastic seats that often feel loose after three years. This is where the cost-per-use calculates favorably—if you amortize $450 across 10 years and two kids, you're at $225 per child, closer to entry-level pricing.

Should I buy this in July, or wait for back-to-school sales?

July is actually a reasonable time to purchase since parents are often installing summer travel plans and back-to-school shopping hasn't inflated prices yet.
