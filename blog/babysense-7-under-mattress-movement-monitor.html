Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Babysense 7 actually FDA-cleared?

Yes, it's FDA-cleared as a movement monitor, but this is important: FDA clearance doesn't mean it prevents SIDS or guarantees it will catch every dangerous situation. It means the device works as marketed—detecting movement. Many parents misunderstand this distinction and expect more protection than the device actually provides.

How often do parents report false alarms?

After proper calibration in the first week, false alarms drop significantly. However, if your baby is a heavy sleeper or sleeps very still, you might get occasional alerts that turn out to be nothing. The calibration process is crucial—don't skip it even though it feels tedious.

Can you use this with a pack-and-play or portable bassinet?

Yes, most portable options work fine since the sensor is just a thin pad. However, the response is slower on softer surfaces compared to firm crib mattresses. If you're moving between multiple sleep locations frequently, this might not be your best option since you'd need to recalibrate each time.

What's the real difference between Babysense 7 and older models?

The Babysense 7 has improved sensitivity algorithms and a faster detection response time compared to the Babysense 5. In practical terms, fewer false negatives and clearer alert distinctions. If you're choosing used, the jump from older versions is worth considering, but the jump from model 5 to 7 is incremental unless you're particularly sensitive to false alarms.
