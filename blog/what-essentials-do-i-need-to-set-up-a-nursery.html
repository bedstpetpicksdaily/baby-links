What Essentials Do I Need to Set Up a Nursery?

A basic nursery requires a safe crib or bassinet, a comfortable mattress with fitted sheets, a dresser for storage, and adequate lighting for nighttime care. You'll also need climate control, blackout curtains, a changing station, and safety fixtures like outlet covers and a baby monitor.

The Short Answer

Setting up a nursery means creating a safe, functional sleeping and care space for your baby. At minimum, you need a crib or bassinet, bedding, storage furniture, a changing area, and a baby monitor. Beyond these basics, consider adding a comfortable chair for late-night feeding sessions and blackout curtains to help regulate your baby's sleep schedule. Proper lighting, temperature control, and safety equipment complete a well-equipped nursery.

The Full Explanation

Sleep Furniture
Your first priority is selecting a safe sleeping surface. A full-size crib meets current safety standards and lasts through toddlerhood, while a bassinet works well for the first 4-6 months. Choose one that's firm, well-constructed, and free from drop-side mechanisms. Ensure it meets CPSC standards and comes with a quality mattress that fits snugly without gaps.

Bedding and Mattress Essentials
Invest in a waterproof mattress protector and at least 3-4 fitted sheets for frequent washing. Skip pillows, blankets, and bumpers—these pose suffocation and SIDS risks. Instead, use a fitted sheet and a sleep sack to keep your baby warm without loose fabric. Quality bedding prevents stains and extends mattress life.

Storage and Organization
A dresser or changing table with built-in storage keeps clothes, diapers, wipes, and supplies within arm's reach during nighttime changes. Look for sturdy furniture that won't tip over. Open shelving or wall-mounted baskets offer accessible storage without taking up floor space. Good organization saves precious time during middle-of-the-night diaper changes.

Changing Station Setup
Designate a dedicated changing area with a padded surface at waist height. Whether you use a changing table, dresser top, or wall-mounted changing station, ensure it's stable and has storage for diapers, wipes, creams, and clothing. Keep essentials within reach to prevent leaving your baby unattended.

Baby Monitor
A reliable baby monitor is essential for peace of mind. Video monitors let you see your baby, while audio-only models are budget-friendly. Look for night vision, temperature monitoring, and range suitable for your home. Monitors with two-way talk features allow you to soothe your baby from another room.

Lighting and Window Treatments
Install dimmable overhead lights and a soft nightlight for visibility during nighttime care. Blackout curtains regulate sleep schedules and block early morning light. Consider warm-toned lighting that won't overstimulate your baby during night feedings. Proper lighting also helps you see clearly during diaper changes and health checks.

Climate Control and Safety
Maintain a room temperature between 68-72°F with a humidifier or dehumidifier as needed. Install a room thermometer to monitor temperature. Secure furniture to walls with anti-tip brackets, cover electrical outlets, and remove cords from reach. Keep windows locked and install safety gates if applicable.

Seating for Comfort
A comfortable chair or glider is invaluable for feeding, rocking, and soothing your baby during long nights.

Frequently Asked Questions

What furniture do I need for a nursery?

The essential furniture includes a crib or bassinet, a dresser (which can double as a changing table), and a comfortable chair or glider for nighttime feedings and bonding. You may also want a closet organizer or wardrobe rack to maximize space and keep baby clothes accessible.

How do I keep a newborn's room at the right temperature?

The ideal nursery temperature is between 68-72°F (20-22°C) to reduce the risk of SIDS. Use a reliable room thermometer to monitor temperature, and dress your baby appropriately in sleep sacks or swaddles rather than relying on blankets or pillows.

What bedding and linens do I need for a crib?

You'll need fitted crib sheets (at least 2-3), waterproof mattress protectors, and a firm mattress that fits snugly in the crib—skip pillows, bumpers, and blankets as they pose SIDS risks. A swaddle or sleep sack is a safe alternative for keeping your baby warm.

What lighting should I use in a nursery?

Install blackout curtains or shades to promote better sleep and make nighttime easier to manage, then add a dimmable overhead light and a soft nightlight for feedings and diaper changes. Choose warm, low-intensity lighting to avoid stimulating your baby during nighttime care.
