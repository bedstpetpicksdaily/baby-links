Travel Strollers for Newborns: Doona vs. UPPAbaby Minu and What to Actually Know

By Best Baby Picks Daily • July 20, 2026 • Contains affiliate links

Traveling with a newborn demands a stroller that handles three competing demands: keeping your baby safe and comfortable, fitting into tight spaces (car trunks, overhead bins, hotel hallways), and not becoming a logistical nightmare when you're already juggling a car seat, diaper bag, and exhaustion.

Not all travel strollers are created equal, and the best choice depends on your specific travel patterns and priorities. The two most compelling options for newborn travel—the Doona Infant Car Seat and the UPPAbaby Minu—take fundamentally different approaches. Understanding the real trade-offs between them, rather than just picking whichever one has the flashier marketing, will save you money and frustration.

Here's what actually matters when choosing a travel stroller for a baby under 6 months, and how these two leading options stack up.

Doona Infant Car Seat & Latch Base

The Doona is a 2-in-1 system: your infant car seat transforms into a stroller with the push of a button and a few clicks. You move your sleeping baby from car to stroller without transferring them, which is genuinely useful during naps. There's no separate stroller to buy, pack, or coordinate with your car seat—one piece of gear does both jobs.

This eliminates a major pain point: the logistics of managing two separate items. For families who prioritize simplicity and the ability to keep sleeping babies asleep during transitions, the integration is real and valuable.

Best for: Parents who want one integrated system, prioritize keeping babies asleep during transfers, and are willing to pay premium pricing for that convenience.

UPPAbaby Minu

The UPPAbaby Minu is a dedicated lightweight travel stroller weighing just 10.2 pounds and folding to 17 x 13 x 10 inches—compact enough for most airplane overhead bins. The seat reclines completely flat (essential for newborns under 6 months), features a deep protective canopy, and includes reliable suspension for handling uneven terrain without jostling a sleeping infant.

This is a traditional stroller that connects to most major car seat brands via standard adapters, rather than replacing your car seat. It's designed for parents who want a stroller that excels at its single job: being lightweight, compact, and smooth-riding during travel.

Best for: Parents who need a dedicated lightweight stroller that fits overhead bins, appreciate flexibility with car seat choice, and want smooth suspension over uneven surfaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum weight for a travel stroller when you're pushing it one-handed while holding a baby?

Aim for under 15 pounds. The UPPAbaby Minu at 10.2 pounds is at the lighter end; the Doona, as a full car seat, will be heavier. If you're constantly managing a baby in one arm, every pound matters through an airport.

Can you use a travel stroller from birth, or do you need a bassinet attachment?

Travel strollers designed for newborns (like both of these) accommodate newborns from birth if they have a full 180-degree flat recline. You do not need a separate bassinet if the seat reclines completely flat. Both the Doona and UPPAbaby Minu meet this requirement.

Will a travel stroller actually fit in an airplane overhead bin?

The UPPAbaby Minu is specifically designed to fit (17 x 13 x 10 inches when folded). The Doona, being a full car seat, is bulkier and less likely to fit overhead; you'll typically gate-check it. If overhead bin space is critical for your trip, the Minu is the safer choice.

Are umbrella strollers safe for newborns?

Traditional umbrella strollers are not recommended for newborns because they don't fully recline and don't provide the flat, supportive surface newborns need for proper spine development and breathing. Both the Doona and UPPAbaby Minu are more substantial than umbrella strollers and designed specifically for newborn safety.

Which option is better if I'm traveling internationally?

The UPPAbaby Minu edges ahead for international travel due to its compact fold and extreme lightness, making it easier to navigate crowded airports and fit in varied car trunk sizes. The Doona's integration is less valuable on longer trips where you'll use the stroller more independently of the car.
