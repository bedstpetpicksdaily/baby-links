What to Look For

  • All-Terrain Wheels with Deep Tread: Look for large, rugged wheels (minimum 7+ inches) with aggressive tread patterns designed for grip on wet pavement, ice, and snow. All-terrain or pneumatic wheels perform significantly better than standard plastic wheels on slippery surfaces. Wheels with ball-bearing swivels also provide better maneuverability when conditions are challenging.
  • Water-Resistant and Quick-Dry Fabrics: The fabric, seat, and canopy should be treated with water-repellent coatings to shed rain and prevent mildew. Look for materials that dry quickly and don't absorb moisture, which can make the stroller heavy and uncomfortable. Sealed zippers and reinforced seams prevent water from seeping into areas where your baby sits.
  • Sturdy Frame with Locking Brakes: A heavier, more rigid frame provides stability on uneven, slippery terrain rather than lightweight frames that tip easily. Locking brakes are essential for keeping the stroller stationary on slopes and icy surfaces. Test the brake system to ensure it holds firmly even on inclines.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What makes a stroller waterproof and good for rain?

    Look for strollers with a water-resistant or fully waterproof fabric canopy, sealed seams, and a weather shield that extends to cover the entire seat area. The best rain strollers have drainage holes in the basket and seat to prevent water pooling, plus quick-drying materials that won't retain moisture or develop mold.

    Are all terrain stroller wheels better for snow?

    Yes, all-terrain strollers with large, sturdy wheels (typically 12+ inches) and good tread patterns provide better traction and flotation in snow compared to standard wheels. Look for strollers with air-filled or foam-filled tires rather than plastic wheels, as they absorb shock and maintain grip on slippery surfaces.

    Can you use a regular stroller in winter weather?

    Regular strollers can be used in mild winter conditions with additional protection like a weather shield and waterproof cover, but they struggle with deep snow and icy terrain due to small wheels and limited traction. For frequent snow use, an all-terrain or off-road stroller is necessary for safety and ease of pushing.

    What stroller features prevent water and snow from getting on the baby?

    A full-coverage rain canopy, weather shield, and apron-style footmuff are essential for keeping your baby dry and warm. The best options have a peek-a-boo window in the canopy for visibility, extendable hoods that fully enclose the seat, and wind-resistant design to prevent water splashing underneath.
