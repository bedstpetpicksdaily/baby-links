What to Look For

  • All-Terrain Wheels with Deep Tread: Look for large, rugged wheels (minimum 7+ inches) with aggressive tread patterns designed for grip on wet pavement, ice, and snow. All-terrain or pneumatic wheels perform significantly better than standard plastic wheels on slippery surfaces. Wheels with ball-bearing swivels also provide better maneuverability when conditions are challenging.
  • Water-Resistant and Quick-Dry Fabrics: The fabric, seat, and canopy should be treated with water-repellent coatings to shed rain and prevent mildew. Look for materials that dry quickly and don't absorb moisture, which can make the stroller heavy and uncomfortable. Sealed zippers and reinforced seams prevent water from seeping into areas where your baby sits.
  • Sturdy Frame with Locking Brakes: A heavier, more rigid frame provides stability on uneven, slippery terrain rather than lightweight frames that tip easily. Locking brakes are essential for keeping the stroller stationary on slopes and icy surfaces. Test the brake system to ensure it holds firmly even on inclines.
