    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do video baby monitors work without WiFi?

    Many video baby monitors offer closed-network viewing, which means they work without internet by connecting directly to a parent unit or smartphone via a local connection. However, WiFi-enabled monitors give you the advantage of checking in from anywhere outside your home, which most parents prefer for peace of mind. Check the monitor's specs to see if it offers both options.

    What video baby monitor features help you sleep better?

    Look for monitors with infrared night vision so you can see clearly without lights, two-way talk-back to soothe your baby remotely, and temperature alerts that notify you if the room gets too hot or cold. Sound and motion detection with smart alerts also help—they'll only wake you if there's actually a problem, not every little noise your baby makes.

    Is it safe to leave a video baby monitor on all night?

    Yes, video baby monitors are designed to run continuously throughout the night safely. Just ensure the monitor has proper ventilation, is placed out of your baby's reach, and isn't positioned directly over the crib as a safety precaution. Most modern monitors use low-power consumption and won't overheat with extended use.

    How far away can you view a baby monitor?

    WiFi-enabled monitors can work from anywhere with an internet connection—across your house, at work, or even while traveling. Closed-network monitors typically have a range of 700-1000 feet in open space, though walls and interference can reduce this distance. If you plan to check on your baby from outside the house regularly, choose a WiFi-enabled model.
