What to Look For

  • Removable, Dishwasher-Safe Trays and Seat Covers: Look for high chairs where the tray, seat cushion, and harness covers pop off easily and can go straight into the dishwasher. This eliminates the need for hand-scrubbing dried food and saves you 10-15 minutes per day. Avoid models with fabric that's permanently attached or has lots of crevices where food gets trapped.
  • Non-Porous Materials and Smooth Surfaces: Prioritize high chairs with vinyl or silicone seat covers, plastic trays, and metal frames rather than wood or fabric. These materials don't absorb liquids, prevent mold and bacteria growth, and can be wiped clean in seconds with a damp cloth. Stainless steel or powder-coated metal bases are also easier to maintain than painted wood.
  • Compact Footprint Without Sacrificing Stability: Choose models that fold partially or have a narrow base to fit in tight spaces, but ensure they still have a wide, weighted frame. The high chair should feel sturdy when your toddler inevitably bounces or shifts around, even with a smaller overall size.
