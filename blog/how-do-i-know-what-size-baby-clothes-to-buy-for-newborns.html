How Do I Know What Size Baby Clothes to Buy for Newborns?

The most reliable way to determine newborn clothing sizes is to buy primarily newborn (NB) and 0-3 month sizes, as most babies wear these for their first 3-6 months. However, since babies grow at different rates and some are born larger than average, it's smart to have a mix of sizes on hand rather than committing entirely to one size.

The Short Answer

Most newborns wear newborn (NB) size clothing for the first 2-4 weeks, then transition to 0-3 month sizes for the remainder of their first three months. Rather than buying all one size, stock your nursery with a combination of NB and 0-3 month clothing to accommodate your baby's growth and avoid waste. Keep in mind that sizing varies by brand—some run smaller or larger—so having flexibility in your closet is essential. Don't buy too much of any single size until you see how your baby grows and how specific brands fit.

The Full Explanation

Understanding newborn clothing sizes requires knowing that there's no universal standard across manufacturers. Here's what you need to know:

Standard Newborn Size Categories

Newborn (NB) size is typically designed for babies weighing 5-8 pounds, which is the average newborn weight range. This size usually fits babies from birth through 2-4 weeks old. However, if you have a larger baby (8+ pounds), newborn sizes may be too snug, and 0-3 month clothing might be better from day one.

0-3 Month size is designed for babies weighing 8-12 pounds and is the most versatile size for the first three months. Many babies skip newborn sizes entirely if they're born on the heavier side, or transition quickly from NB to 0-3 month clothing.

3-6 Month size is generally too large for a typical newborn but can be useful if you have a particularly large baby or want clothing that will last longer into the first year.

Why You Can't Predict Exactly

You won't know your baby's exact size until they're born and you can see their weight and length. Even then, babies grow rapidly and unpredictably. Some babies gain a pound per week, while others grow more slowly. Additionally, brand sizing varies significantly—what fits perfectly in one brand's 0-3 month size might be too large or small in another brand's version of the same size.

The Best Strategy for Purchasing

Rather than buying a complete wardrobe in one size, purchase a smaller quantity of multiple sizes. A practical approach is to buy about 4-5 newborn-sized onesies and 6-8 in 0-3 month sizes. This ensures you have clean clothes available while accommodating growth and brand variations. You can always purchase more once you see how your baby grows and which brands fit best.

Factors That Affect Sizing

Birth weight: Babies born weighing 9+ pounds will likely skip newborn sizes entirely. Babies born prematurely or with lower birth weight may stay in newborn sizes longer.

Growth rate: All babies grow at different rates. Some infants gain weight rapidly while others grow more slowly, affecting how long they wear each size.

Brand differences: High-end brands often run smaller and more tailored, while budget brands tend to run loose and larger. Carter's and OshKosh, for example, size differently from luxury brands.
