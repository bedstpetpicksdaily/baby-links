Tommee Tippee Closer to Nature: Pacifiers vs. Bottle Warmer—Which Should You Buy?

By Best Baby Picks Daily • July 20, 2026 • Contains affiliate links

New parents often find themselves buying multiple products from the same trusted brand, assuming that quality in one category guarantees quality across the board. Tommee Tippee's Closer to Nature line spans feeding essentials—but two of their most popular products serve completely different purposes and solve different problems.

The Closer to Nature Pacifiers target oral development and natural sucking patterns, while the Closer to Nature Bottle Warmer tackles the 3 AM feeding-time crisis. Both carry the brand name, both have solid customer ratings, but they compete for budget space and shelf real estate in your nursery. This guide compares what each product actually delivers, so you can decide which one (or both) deserves a spot in your baby gear rotation.

Tommee Tippee Closer to Nature Pacifiers

Orthodontically designed pacifiers available in multiple stages (newborn 0-6 months, 6-18 months, and 18+ months) that mimic natural breastfeeding and support healthy oral development. Made from soft, flexible silicone that's gentle on baby's gums and mouth, these are dishwasher-safe and designed to work for both bottle-fed and breastfed babies.

Best for: Parents prioritizing oral health development, mixed-feeding families (breast and bottle), and those willing to invest in stage-appropriate sizing as babies grow.

Tommee Tippee Closer to Nature Bottle Warmer

A water-based bottle warmer that heats bottles to feeding temperature in 4-8 minutes (depending on starting water temperature), using gentle indirect heat rather than direct exposure. Operates with simple one-button control and automatic shut-off, accommodates multiple bottle sizes and shapes including anti-colic bottles, and typically costs $30-50.

Best for: Parents doing frequent bottle feeding who value time savings during night wakings, those using varied bottle types, and households where convenience justifies the cost over manual warm-water methods.

Manual Water-Bath Method (Budget Alternative)

Running bottles under warm water or placing them in a bowl of hot water—the method the Bottle Warmer is compared against. No purchase required, works with any bottle, but requires active attention and inconsistent heating times, particularly during exhausted 2-3 AM feeding sessions.

Best for: Minimalist parents, occasional bottle users, or families on a tight budget who don't mind adding 10-15 minutes to the feeding routine.

Our Verdict

These are not competing products—they solve different problems. Buy the Pacifiers if you're intentional about oral development and plan to nurse, bottle-feed, or mix both; they justify their higher price through stage-appropriate design and durability across your baby's first 18+ months. The investment makes sense if you have multiple children or plan to resell them.

Buy the Bottle Warmer if you're doing frequent bottle feeding (whether formula or expressed breast milk) and value the 4-8 minute time savings enough to justify $30-50 as a convenience purchase rather than a necessity. At 3 AM with a hungry baby, those minutes matter. Skip it if you're bottle-feeding occasionally or prefer the simplicity and cost of running warm water.

For budget-conscious families, the pacifiers deliver better long-term value per month of use. The bottle warmer is optional convenience spending. Buying both makes sense only if you're actively bottle-feeding multiple times daily and committed to the orthodontic pacifier investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Tommee Tippee Closer to Nature Pacifiers if I'm exclusively bottle-feeding?

Yes, they're designed to work for bottle-fed and breastfed babies alike. The orthodontic shape supports natural sucking patterns regardless of feeding method, though some exclusively bottle-fed babies may reject them in favor of simpler designs.

How much time does the Bottle Warmer actually save compared to running warm water?

The Warmer heats bottles in 4-8 minutes depending on starting water temperature, which typically saves 5-10 minutes over manual water-bath methods. For 2-3 AM feedings when you're exhausted, that time difference feels significant.

Are the pacifiers safe for newborns from birth?

Yes, the newborn stage (0-6 months) pacifiers are specifically designed for newborns and meet safety standards. The orthodontic design supports natural sucking patterns from birth, though some pediatricians recommend waiting a few weeks if breastfeeding to establish latch.

Why do heating times on the Bottle Warmer vary so much between reviews?

Water mineral content, initial water temperature, and bottle material all affect heating speed more than the marketing acknowledges. Hard water may slow heating, and cold-start bottles take longer than room-temperature ones. The warmer lacks a temperature display, so you're relying on its timer, which isn't always precise.

Should I buy both products together?

Only if you're actively bottle-feeding multiple times daily and also want orthodontic pacifiers for oral development. If you're occasionally bottle-feeding or exclusively breastfeeding, prioritize the pacifiers. If you're exclusively bottle-feeding on a budget, skip both the warmer and premium pacifiers in favor of simpler, cheaper alternatives.
