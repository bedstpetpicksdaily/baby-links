    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the safest infant car seat for newborns?

    Rear-facing infant car seats are considered the safest option for newborns because they provide better support for the head, neck, and spine during crashes. Top-rated models include the Nuna Pipa Rx, Graco SnugRide SnugLock, and Cybex Cloud Q, all of which have excellent safety ratings and features like side-impact protection. The safest seat is one that's properly installed, fits your vehicle, and matches your lifestyle needs.

    How long should a baby stay in an infant car seat?

    Most infant car seats accommodate newborns from birth up to 30-35 pounds or until the baby reaches the maximum height limit, typically around 12-18 months depending on the model. Once your child exceeds the weight or height limits, you'll need to transition to a convertible or forward-facing car seat. Always consult your specific car seat's manual for exact specifications.

    Does an expensive infant car seat mean it's safer?

    Not necessarily—all car seats sold in the US must meet the same federal safety standards (FMVSS 213), so a $150 seat provides the same baseline protection as a $400 seat. The price difference typically reflects extra features like easier installation, premium fabrics, or portability rather than crash safety. What matters most is proper installation and keeping the seat rear-facing as long as possible.

    What should I look for when choosing an infant car seat?

    Look for seats with good crash test ratings from NHTSA, easy-to-use installation systems (like LATCH or base compatibility), side-impact protection, and compatibility with your vehicle. Consider practical factors like weight for portability, fabric washability, and whether you need a matching stroller. Read parent reviews on safety features and customer service, as these often reveal real-world usability issues.
