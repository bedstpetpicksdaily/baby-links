Baby Monitor Buying Guide: 3 Key Features to Consider

  • Video Quality & Night Vision: Look for HD resolution (720p or higher) and infrared night vision to see your baby clearly in any lighting condition. This is especially important if you plan to monitor from another room during nighttime.
  • Connectivity Options: Choose between closed digital (no WiFi required for privacy) or WiFi-enabled (remote viewing from anywhere). Consider your comfort level with internet-connected devices and your home WiFi security.
  • Additional Features: Evaluate two-way talk-back, temperature monitoring, lullabies, and motion/sound alerts based on your specific needs. Some monitors offer advanced features like breathing detection or sleep tracking—prioritize what matters most to your family.

Frequently Asked Questions About Baby Monitors

Are WiFi baby monitors safe for privacy?

WiFi baby monitors can be safe when you choose reputable brands with strong security standards and enable two-factor authentication on the app. However, if privacy is your top concern, closed-digital monitors like the Infant Optics DXR-8 that don't connect to the internet eliminate WiFi hacking risks entirely.

What's the difference between audio-only and video monitors?

Audio-only monitors are lightweight and affordable, ideal for monitoring sleeping babies in adjacent rooms. Video monitors provide visual confirmation of your baby's position, safety, and condition, making them worth the extra investment for parents who want maximum reassurance and the ability to see their baby instantly.
