  • Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use a split screen baby monitor with two different rooms?

    Yes, most split screen baby monitors allow you to view two separate rooms simultaneously on one screen, making them ideal for monitoring multiple children or a nursery plus a playroom. You can typically switch between rooms individually or keep both feeds displayed at once, depending on the monitor's capabilities.

    Do split screen baby monitors work without WiFi?

    Most top-rated split screen monitors offer closed WiFi systems that work without internet, using a private connection between the parent unit and cameras for better security and reliability. However, some models also offer WiFi connectivity as an option if you want to monitor remotely from your smartphone when away from home.

    What is the maximum range for a split screen baby monitor?

    Closed-system split screen monitors typically have a range of 700-900 feet in open space, though walls and interference can reduce this distance. WiFi-enabled models have unlimited range as long as you have internet access, allowing you to check on your baby from anywhere.

    Are split screen baby monitors safe and secure?

    Closed-system monitors (non-WiFi) are generally more secure since they don't connect to the internet and can't be hacked remotely. If you choose a WiFi-enabled model, look for features like encrypted connections, two-factor authentication, and secure password protection to keep your feed private.
