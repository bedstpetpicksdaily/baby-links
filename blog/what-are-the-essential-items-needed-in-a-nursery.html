A nursery requires a safe sleep space (crib or bassinet), storage for clothes and supplies, changing facilities, lighting, temperature control, and a baby monitor to keep watch from other rooms. These essentials create a functional, comfortable environment where your baby can sleep safely and you can easily access everything needed for daily care.

The Short Answer

Every nursery needs five core categories: a safe sleep surface, storage solutions, changing and diaper facilities, appropriate lighting and climate control, and a monitoring system. Beyond these basics, comfort items like blackout curtains, a sound machine, and soft furnishings help create a soothing environment. The specific items depend on your space, budget, and parenting style, but these fundamentals apply to virtually all nurseries.

The Full Explanation

Safe Sleep Space

Your most important purchase is a safe place for your baby to sleep. A full-size crib meets safety standards and lasts through toddlerhood, while a bassinet keeps your newborn closer for nighttime feedings. Some parents use both—a bassinet in the bedroom for the first 6 months, then transitioning to a crib in the nursery. Whichever you choose, ensure it has a firm mattress, fitted sheets, and nothing else inside (no pillows, blankets, or bumpers, which increase SIDS risk). A quality crib mattress designed for proper support is essential; many parents invest in a waterproof mattress protector to guard against accidents.

Storage and Organization

Babies require an enormous amount of stuff: clothing in multiple sizes, blankets, swaddles, sleep sacks, and seasonal items. A dresser with drawers keeps clothes organized and accessible, while wall shelving or a closet system manages other supplies. Consider vertical storage to maximize space in smaller rooms. Many parents find that open shelving works well for frequently used items like diapers, wipes, and diaper cream, while closed storage keeps dust off clothes.

Changing Station

You'll change thousands of diapers, so a dedicated changing area saves your back and keeps supplies within arm's reach. A changing table with storage underneath is ideal, but a dresser with a changing pad on top works equally well and provides dual functionality. The changing surface should be at a comfortable height (typically 30-36 inches), have safety rails, and be positioned away from any hazards. Stock your changing station with diapers in multiple sizes, wipes, diaper cream, a small trash can with a lid, and a few toys or mobile to entertain your baby during changes.

Lighting

Proper lighting supports your baby's circadian rhythm and makes nighttime care easier. Install dimmable overhead lighting for daytime play and cleaning. Add soft, warm lighting for nighttime changes and feedings—many parents use a small lamp with a 40-watt bulb or a dedicated nursery night light. Blackout curtains or shades help establish healthy sleep patterns by blocking external light, especially important during summer months or early morning hours. Some families use color-changing smart lights to create different moods or help signal bedtime.

Temperature and Air Quality

Babies sleep best in a cool room—ideally between 68-72°F (20-22°C). Use a reliable room thermometer to monitor temperature, and a portable fan or space heater to adjust as needed. A white noise machine masks household sounds and creates a consistent audio environment that supports sleep. Many parents combine this with a humidifier during winter months to prevent dry air from irritating your baby's skin and airways.
