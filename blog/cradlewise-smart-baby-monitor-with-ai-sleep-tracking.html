Frequently Asked Questions

How does Cradlewise's AI sleep tracking compare to competitor monitors like Nanit or Mella?

Cradlewise's AI focuses specifically on sleep *stages* (light vs. deep sleep), which is useful for understanding wake patterns. Nanit emphasizes overall sleep metrics and trend analysis. Mella is simpler and cheaper ($150-200 range). If detailed stage-by-stage breakdown matters to you, Cradlewise wins. If you just want 'is baby sleeping' data, Nanit or a basic monitor works fine.

Will this monitor actually help if my baby has sleep regressions?

Partially. The AI can confirm whether your baby is waking from light sleep (normal, often self-soothing possible) or deep sleep (usually something's genuinely wrong—hunger, discomfort). This distinction stops you from rushing in at every peep. However, it won't prevent regressions or create better sleep habits—that's on you and time. Treat it as a diagnostic tool, not a solution.

Is the two-way audio actually useful, or is it a gimmick?

It's useful in limited situations. Soothing your baby's fussing from another room works occasionally. But most parents find it most valuable for communicating with caregivers or hearing when your baby is genuinely awake vs. doing normal sleep shuffling. Not a make-or-break feature, but handy when it works.

Does the monitor work reliably with older wifi routers, or do I need to upgrade my setup?

Cradlewise needs solid 2.4GHz wifi (5GHz can cause dropouts). If your router is more than 5 years old or you have wifi dead zones, you might experience connection issues. Budget an extra $50-80 for a mesh wifi system if your home has poor coverage. Worth doing anyway for overall stability.
