Frequently Asked Questions

Does the AI breathing detection work if your baby sleeps on their side?

Mostly, but with caveats. The AI needs a clear view of the chest to track breathing patterns. Side-sleeping can reduce accuracy since you're seeing the baby at an angle. Cradlewise recommends back-sleeping position for optimal detection, which aligns with SIDS safety guidelines anyway. If your baby is an active roller, you'll get occasional false alerts when they shift positions and the camera loses the chest view.

Is the AI breathing detection medically approved or just marketing?

It's legitimate technology, but not FDA-cleared as a medical device. Cradlewise uses computer vision to track chest wall movement, which is the same method hospitals use—but hospital monitors have more sophisticated sensors. This monitor is a safety tool, not a replacement for medical-grade breathing monitors. Parents with preemies or diagnosed breathing conditions should consult their pediatrician before relying on it as a primary monitor.

How often do false alerts happen in real use?

Based on the 500+ reviews and user reports, false alerts happen rarely once the AI is properly trained (under 2-3 per week for most users). Most occur during position changes or when blankets shift. The real frustration isn't the frequency—it's that you can't easily distinguish false alerts from real ones without checking the video feed, which defeats the peace-of-mind purpose. Some parents disable alerts after a few days of false positives, essentially downgrading to a regular video monitor.
