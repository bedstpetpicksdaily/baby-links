    What is the best compact stroller for newborns?

    The best compact strollers for newborns offer a fully reclined seat, proper head and neck support, and a bassinet or carrycot option. Look for models like the GB Pockit+ or Cybex Libelle that provide newborn-safe features while maintaining a lightweight, foldable design for easy portability.

    Can you use a compact stroller from birth?

    Most compact strollers cannot be used from birth because they don't fully recline or lack proper newborn support. However, select models designed for newborns (like the Uppababy Minu or Nuna Pepp Next) offer full recline and flat sleeping surfaces suitable from day one.

    How much weight can a compact stroller hold?

    Most compact strollers support children from birth up to 40-50 pounds, accommodating both newborns and toddlers through age 3-4. Always check the manufacturer's specifications, as weight limits vary significantly between brands and models.

    Are compact strollers safe for newborns?

    Compact strollers are safe for newborns only if they offer full recline, proper head support, and meet JPMA safety standards. Avoid ultra-lightweight models without adequate padding or recline for newborns; instead, choose strollers specifically tested and certified for newborn use from birth.
