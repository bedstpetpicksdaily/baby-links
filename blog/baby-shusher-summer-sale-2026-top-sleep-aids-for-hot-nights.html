Why Babies Struggle to Sleep in Summer Heat

Newborns and infants have difficulty regulating their body temperature, making them especially sensitive to warm weather. When rooms become too hot, babies experience restless sleep, frequent night wakings, and increased fussiness. Beyond temperature control, environmental noise—from air conditioning units to neighborhood sounds—can further disrupt sleep cycles. This is where white noise machines and soothing devices become invaluable. They mask disruptive sounds while creating a consistent, calming auditory environment that helps babies drift off faster and sleep deeper.
