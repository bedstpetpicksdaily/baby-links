Choosing the right stroller for your newborn is one of the most important decisions you'll make as a new parent. Your newborn needs a stroller that provides proper support, safety, and comfort during those crucial first months. The right choice now will set you up for success through your baby's first year and beyond.

What to Look For

  • Full Recline Capability: Newborns cannot sit upright and need a stroller that reclines completely flat or nearly flat. This supports their developing spine and keeps their airway open, making it safer for extended naps and outings. Look for strollers with multiple recline positions that go all the way back.
  • Sturdy Frame and Suspension: A newborn's delicate body needs excellent shock absorption and stability. Choose a stroller with a robust frame, quality suspension system, and smooth wheels that can handle various terrains without jolting your baby. This matters even more since newborns sleep frequently in their strollers.
  • Deep Bassinet or Carrycot Design: Newborns benefit from a bassinet-style seating area that keeps them cocooned and secure. This enclosed design protects them from wind, weather, and overstimulation while providing a safe sleeping space. It's especially helpful for newborns who spend significant time sleeping during outings.

What to Avoid

  • Lightweight Umbrella Strollers: While these are portable and affordable, they lack the support and recline capabilities newborns require. Most umbrella strollers don't recline fully flat and have minimal suspension, making them uncomfortable and potentially unsafe for newborns under 3-4 months old. Save these for when your baby is older and more mobile.
  • Car Seat Frame Only: Though convenient, using just a car seat on a basic frame without a proper stroller isn't ideal as your primary solution. Car seats aren't designed for extended use outside the vehicle, don't provide adequate ventilation for long periods, and lack the comfort features your newborn needs for naps at home. Use car seat frames as a complement to a full stroller, not a replacement.
  • Jogging or All-Terrain Strollers: These are designed for active parents

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What features should a newborn stroller have?

    A newborn stroller should have a fully reclinable seat (at least 170 degrees) to support a flat sleeping position, since newborns can't sit upright. Look for good suspension to smooth out bumps, a spacious bassinet or carrycot, and sturdy brakes for safety on inclines.

    Can you use a regular stroller for a newborn?

    Most regular strollers are designed for babies 6 months and older, so they're not ideal for newborns who need a completely flat surface. You'll need a stroller with a bassinet, carrycot, or seat that reclines fully flat to safely accommodate a newborn.

    How much should I spend on a newborn stroller?

    Quality newborn strollers typically range from $300-$800, with budget-friendly options starting around $200 and premium brands costing $1,000+. Investing in a stroller you'll use for 3-4 years often makes mid-range options ($400-600) the best value.

    What's the difference between a bassinet stroller and a travel system?

    A bassinet stroller is a standalone unit with a flat carrycot attachment ideal for newborns, while a travel system combines a stroller frame with a detachable infant car seat. Travel systems offer convenience for transitioning between car and stroller, but bassinet strollers typically provide better support for a newborn's flat sleep position.
