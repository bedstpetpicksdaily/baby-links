Best Baby Stroller for Twins

Choosing a stroller for twins is one of the most important investments you'll make as a parent of multiples. Unlike single-baby strollers, a twin stroller must balance functionality, maneuverability, and comfort for two growing babies while remaining practical for everyday use. Getting this decision right means smoother outings, better organization, and genuine relief during those busy days when you're managing twice the gear.

What to Look For

  • Side-by-Side vs. Tandem Configuration: Side-by-side strollers offer equal visibility and interaction between twins but are wider and harder to navigate through doorways and aisles. Tandem (front-to-back) strollers are narrower and more maneuverable but the rear child has a limited view. Consider your lifestyle—if you frequent narrow stores or use public transit frequently, tandem might be better; if you want both children equally engaged, side-by-side wins.
  • Weight Capacity and Durability: Twin strollers must support two children safely, so look for models rated for at least 50 pounds total capacity with sturdy frames that won't wobble or tip. The wheels, brakes, and folding mechanism need to withstand double the wear and tear of a single stroller, so investing in quality construction now prevents headaches (and safety issues) later.
  • Ease of Folding and Storage: With two babies, your hands are full. Look for strollers that fold compactly with one hand or require minimal effort—you'll be doing this repeatedly while managing two toddlers. Check the folded dimensions against your car trunk space before purchasing, as many twin strollers are considerably larger than single models.
