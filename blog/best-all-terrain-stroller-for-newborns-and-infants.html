What to Look For

  • Superior suspension and shock absorption: Newborns and infants have delicate spinal columns that are still developing, so prioritize strollers with advanced suspension systems that absorb bumps from gravel, trails, and cobblestones. This protects your baby's neck and back while allowing smoother rides on rough terrain.
  • Fully reclinable seat for newborn safety: Infants cannot sit upright, so your stroller must have a seat that reclines completely flat or nearly flat to properly support their head and neck. This is non-negotiable for babies under 6 months old and ensures they're not at risk of positional asphyxia on bumpy terrain.
  • Durable, large wheels with locking capabilities: Look for air-filled or foam-filled wheels that are at least 12 inches in diameter and a sturdy frame that can handle roots, rocks, and sand without tipping. A reliable hand brake and wheel locks are essential safety features when navigating steep or challenging terrain.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can you use an all terrain stroller for a newborn?

    Yes, but you need to choose carefully—your newborn needs a stroller with a fully reclinable seat that lies completely flat, which is crucial for proper spinal support in the first months. Look for models that offer a bassinet attachment or reversible seat that allows your infant to lie flat, as most all terrain strollers come with seats that recline but don't fully flatten.

    What is the best all terrain stroller for newborns under $500?

    The Baby Trend Expedition Go is a solid budget option around $400-500 that offers a full recline for newborns and decent off-road capability with large wheels. The Graco Premier Modes Nest also falls in this price range and provides a bassinet option that's better for newborns than traditional seats.

    How do I know if an all terrain stroller is safe for my newborn?

    Check that the stroller has a full flat recline (0-180 degrees), strong suspension to absorb bumps, and a five-point harness for safety. Verify it meets ASTM F833 safety standards and has positive reviews from parents specifically mentioning newborn comfort and proper spinal support.

    What's the difference between an all terrain stroller and a regular jogging stroller for newborns?

    All terrain strollers have larger, more durable wheels and suspension designed for multiple surfaces like gravel and light trails, while jogging strollers are specifically built for running with a fixed front wheel and narrower frame. For newborns, all terrain strollers are generally better since they offer more recline options, whereas jogging strollers often restrict recline for safety during running.
