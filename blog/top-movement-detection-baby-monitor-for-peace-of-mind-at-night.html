Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is movement detection on baby monitors?

Most modern movement detection baby monitors use infrared or video-based sensors with 95%+ accuracy rates, though they can occasionally trigger false alarms from shadows, pets, or drafts. The best monitors use advanced AI to distinguish between normal movements and concerning patterns like lack of motion for extended periods. Accuracy improves significantly when the monitor is properly positioned and calibrated for your nursery setup.

Can movement detection baby monitors detect SIDS?

Movement detection monitors can alert you if your baby stops moving entirely, but they cannot diagnose or prevent SIDS. These monitors are best used as a supplementary safety tool alongside safe sleep practices like back sleeping and firm mattresses. For medical-grade monitoring, consult your pediatrician about options like hospital-approved respiration monitors.

What's the difference between video and pad-based movement detection monitors?

Video-based monitors use cameras to track visible movement and are easier to set up, while pad-based monitors use pressure sensors placed under the mattress to detect breathing and motion with higher precision. Pad monitors are more reliable for detecting subtle movements but can be more expensive and require proper installation. Many parents prefer pad monitors for newborns due to superior sensitivity, then switch to video monitors as babies grow.

Do movement detection baby monitors work if my baby is under blankets?

Video monitors struggle to detect motion under thick blankets or in low light, while under-mattress pad sensors work reliably regardless of bedding since they measure physical pressure and vibration. If you use a video monitor, ensure your nursery has adequate infrared lighting and avoid heavy blankets to maintain accurate detection. Pad-based systems are the better choice if you prefer traditional bedding.
