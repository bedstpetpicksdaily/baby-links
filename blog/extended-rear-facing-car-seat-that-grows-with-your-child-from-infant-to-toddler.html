    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long can a child stay rear facing in a convertible car seat?

    Most extended rear facing convertible car seats allow children to remain rear facing until they reach 40-50 pounds or 50+ inches in height, typically around ages 3-4. This extended period significantly reduces injury risk compared to forward facing, as rear facing seats better protect a child's developing head, neck, and spine during crashes.

    When should I switch from rear facing to forward facing?

    The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping children rear facing as long as possible, ideally until they outgrow the seat's rear facing limits by height or weight. You should only switch to forward facing once your child exceeds the maximum rear facing specifications listed in your specific car seat's manual, not based on age alone.

    Can you use a rear facing convertible car seat from newborn?

    Yes, most convertible car seats designed for extended rear facing can be used from birth with proper newborn inserts and adjustable harness positions. Always verify that the seat accommodates your newborn's weight (typically 5-8 pounds minimum) and follow the manufacturer's installation instructions for infant use.

    Is a convertible car seat worth it vs. an infant car seat?

    A convertible car seat can save you money long-term since you won't need to purchase a separate infant seat, and it grows with your child up to age 4-5. However, if you plan multiple children or frequently travel via rideshare, an infant seat's portability may be worth the additional investment despite needing two seats over time.
