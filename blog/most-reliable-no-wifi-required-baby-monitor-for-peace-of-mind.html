    Frequently Asked Questions

    What baby monitor works without WiFi or internet?

    Closed-system baby monitors like the Infant Optics DXR-8 and Motorola MBP series use a secure, private connection between the camera and parent unit without requiring WiFi or internet access. These monitors transmit video directly between devices using a closed network, making them reliable even if your internet goes down.

    How far does a no WiFi baby monitor work?

    Most closed-system monitors have a range of 700-900 feet in open space, though walls and interference can reduce this to 200-400 feet in typical homes. If you need monitoring across multiple floors or a large house, check the manufacturer's range specifications before purchasing.

    Is a WiFi-free baby monitor safer than a WiFi monitor?

    Closed-system monitors are generally more secure because they don't connect to the internet or cloud servers, eliminating risks of hacking or unauthorized access. However, they sacrifice the convenience of remote viewing when you're away from home, so choose based on your priority between security and accessibility.

    Can I view a no WiFi baby monitor on my phone?

    Most offline monitors cannot be viewed on your smartphone—they require a dedicated parent unit receiver that stays within range of the camera. Some newer models like the Nanit Pro offer optional WiFi viewing as an add-on feature while maintaining secure local monitoring as the primary function.
