Frequently Asked Questions

Does the expandable seat feel stable with two kids, or does it wobble?

The dual configuration is genuinely stable. I've loaded it with a 35-pound kindergartener in the main seat and a two-year-old in the expanded section, totaling nearly 60 pounds, and there's zero frame flex. The wheelbase widens appropriately to handle the weight distribution. It's noticeably heavier to push (which is expected), but stability never becomes a concern even on uneven terrain.

How does it handle summer heat and UV exposure?

The fabric held up remarkably well through July and August without fading significantly. That said, I did use a stroller shade accessory during peak afternoon hours—not because the fabric degraded faster, but because the kids were uncomfortable in direct heat. The canopy is decent but doesn't block side sun exposure, which matters when you're parked at the playground for extended periods.

Is the expandable seat actually worth it versus buying a tandem stroller?

The Gazelle S flexibility wins if you want single-stroller compactness initially. I've tested it against dedicated tandem models, and the Gazelle feels more nimble solo. If you already know you'll have two kids close together, a traditional tandem might be simpler. But if there's an age gap or you value maneuverability between kid additions, the expandable seat design is genuinely convenient—you're not wrestling a wide double stroller through narrow spaces while your first child is an only child.
