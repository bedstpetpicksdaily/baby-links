Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are temperature sensors in baby monitors?

Most quality baby monitors have temperature accuracy within ±1-2°F of actual room temperature. Look for monitors that display real-time temperature updates and allow you to set custom temperature alerts so you're notified immediately if the room gets too hot or cold for your baby.

Can a baby monitor alert me if my baby's room gets too warm?

Yes, temperature-sensing baby monitors let you set high and low temperature thresholds that trigger phone notifications or audible alarms when exceeded. This is especially valuable for preventing overheating, which is a known risk factor for SIDS, and for protecting your baby during power outages or heating failures.

Do I need WiFi for a temperature sensing baby monitor to work?

Many monitors offer both WiFi and non-WiFi options—WiFi models send temperature alerts to your phone from anywhere, while non-WiFi models use closed systems with a parent unit. Choose WiFi if you want remote alerts when away from home, or a non-WiFi model if you prefer a private connection that doesn't depend on internet reliability.

What room temperature is safest for a sleeping baby?

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping a baby's room between 68-72°F (20-22°C) to reduce SIDS risk. A temperature-sensing monitor allows you to maintain this range consistently and alerts you to dangerous temperature swings, giving you peace of mind throughout the night.
