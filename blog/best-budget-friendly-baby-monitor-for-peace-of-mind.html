Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest baby monitor that actually works well?

Budget-friendly monitors like the Motorola MBP8 or VTech DM111 typically cost $50-80 and offer solid performance with good range, night vision, and two-way talk features. While they lack WiFi connectivity, they provide reliable closed-system monitoring without monthly subscription fees, making them excellent value for parents who don't need smartphone access.

Do I really need a WiFi baby monitor or is a regular monitor enough?

A regular closed-system monitor is sufficient if you stay within range (usually 700-1000 feet) and only monitor from home, but WiFi monitors are worth the extra $20-40 if you need to check on your baby from work, outdoor spaces, or while away from home. Consider your lifestyle and daily routine before deciding—many parents find the remote viewing capability invaluable for peace of mind.

What baby monitor features are worth paying extra for?

Night vision, two-way audio, and temperature monitoring are essential features found on most budget monitors ($50-100 range). Pan-and-tilt, lullabies, and smartphone connectivity are nice-to-haves that increase costs; prioritize based on your needs rather than buying every feature available.

Are cheap baby monitors safe and do they have privacy concerns?

Reputable budget brands like Motorola, VTech, and Summer Infant use encrypted connections and have solid safety records, though closed-system monitors are generally more secure than WiFi ones since they aren't internet-connected. Always buy from established manufacturers, check reviews for security concerns, and use strong passwords if you choose a WiFi monitor.
