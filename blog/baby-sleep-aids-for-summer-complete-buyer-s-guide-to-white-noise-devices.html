Why White Noise Matters for Summer Sleep

Summer disrupts established sleep patterns in ways parents don't always anticipate. Increased outdoor noise, brighter mornings, and the general hustle of the season can make naptime and bedtime battles harder than ever. White noise works by masking these environmental sounds and creating a consistent auditory environment that signals to babies it's time to sleep.

Beyond sound masking, white noise mimics the familiar environment of the womb, which naturally soothes newborns and young infants. The continuous, gentle sound helps regulate baby's nervous system and can reduce startle responses that often wake sleeping babies. For summer specifically, a reliable white noise device means you can maintain sleep routines whether you're at home, on vacation, or dealing with unexpected household noise.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use white noise devices for newborns safely?

Yes, white noise is safe for newborns when used correctly. Keep the volume at 50 decibels or lower (similar to rainfall), position the device at least 7 feet from the crib, and ensure it doesn't get too hot. Baby Shusher is specifically designed with infant safety standards in mind.

How long should I use white noise for baby sleep?

White noise can be used for naps and nighttime sleep throughout infancy and early childhood. Most parents use it until age 3-4, though some continue longer. Using white noise consistently helps establish strong sleep associations.
