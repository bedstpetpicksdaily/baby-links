    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the safest infant car seat for a newborn?

    Rear-facing infant car seats are considered the safest option for newborns because they support the head, neck, and spine during crashes. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) recommends keeping infants rear-facing until age 2 or until they reach the height and weight limits of their seat. Top-rated options include the Chicco KeyFit 35, Graco SnugRide, and Britax B-Safe, all of which have excellent crash test ratings and are easy to install correctly.

    How do I know if a car seat is installed correctly?

    A properly installed car seat should move no more than 1 inch side-to-side or front-to-back at the base where the belt goes through. You can check the installation using the seat's built-in level indicator, and many hospitals and fire departments offer free installation checks. Many car seats also come with a base that clicks securely into place, which reduces installation errors and makes it easier for first-time parents.

    Which car seat has the easiest installation for first-time parents?

    Car seats with a dedicated base that uses LATCH (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) or a rigid latch system are easiest to install, as they eliminate guesswork and reduce installation errors. The Chicco KeyFit 35 and Graco SnugRide 35 both feature simple base systems that click securely into place and provide clear indicators when properly installed. Models with one-handed adjustment straps and clear labeling also make setup less stressful for nervous parents.

    What should I look for in a safe infant car seat?

    Look for seats with excellent NHTSA and IIHS crash test ratings, a secure base or latch system, side-impact protection, and a five-point harness for optimal safety. The seat should also have adjustable headrests, be easy to install correctly, and come with clear instructions and customer support. Additionally, choose a seat that fits your vehicle type and lifestyle—convertible seats that grow with your child are a cost-effective investment for first-time parents.
