What to Look For

  • Lightweight and compact design: Look for car seats that weigh under 10 pounds and have a narrow profile. Lighter seats are easier to install and reinstall at different vehicles or locations, and a compact design means it won't dominate your car's interior or make passengers feel cramped during those long stretches.
  • Extended recline options: Since your baby will be sitting for hours, the ability to adjust the recline angle is crucial for maintaining proper spinal alignment and allowing for more comfortable rest. A seat that offers multiple recline positions helps prevent the slouched posture that can occur during long drives and reduces pressure on your baby's neck and back.
  • Superior side-impact protection and ventilation: Long journeys mean prolonged sun exposure and temperature fluctuations inside the vehicle. Choose a seat with excellent side-impact wings, energy-absorbing foam, and breathable fabric that won't cause your baby to overheat during summer travel or feel uncomfortable in varying climates.
