Finding the right stroller for a newborn when you're planning to travel or tackle uneven terrain is one of the most important decisions you'll make in your first months of parenthood. Unlike standard urban strollers, an all-terrain option needs to handle everything from cobblestone streets to gravel paths while still providing the safety and comfort your newborn requires. This guide will help you navigate the specific features that matter most for this demanding combination of needs.

What to Look For

  • Suspension System and Wheel Quality: Look for strollers with independent suspension on all four wheels and large, air-filled tires (typically 12+ inches in diameter). This setup absorbs bumps and vibrations that could otherwise jostle a newborn, while providing genuine traction on loose or uneven surfaces. Avoid smaller wheels or solid tires, which transmit every bump directly to your baby.
  • Newborn-Specific Recline and Support: Ensure the stroller fully reclines to a flat position, which is essential for newborns who cannot sit upright. Check that the seat provides adequate head and neck support, and verify that it's compatible with infant car seats if you plan to use a travel system. Some all-terrain strollers sacrifice proper newborn support, so this feature is non-negotiable.
  • Weight and Portability for Travel: All-terrain strollers are naturally heavier, but prioritize models under 30 pounds that fold compactly enough to fit in car trunks or overhead compartments. Consider whether the fold is one-handed (ideal for traveling with a newborn) and whether it has a travel bag included. Balance durability with practicality—you need something that won't require an extra suitcase.
