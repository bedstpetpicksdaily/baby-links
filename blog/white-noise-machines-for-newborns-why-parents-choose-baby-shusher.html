Understanding White Noise for Newborn Sleep

Newborns are adjusting to life outside the womb, where they were surrounded by constant, gentle sounds—including mom's heartbeat, blood flow, and muffled external noise. This acoustic environment actually helped babies sleep deeply and feel secure. When babies enter the quiet modern world, many struggle to settle down. White noise replicates that familiar, womb-like soundscape, triggering the calming reflex that helps newborns relax and sleep longer.

Research supports this approach. Studies show that consistent white noise can help babies fall asleep faster, sleep deeper, and experience fewer night wakings. For parents dealing with colic, overstimulation, or simply an alert baby, white noise machines provide a non-invasive, safe way to create an optimal sleep environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is white noise safe for newborns?

Yes, when used appropriately. White noise at safe volume levels (around 50-60 decibels) can help babies sleep without causing hearing damage. Baby Shusher is specifically designed with safe sound levels for infants. Always ensure the device is placed at a safe distance from the crib and set to an appropriate volume.

How long should I use white noise for my baby?

White noise can be used throughout infancy and early childhood. Many parents continue using it as a sleep aid for toddlers and older children. You can use it for naps, nighttime sleep, or during fussy periods. There's no set age limit, though you may find it naturally phases out as your child grows.
