Frequently Asked Questions

Is ISOFIX really safer than a regular seat belt installation?

Yes, measurably. ISOFIX anchors directly to your vehicle's frame, eliminating slack and reducing the chance of improper installation. Seat belt setups depend on correct routing and tension—easy to get wrong. ISOFIX takes the guesswork out and provides a more stable connection during both normal driving and crash scenarios. However, both methods meet safety standards when installed correctly; ISOFIX just removes installation error as a variable.

Can I use this base with car seats from other brands?

No—the Paxfix Base is proprietary to Maxi-Cosi infant carriers. It won't work with Graco, Chicco, Cybex, or other brands' seats. If you think you might switch brands later or have family members with different car seats, a universal ISOFIX base is worth the extra flexibility, even if it costs slightly more upfront.

Does this base work in older cars without ISOFIX anchors?

No. Your vehicle must have ISOFIX anchor points built into the seat frame (most cars from 2006 onward have them). Check your vehicle manual to confirm. If your car lacks ISOFIX, this base won't work—you'd need a standard seat belt installation or a vehicle upgrade. This is a real limitation for older car owners, so verify compatibility before buying.

What's the price range, and is it worth it compared to universal bases?

Pricing varies, but Maxi-Cosi branded bases typically run $80-120, while quality universal ISOFIX bases often cost $50-80 and work across multiple brands. If you're budget-conscious and not locked into Maxi-Cosi, the savings add up—especially if you plan to use multiple car seats over time. However, if you already own compatible Maxi-Cosi gear, the extra cost for a brand-specific base may be justified by proven compatibility.
