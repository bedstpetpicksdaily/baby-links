Frequently Asked Questions

How long can my child actually stay rear-facing in this seat?

The Joie Elevate R129 accommodates rear-facing up to approximately 4 years old (or until the child reaches the maximum rear-facing weight limit, typically around 18-20kg depending on your specific model). This is significantly longer than traditional boosters, making it genuinely useful for keeping toddlers rear-facing into the preschool years. Check your manual for exact limits—they vary slightly by market.

Will three of these fit across a standard back seat?

At 33.5cm wide, yes—most sedans and crossovers can fit three, though it's snug. Some truck back seats offer more room. Measure your vehicle's back seat width before ordering if you're doing a three-across setup. This is where the narrow profile actually matters versus wider competitors that force you into a two-seat compromise.

Is the R129 certification actually better than older i-Size seats I might already own?

R129 is the newest EU safety regulation with stricter side-impact requirements and better testing protocols than previous i-Size (R44/04). In real terms: yes, it's better. But if you already own an i-Size seat that's clean and functional, replacing it purely for the R129 certification isn't urgent. Use this seat for new purchases or upgrades. The difference matters most in new vehicles where you're making fresh choices.
