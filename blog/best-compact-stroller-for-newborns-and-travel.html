What to Look For

  • Ultra-Compact Fold Under 20 Pounds: When you're managing a car seat, diaper bag, and a tiny human, weight matters. Look for strollers that collapse down to cabin-friendly dimensions (ideally under 22 inches) and weigh less than 15 pounds so you can actually carry them one-handed while holding your baby. Aluminum frames beat plastic for durability without the extra heft.
  • Newborn-Friendly Recline and Support: Newborns can't sit upright, so your stroller must recline fully flat or nearly flat. The seat should provide proper head and neck support, and bonus points if it's compatible with your infant car seat—this let's you click the seat directly onto the stroller frame, avoiding the need to wake a sleeping baby at every stop.
  • One-Handed Operation and Easy Setup: When you're traveling solo with a newborn, your hands are always full. Prioritize strollers with single-hand fold mechanisms, smooth one-handed steering, and quick assembly. If it takes five minutes to set up at the airport gate, you've chosen wrong.
