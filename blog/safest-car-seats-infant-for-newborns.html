What to Look For in a Newborn Car Seat

  • Rear-Facing Design with Extended Weight Limits: The safest position for infants is rear-facing, and you'll want a seat that accommodates newborns starting at 4-5 pounds and extends to at least 30-35 pounds. Rear-facing seats provide crucial support for your baby's head, neck, and spine during sudden stops or collisions.
  • Five-Point Harness System with Proper Installation Features: Look for a car seat with an adjustable five-point harness that grows with your baby and includes either LATCH (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) connectors or a secure base system. The installation method matters as much as the seat itself—improper installation is the leading cause of car seat misuse.
  • Side-Impact Protection and Energy-Absorbing Materials: Premium newborn car seats include reinforced side panels, energy-absorbing foam, and a sturdy shell designed to protect against both frontal and side-impact collisions. Check that the seat has passed all NHTSA standards and consider additional safety certifications.
