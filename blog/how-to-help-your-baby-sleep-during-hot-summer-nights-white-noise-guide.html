Why White Noise Works for Summer Sleep

Babies are naturally soothed by consistent, monotonous sounds that recreate the acoustic environment of the womb. During pregnancy, babies hear a constant, low-frequency hum around 50-60 decibels—similar to a washing machine or vacuum cleaner. White noise replicates this familiar auditory environment, triggering the calming reflex that helps babies settle down and sleep more soundly. During summer months when external noise levels spike, white noise becomes an essential tool for maintaining sleep quality and duration.

Temperature Control Combined with Sound Management

While white noise addresses auditory distractions, managing room temperature is equally important for summer sleep success. Ideally, babies sleep best in rooms between 68-72°F (20-22°C). Pair your air conditioning or fan with a quality white noise machine like the Baby Shusher to create the ultimate sleep environment. Frequently Asked Questions

Is white noise safe for babies?

Yes, white noise is safe for babies when used appropriately. Keep volume at 50-60 decibels (similar to normal conversation) and position devices at least 7 feet away from the crib. The Baby Shusher is specifically designed with infant safety in mind and meets all safety standards for baby products.

Can white noise damage my baby's hearing?

No, if used correctly. Unsafe volumes exceed 85 decibels, while the Baby Shusher operates at gentle, safe levels specifically calibrated for infant ears. Always monitor volume levels and avoid placing sound machines directly inside the crib.

How long should I use white noise each night?

You can use white noise for the entire night or just during the initial sleep onset phase. Many parents use it throughout sleep sessions to maintain the soothing environment. The Baby Shusher can run continuously, giving you flexibility to match your family's preferences.

Will my baby become dependent on white noise?

White noise creates a positive sleep association rather than problematic dependency. Since the shushing sound mimics natural parental soothing, it's considered a healthy sleep aid. You can gradually reduce use as your child grows if desired.
