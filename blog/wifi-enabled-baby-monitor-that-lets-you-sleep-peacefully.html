    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is a WiFi baby monitor safe and secure?

    Modern WiFi baby monitors use encryption and secure servers to protect video feeds, but security depends on choosing reputable brands with regular security updates. Always use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and avoid budget monitors with poor security reviews. Look for monitors that comply with data privacy standards and don't store footage on unsecured cloud servers.

    Can I watch my baby monitor on my phone from anywhere?

    Yes, WiFi-enabled monitors let you view live video on your smartphone from any location with internet connection through dedicated apps. You'll typically need to create an account, connect the monitor to your home WiFi, and log in on your phone to access the feed remotely. Some monitors offer cloud storage for recorded clips, allowing you to review footage even when away from home.

    What internet speed do I need for a WiFi baby monitor?

    Most WiFi baby monitors require a minimum of 2-5 Mbps upload speed for reliable streaming, though faster speeds provide better video quality. If you have multiple devices streaming simultaneously, aim for at least 10 Mbps. Many parents successfully use standard home WiFi networks; you can test your speed using free online tools before purchasing.

    Do WiFi baby monitors work if my internet goes down?

    No, WiFi monitors require an active internet connection to transmit video to your phone, so they won't work during outages. However, some monitors have local viewing options where you can see the feed on a separate parent unit within your home using a direct connection. If internet reliability is a concern, consider hybrid monitors that work both via WiFi and with a dedicated parent unit.
