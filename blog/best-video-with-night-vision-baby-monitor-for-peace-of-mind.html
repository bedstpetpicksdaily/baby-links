    Frequently Asked Questions

    How far can night vision baby monitors see in the dark?

    Most quality night vision baby monitors can see between 6 to 15 feet clearly in complete darkness, with some premium models reaching up to 20+ feet. The distance depends on the infrared LED power and camera lens quality, so check the manufacturer's specifications for the room size you need to monitor.

    Is infrared night vision safe for my baby's eyes?

    Yes, infrared night vision used in baby monitors is completely safe for babies' eyes because it uses invisible infrared light that doesn't emit harmful radiation or disturb sleep. The infrared LEDs operate at wavelengths that are outside the visible light spectrum, so they won't cause any damage even with prolonged exposure.

    What's the difference between night vision and thermal imaging on baby monitors?

    Night vision uses infrared LEDs to illuminate the room and capture visible details like your baby's position and facial features, while thermal imaging detects heat signatures and shows temperature variations. For most parents, night vision is preferred because it provides clearer, more detailed images that help you see exactly what your baby is doing.

    Do I need WiFi for a baby monitor with night vision?

    Not necessarily—many baby monitors offer local closed-system viewing between a parent unit and camera without WiFi, which provides more privacy and security. However, WiFi-enabled monitors let you check on your baby remotely from your phone when away from home, so choose based on whether you need remote access.
