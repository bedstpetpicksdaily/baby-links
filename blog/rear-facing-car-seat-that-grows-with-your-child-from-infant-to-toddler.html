Frequently Asked Questions

How long can a child stay in a rear-facing car seat?

Most convertible rear-facing car seats can accommodate children from birth up to 40-50 pounds, typically lasting until age 3-4 years old. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping children rear-facing as long as possible, ideally until they reach the seat's maximum rear-facing weight or height limit. Always check your specific seat's manual for exact limits, as they vary by model.

Is a convertible car seat safer than an infant carrier?

Convertible car seats that accommodate rear-facing from birth offer similar safety protection to infant carriers but with the advantage of lasting much longer without needing to purchase a second seat. They're tested to the same safety standards and provide excellent protection for newborns and older infants when used correctly. The main trade-off is that convertible seats are heavier and less portable than dedicated infant carriers.

Can you move a rear-facing car seat from front to back seat?

Yes, you can move most convertible car seats between vehicles, but the safest position is always the back seat, preferably in the center. Never place a rear-facing car seat in the front seat, especially if your vehicle has an active airbag, as it can be dangerous during deployment. Check your car seat manual and vehicle manual for specific installation guidelines when switching locations.

What's the difference between a convertible and an all-in-one car seat?

Convertible seats typically go from rear-facing infant to forward-facing toddler, while all-in-one seats usually add a third booster stage for older children (up to 100+ pounds). All-in-one seats last longer and eliminate the need for multiple seat purchases, but they're bulkier and may not be ideal if you frequently switch cars. Choose based on your budget and how long you plan to use the seat in your vehicle.
