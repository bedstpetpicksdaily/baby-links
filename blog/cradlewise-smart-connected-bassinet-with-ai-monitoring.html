Frequently Asked Questions

How does Cradlewise's AI differ from a standard video monitor with motion alerts?

Standard monitors detect any motion in frame; Cradlewise claims to analyze movement patterns and breathing to identify distress specifically. Owners report significantly fewer false positives (the monitor waking you because your baby shifted position). That said, no AI is perfect—some users still get alerts for normal sleep movements until they adjust sensitivity settings. The real difference is consistency: Cradlewise catches actual problems more reliably than comparing random motion to a preset threshold.

Does the automatic rocking actually help babies sleep longer?

Anecdotal evidence from reviews suggests it helps babies fall asleep faster and may reduce brief awakenings. However, this varies dramatically by infant temperament. Some babies respond brilliantly to gentle motion; others find it disruptive. You can't predict your baby's preference beforehand, which is a gamble at this price point. If you already know your baby loves motion (from a swing or car rides), this feature has high probability of paying off. If you're unsure, test with a cheaper bassinet first.

Is the app monitoring secure, and does it require good WiFi?

Cradlewise uses encrypted connections and doesn't store video on servers (monitoring is local network-based). Privacy is solid by baby monitor standards. On WiFi stability: the app requires consistent 2.4GHz connection. Users with weak WiFi signals report dropped connections, so verify your home network before buying. If you have WiFi dead zones in your nursery, this becomes an expensive paperweight. Test your signal strength first.

How does Cradlewise compare to alternatives like SNOO or Hatch Restore?

SNOO ($1,595) also has auto-rocking but focuses on swaddled newborns specifically—limited to 6 months. Cradlewise works longer but costs similarly and lacks SNOO's proven clinical data. Hatch Restore ($200–$400) is a sound machine + light combo, not a bassinet. If you need 12+ months of use, Cradlewise offers better longevity than SNOO. If you want bassinet + monitoring + smart features at lower cost, you're looking at $700–$900 alternatives that skip the AI (Babyletto, Graco Sense2Snooze). Cradlewise isn't objectively better—it's just more expensive with different trade-offs.
