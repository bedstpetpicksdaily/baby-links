Choosing a stroller isn't one-size-fits-all. An urban parent navigating crowded sidewalks needs something completely different from someone who jogs on trails or travels frequently with a newborn. We've tested and compared the actual strollers mentioned most across 2026 reviews to help you match your lifestyle to the right model—not just pick whatever gets promoted loudest.

This guide covers the specific products parents are actually choosing: the Baby Trend Expedition Jogger for serious off-road use, the UPPAbaby Cruz V2 for city navigation, and lightweight options like the Babyzen YOYO2 for frequent travelers. We'll show you the real trade-offs between each, so you can decide what actually matters for your family.

Baby Trend Expedition Jogger

A three-wheel jogging stroller with large air-filled tires and independent suspension on each wheel. Features a hand brake that locks into jogging mode, swivel front wheel that unlocks for running, and a generously sized canopy with UPF 50+ protection. Tested on pavement, dirt paths, gravel, and rougher terrain at nature preserves with consistent performance and over 500 customer reviews averaging 4.3 stars.

The storage basket holds groceries, water bottles, and diaper bags without blocking wheel movement. The brake lever requires intentional pressure to fully engage—something to be aware of if you're rushing.

Best for: Parents who jog regularly, live near trails, or need serious off-road capability for camping and unpaved areas

UPPAbaby Cruz V2

A compact urban stroller designed specifically for city living. Folds to just 23 x 18 x 10 inches with one-handed operation, making it ideal for tight apartment spaces and car trunks. Features large, puncture-resistant rubber wheels designed to handle cobblestones, brick, tree roots, and potholes without jarring. Robust, water-resistant fabric and a canopy that extends far enough for rain and UV protection.

Built for navigating crowded sidewalks while remaining nimble enough for public transit scenarios where you're managing a baby, bags, and a folded stroller simultaneously.

Best for: Urban parents dealing with rough pavement, unpredictable weather, limited storage space, and frequent public transportation use

Babyzen YOYO2

A lightweight stroller weighing just 13.9 pounds that folds smaller than most airline carry-ons. Fully reclining seat suitable for newborns, pairs seamlessly with all major car seats, and transitions smoothly as your child becomes a toddler. The compact design doesn't sacrifice functionality, and the one-handed folding makes it genuinely practical for parents juggling car seats and diaper bags.

Purpose-built for families managing both newborn and toddler phases simultaneously, or for parents who travel frequently and need maximum portability without constant compromise on features.

Best for: Parents with newborns transitioning to toddlers, frequent travelers, or anyone who carries their stroller in and out of vehicles multiple times daily

Standard All-Terrain Strollers (General Category)

Broader category featuring wheels at least 7 inches in diameter with decent suspension systems. These handle uneven surfaces better than lightweight compact designs but offer less nimbleness than jogging-specific models. Require two-handed folding in many cases and typically weigh 18-25 pounds. Offer weather-resistant materials and good canopy coverage.

Fall between the lightweight travel strollers and dedicated jogging models in both capability and portability—useful if you need moderate off-road ability without committing to a jogger, but you don't need premium urban design.

Best for: Parents in suburban areas or those who encounter mixed terrain (some trails, some pavement) but don't jog or travel constantly

Our Verdict

There's no single 'best' stroller because the right choice depends entirely on your actual lifestyle. If you're jogging regularly or hiking trails, the Baby Trend Expedition Jogger delivers the suspension and tire quality you need—its 4.3-star rating and consistent real-world performance across varied terrain justify the investment. The brake issue is a legitimate concern only if you're the type to rush; otherwise it's a proven option.

For urban parents dealing with rough city pavement, crowded sidewalks, and tight spaces, the UPPAbaby Cruz V2's one-handed fold and puncture-resistant wheels solve real problems that cheap strollers ignore. Yes, it costs more, but the compact fold (23 x 18 x 10 inches) actually saves you money by reducing the need for a separate compact travel stroller.

If you're traveling frequently, moving between a newborn and toddler phase, or simply want maximum portability, the Babyzen YOYO2 at 13.9 pounds is genuinely in a different category—you're trading some rough-terrain capability for the practical advantage of actually carrying your stroller without strain. Standard mid-range all-terrain strollers fall between these options but don't excel at any single task, so they're worth considering only if your needs are truly mixed without clear priority.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you actually use a jogging stroller for daily city walking?

Yes, but it's heavier than necessary. The Baby Trend Expedition works fine for daily use, but you're carrying extra weight and bulk for suspension features you won't utilize on smooth pavement. If you jog even semi-regularly, it's worth it. If you only walk city streets, the UPPAbaby Cruz is more practical.

Is the Babyzen YOYO2 suitable for newborns, or only toddlers?

It's fully suitable for newborns because the seat reclines completely flat, meeting the safety requirement for newborn positioning. It pairs with all major car seats, so you can use it as a travel system from birth onward.

What's the actual weight difference, and does it matter in practice?

The Babyzen YOYO2 is 13.9 pounds, the UPPAbaby Cruz is around 18-20 pounds, and the Baby Trend Expedition is approximately 27-30 pounds. The difference between 14 and 20 pounds is noticeable when you're carrying it frequently (airport, car transitions). The difference between 20 and 30 pounds matters less if you're not moving it constantly, but it's real if you're getting in and out of a car multiple times daily.

Can an all-terrain stroller handle both sand and urban pavement equally well?

All-terrain strollers like the Baby Trend Expedition handle both, but they're optimized for rough surfaces. On smooth urban pavement, you'll notice the heavier frame and larger tires compared to a dedicated urban design like the Cruz V2. Conversely, the Cruz V2 is fine for light beach use but isn't ideal if sand is frequent.

Is the Baby Trend Expedition's brake issue a dealbreaker?

No. It requires intentional pressure to fully engage, which means you need to consciously lock it rather than it engaging automatically. Once you're aware of this, it's not dangerous—just different from strollers with automatic or lighter-touch brakes. Many owners report no issues after learning this quirk.

Which stroller folds the fastest for frequent in-and-out use?

The Babyzen YOYO2 and UPPAbaby Cruz V2 both have one-handed folding mechanisms designed for speed. The YOYO2 folds slightly faster due to its simpler mechanism and lighter weight, but the Cruz V2 is only slightly slower. The Baby Trend Expedition requires both hands and more steps, making it slower.
