    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the safest booster car seat for a 6 year old?

    High-back booster seats are generally considered safest for younger children because they provide head and neck support in side-impact crashes. Look for models that have passed all crash tests, such as those rated highly by NHTSA, and ensure the booster seat properly positions the vehicle's seat belt across your child's chest and lap. Popular safe options include the Graco Turbobooster and Britax Frontier ClickTight, both known for their safety ratings and ease of use.

    How do I know if a booster seat is properly installed?

    The vehicle's seat belt should lay flat across your child's chest (not their neck) and lap (not their stomach), with the booster seat sitting firmly on the vehicle seat with minimal movement side-to-side. Most modern booster seats have guides or grooves showing proper belt positioning, and many come with installation guides or videos. You can also get a free installation check at your local fire department or police station through certified child passenger safety technicians.

    When can my child move from a car seat to a booster seat?

    Your child should transition to a booster seat once they've outgrown their forward-facing car seat (typically around age 4-5), but they must meet the minimum height and weight requirements listed on your specific booster seat. Never rush this transition—it's safer to keep them in a car seat longer than to move to a booster too early. Check your booster seat's manual for exact requirements, as these vary by model.

    What features should I look for in a safe booster car seat?

    Look for a high-back design (especially if your vehicle doesn't have tall headrests), side-impact protection, a secure base that doesn't slide around, and adjustable headrest that grows with your child. LATCH connectors, cup holders, and removable cushions are helpful but secondary—focus first on crash test ratings from NHTSA and real-world safety reviews. Avoid used booster seats or those with unclear accident history, as hidden damage can compromise safety.
