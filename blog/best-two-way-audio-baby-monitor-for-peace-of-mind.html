Frequently Asked Questions

Can you talk through a baby monitor and hear baby cry?

Yes, two-way audio baby monitors let you speak through the monitor so your baby can hear your voice, and you can listen in real-time to hear cries, babbling, or other sounds. This two-way communication is especially useful for soothing a fussy baby without entering the room or for older toddlers who can respond to your voice.

What is the best range for a baby monitor with two way talk?

Most quality two-way audio monitors offer a range of 700-1000 feet in open space, though walls and interference can reduce this. For most homes, a range of 700+ feet is sufficient, but if you need monitoring across multiple floors or a large property, look for WiFi-enabled monitors that work through your home network instead of limited radio range.

Is it safe to use two way talk on baby monitor all night?

Yes, two-way audio monitors are safe to use throughout the night and won't disturb your baby's sleep if used appropriately. The key is using a moderate volume so your voice is comforting rather than startling, and avoiding frequent talking unless necessary—most parents use it only when they hear their baby fussing.

Do two way baby monitors work through walls and closed doors?

Two-way monitors work through walls and closed doors, but the audio quality and range may decrease depending on wall thickness and building materials. WiFi-based monitors are generally more reliable through obstacles than radio-frequency monitors, making them better for monitoring from different rooms or floors with doors closed.
