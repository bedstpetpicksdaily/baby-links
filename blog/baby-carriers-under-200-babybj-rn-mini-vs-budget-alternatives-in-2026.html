Frequently Asked Questions

Do I actually need the newborn insert, or can I start at 8 pounds without it?

The BabyBjörn Mini Organic Cotton includes the newborn insert, eliminating a separate $40 purchase. The insert works without awkward bunching or constant readjustment during typical outings—it's functional, not a gimmick. Budget carriers may require this add-on separately, so factor that into total cost comparison.

How much does organic cotton actually matter in real-world use?

Noticeable, but climate-dependent. In summer heat (88 degrees+), organic cotton stays visibly drier against the parent's body and prevents the heat-trapping issues common in synthetic carriers. In moderate or cool climates, or for sporadic babywearing, the difference is negligible. This is a genuine advantage only if you're regularly wearing a carrier during warm months.

Why is the BabyBjörn Mini so much more expensive if the Ergobaby Omni 360 does the same thing?

The Ergobaby Omni 360 doesn't do the same thing—it covers a wider weight range, has different ergonomics, and carries a broader feature set. If I outgrow the BabyBjörn Mini at 24 pounds, what do I do?

You buy a second carrier. This makes your total investment higher than choosing a single all-in-one carrier from the start.
