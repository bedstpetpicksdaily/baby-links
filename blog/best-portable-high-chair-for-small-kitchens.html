    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the smallest portable high chair that folds up?

    The smallest portable high chairs typically fold down to 12-15 inches wide and weigh between 5-8 pounds, making them easy to store in closets or under sinks. Look for models like the Summer Infant Pop and Sit or Graco Blossom that collapse completely flat and fit in standard storage spaces. These compact models usually have a footprint of around 2-3 square feet when fully expanded.

    Can you use a portable high chair as a regular chair in a small kitchen?

    Yes, many portable high chairs are designed to function as both a feeding seat and occasional regular chair, allowing them to serve dual purposes in tight spaces. Models with removable trays and adjustable heights work best for this, as you can remove the tray and use it for older toddlers at the regular table. However, they're typically less sturdy than standard dining chairs for prolonged use, so check weight limits and stability before relying on them as primary seating.

    What's the difference between a portable high chair and a booster seat for small kitchens?

    Portable high chairs are standalone seats with legs that take up more floor space but offer independent feeding stations, while booster seats attach directly to regular chairs and require minimal storage. Booster seats are generally better for small kitchens since they don't add extra furniture, but portable high chairs provide more safety features and work at any table height. Choose based on whether you have floor space available and whether your existing chairs are compatible with boosters.

    How much counter or floor space do I need for a compact portable high chair?

    Most compact portable high chairs require a footprint of about 24-30 inches wide and 20-28 inches deep when set up, which fits in most small kitchen corners. When folded, they compress to roughly the size of a large suitcase (12-18 inches wide) that can slide under a counter or in a cabinet. Measure your available space before purchasing and check the product dimensions in both expanded and folded positions to ensure it fits your specific kitchen layout.
