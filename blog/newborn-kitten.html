Frequently Asked Questions

What do newborn kittens need to survive without their mother?

Newborn kittens require bottle feeding with kitten formula every 2-3 hours (including overnight) until 4 weeks old, as they cannot digest regular milk. They also need a warm environment maintained at 85-90°F, as they cannot regulate their body temperature for the first few weeks.

How much does it cost to care for a newborn kitten?

Initial costs typically range from $500-$1,500 for formula, bottles, heating equipment, and initial veterinary care, with monthly expenses of $50-$100 for formula and supplies. If the kitten requires emergency care or bottle feeding complications arise, costs can exceed $2,000.

At what age can newborn kittens be left alone?

Newborn kittens cannot be left alone and require round-the-clock care and feeding for the first 4-6 weeks of life. After 8 weeks, they may be left alone for a few hours, but young kittens under 12 weeks should never be left alone for more than 2-3 hours.

Do newborn kittens need vaccinations and when should they start?

Kittens should receive their first FVRCP vaccine (feline distemper and respiratory disease) at 6-8 weeks old, followed by boosters every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks. Rabies vaccination is typically given at 12 weeks and is legally required in most areas.
