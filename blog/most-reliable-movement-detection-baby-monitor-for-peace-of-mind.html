Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are movement detection baby monitors at detecting SIDS?

Movement detection monitors use sensitive sensors or video analysis to track your baby's chest movements and alert you if motion stops for a set period (usually 20 seconds). While they provide real-time alerts, pediatricians emphasize they're not replacements for safe sleep practices like back sleeping and firm surfaces—use them as an additional comfort tool, not a medical device.

What's the difference between under-mattress sensors and video-based movement detection?

Under-mattress sensors (like Owlet) detect physical movement through vibration and pressure, while video monitors use AI to track chest movement visually through a camera. Mattress sensors are generally more reliable for actual movement detection, but video monitors offer the added benefit of seeing your baby and may have fewer false alarms depending on the technology.

Do movement detection baby monitors have a lot of false alarms?

False alarms are common with under-mattress sensors, especially if your baby rolls around, kicks blankets, or if the pad shifts—this can lead to unnecessary anxiety. Higher-end models with better AI calibration and adjustable sensitivity settings tend to have fewer false positives, so check reviews specifically mentioning false alarm frequency.

Which movement detection baby monitor is most reliable according to parents?

The Owlet Smart Sock and Nanit Plus are consistently rated highest for reliability by parents, with Owlet praised for accurate heart rate and oxygen monitoring alongside movement detection, while Nanit offers superior video quality and AI-powered movement tracking. Both have strong user reviews, though Owlet has been more widely tested for SIDS detection specifically.
