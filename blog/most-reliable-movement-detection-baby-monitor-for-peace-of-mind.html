What to Look For

  • Under-Mattress Sensor Technology: The most reliable movement detection monitors use pressure-sensitive pads placed under the crib mattress that detect even subtle movements and breathing patterns. This non-contact method eliminates false alarms from loose blankets or clothing while providing accurate data about your baby's activity levels throughout the night.
  • Dual Alert Systems with Phone Connectivity: Look for monitors that combine audible alerts at the base station with smartphone notifications, ensuring you're alerted even if you're in another part of the house or outside the range of the portable unit. This redundancy means you'll never miss an important notification during critical sleep hours.
  • Extended Battery Life and Backup Power: Since movement detection monitors require consistent power to function properly, choose models with batteries lasting 12+ hours and built-in backup systems. Power failures shouldn't compromise your baby's safety monitoring, so prioritize units with redundant power options and low-battery warnings.
