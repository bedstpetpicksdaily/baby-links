Buying Guide: 3 Things to Look For

  • Weight and Portability: Consider how often you'll fold and transport the stroller. Lightweight models (under 15 lbs) are easier to manage on stairs and in car trunks, while heavier strollers often offer more features and durability.
  • Terrain Compatibility: Think about your environment. Joggers with larger wheels work best on uneven surfaces and trails, while compact strollers excel in tight urban spaces and malls.
  • Longevity and Growth: Determine if you need the stroller for one child or multiple years. Reversible seats, modular systems, and double configurations offer better long-term value for growing families.

FAQ About Strollers

What's the difference between a jogging stroller and a regular stroller?

Jogging strollers feature larger wheels, better suspension, and a fixed front wheel for stability during runs. Regular strollers prioritize maneuverability and typically have smaller, swiveling wheels better suited for shopping and casual walks. Jogging strollers are heavier but designed for uneven terrain, while standard strollers focus on compact storage and easy turns.

How do I choose between a single and double stroller?

Single strollers work well if you have one child or significant age gaps between kids, offering better maneuverability and lighter weight. Double strollers are essential for twins or closely-spaced children but are bulkier and more expensive. Consider your lifestyle, car space, and whether you'll need the stroller long-term before deciding.
