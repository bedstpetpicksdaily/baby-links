What to Look For

  • Extended Rear-Facing Capacity: Choose a seat that accommodates children up to at least 40-50 pounds or 50+ inches, allowing your child to remain rear-facing well beyond age two (when the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends transitioning to forward-facing). Higher weight and height limits mean longer use and greater safety benefits during the critical early years.
  • Easy Installation and LATCH System: Look for car seats with intuitive LATCH (Lower Anchors and Tethers for Children) connections, clear indicator lights showing proper installation, and adjustable bases that accommodate different vehicle angles. A properly installed seat is exponentially safer than one that seems secured but has gaps or movement.
  • Comfort and Recline Options: Since rear-facing seats position your infant at an angle, adjustable recline settings are essential for proper spinal alignment and comfort during longer trips. Removable, machine-washable seat covers and adequate padding also matter when you're dealing with spit-up, diaper accidents, and daily wear.
