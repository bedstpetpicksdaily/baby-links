Traditional Sound Machines in Hot Weather

Standard sound machines offer a wider variety of sounds—from white noise to nature sounds to rainfall—giving parents more customization options. However, many traditional sound machines have a larger footprint and require more power, which can generate additional heat in an already warm nursery. This is a significant drawback during summer months when every degree matters for your baby's comfort.

Additionally, some sound machines feature night lights or display screens that emit light and heat. While these features can be appealing in winter, they become problematic during hot weather when you're trying to keep the room as cool as possible. The extra heat and light can disrupt your baby's sleep quality and make the room feel even warmer.
