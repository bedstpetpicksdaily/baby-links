What to Look For

  • Suspension and Wheel Quality: Look for strollers with independent suspension on all four wheels and larger diameter tires (at least 12 inches) made of rubber rather than plastic. This combination absorbs bumps and vibrations that could disturb a sleeping newborn while maintaining stability on uneven ground.
  • Flat Recline and Infant Safety: Since newborns cannot sit upright, ensure the seat reclines completely flat and has a sturdy frame that won't tip when fully reclined. The stroller should also accommodate an infant car seat or have a bassinet insert designed specifically for newborns under 3-4 months old.
  • Swivel vs. Fixed Front Wheel: Choose a model with a lockable front wheel that can be fixed straight for off-road terrain and swiveled for on-pavement maneuverability. This flexibility ensures you can navigate both smooth sidewalks and backcountry trails without compromising control.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use an all terrain stroller for a newborn?

Yes, but only if the stroller has a fully reclined seat or compatible bassinet/carrycot attachment, since newborns cannot sit upright. Always check that the model supports a completely flat position and meets safety standards for newborns before purchasing. Most all terrain strollers are designed for babies 3+ months, so verify the minimum age requirements.

What's the difference between an all terrain stroller and a regular stroller?

All terrain strollers have larger, air-filled tires, stronger suspension systems, and heavier-duty frames designed for rough surfaces like trails, sand, and gravel, while regular strollers have smaller wheels and less suspension suited only for pavement. All terrain strollers are bulkier and harder to fold, making them less convenient for urban travel or tight car trunks. Choose all terrain only if you'll regularly venture off paved surfaces.

How much weight can an all terrain stroller hold?

Most all terrain strollers support 50-75 pounds, with some heavy-duty models reaching up to 100 pounds, allowing use from infancy through age 5-6. Check the manufacturer's specifications for your exact model, as weight limits vary significantly between brands. Keep in mind that exceeding the weight limit can damage the frame and compromise safety.

Are all terrain strollers good for newborn travel on airplanes?

All terrain strollers are generally not ideal for air travel because they're bulky, heavy, and difficult to fold, which can lead to gate-checking fees and damage. For frequent flying with a newborn, a lightweight travel stroller or travel system is more practical, though you could check if your all terrain model gate-checks for free with your airline. Reserve the all terrain stroller for destination trips rather than frequent flying.
