Frequently Asked Questions

What is the safest car seat for a newborn?

Rear-facing infant car seats are considered the safest option for newborns because they provide optimal support for their delicate neck and spine during crashes. Look for seats that have passed NHTSA crash tests, include side-impact protection, and have a five-point harness system. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping babies rear-facing until age 2 or until they reach the seat's weight limit.

Do expensive car seats have better safety ratings than budget options?

Price doesn't determine safety—all car seats sold in the US must meet strict federal safety standards, so a $200 seat and a $500 seat provide equal crash protection if both are properly installed. What you're paying more for with premium seats is typically extra features like easier installation, premium fabrics, or convertible designs. Focus your budget on finding a seat that fits your vehicle well and that you'll use correctly every time.

How important is proper car seat installation for safety?

Proper installation is critical—studies show that 85% of car seats are installed incorrectly, which significantly reduces protection in a crash. Even small mistakes like incorrect harness tightness or wrong recline angle can compromise safety, so invest time in reading your manual thoroughly or schedule a free inspection with a certified child passenger safety technician. Many hospitals and fire departments offer installation checks before you leave the hospital.

Should I buy a used car seat or always get a new one?

Buying a used car seat is risky because you won't know if it's been in an accident, which can cause invisible structural damage that compromises safety in a crash. If the seat has been involved in any accident, even a minor one, it should be replaced. For peace of mind as a first-time parent, purchasing a new seat ensures you have the full safety history and current safety features.
