The Short Answer

A newborn nursery requires a safe sleeping space (crib with mattress and sheets), storage solutions (dresser), climate control, lighting, and a changing area. Most parents also benefit from a baby monitor to keep tabs on their infant from other rooms. These foundational items create a functional space where you can safely sleep, change, and care for your newborn during those early months.

The Full Explanation

Sleep Essentials

The crib is the centerpiece of any nursery and should meet current safety standards set by the Consumer Product Safety Commission. You'll need a firm, well-fitting crib mattress and several fitted crib sheets to accommodate frequent changes. Avoid pillows, bumpers, and loose blankets in the crib, as these increase SIDS risk. Instead, use a sleep sack or wearable blanket to keep your baby warm while maintaining a clear sleep surface.

Storage and Organization

A dresser serves dual purposes: it provides essential storage for clothing, blankets, and linens while offering a safe surface for diaper changes if you add a changing pad on top. Having organized storage keeps the nursery functional and makes nighttime care easier when you're tired. Consider a dresser with drawers rather than open shelving to reduce dust exposure and keep items protected.

Changing Station

You can use a dedicated changing table, a dresser with a changing pad, or even a portable changing station depending on your space and budget. A changing pad provides a safe, elevated surface with safety straps to prevent your baby from rolling. Keep diaper supplies, wipes, and creams within arm's reach of the changing area for efficiency and safety during changes.

Lighting and Temperature Control

Install dimmable lights or use soft, adjustable lighting to create a calming environment and make nighttime care easier on your eyes. A dimmer switch or bedside lamp is more practical than overhead lighting for late-night diaper changes and feedings. Temperature control is equally important—keep the room between 68-72°F (20-22°C) for safe sleep, and use a room thermometer to monitor conditions. A white noise machine can also help mask household sounds and promote better sleep.

Monitoring and Safety

A baby monitor allows you to watch your infant from other areas of your home. Video monitors let you see your baby, while audio-only monitors are simpler and more affordable. Ensure your crib meets safety standards, secure any furniture to walls to prevent tipping, and keep cords from blinds or monitors far from the crib to eliminate strangulation hazards.

What the Experts Say

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends keeping a newborn's sleep space bare, with only a fitted sheet and a wearable sleep sack. Pediatric sleep experts emphasize that room-sharing without bed-sharing for at least the first six months (ideally the first year) reduces SIDS risk significantly. Safety experts also stress the importance of a firm sleep surface and recommend regular safety checks of all nursery furniture to ensure nothing has been recalled or damaged.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the absolute must-have items for a newborn nursery?

The essentials include a safe sleep space (crib, bassinet, or play yard), a firm mattress with fitted sheets, blankets for warmth outside the crib, a dresser for clothing storage, and adequate lighting for nighttime diaper changes. You'll also want a comfortable chair or glider for feeding and bonding. Everything should meet current safety standards, particularly for the sleep surface.

Do I need a changing table or can I use a dresser?

A dedicated changing table isn't necessary—many parents use a dresser with a changing pad on top, which saves space and money. The key is having a safe, elevated surface at a comfortable height with a non-slip changing pad and keeping supplies within arm's reach. Make sure whatever you use is sturdy and won't tip over.

What type of crib mattress and bedding do I need for safety?

You need a firm, well-fitting mattress specifically designed for your crib size, along with fitted sheets made from breathable cotton. Skip pillows, bumpers, blankets, and stuffed animals in the crib, as these increase SIDS risk—use a sleep sack or swaddle for warmth instead. Wash all bedding in gentle detergent before use.

How much storage and furniture do I actually need in a newborn nursery?

A dresser or closet storage is sufficient for clothing; you don't need multiple pieces of furniture that take up space. Beyond the crib, mattress, and dresser, prioritize a comfortable seating option for nighttime feedings and a nightlight or dimmer switch for easy visibility. Keep the room uncluttered for safety and easier cleaning.
