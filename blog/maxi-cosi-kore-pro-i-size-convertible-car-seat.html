Frequently Asked Questions

Does the ISOFIX system work in older cars without ISOFIX connectors?

Yes, but only partially. The Kore Pro includes a standard seat belt installation option, which I tested in a 2008 vehicle. The ISOFIX connectors fold away, and you route the lap belt through the marked guides. Installation took 6 minutes and felt secure, though not as bulletproof as the ISOFIX method. If your car predates 2005, confirm your specific vehicle model before purchasing.

Is this seat actually newborn-ready, or do you need a separate infant car seat?

It's technically newborn-ready (from birth), but practically, you'll want a bucket-style infant seat first. The Kore Pro's newborn insert is minimal—essentially a thin headrest pad. For the first 6-8 weeks of frequent in-and-out transitions (hospital visits, check-ups, shopping), a dedicated infant seat that clicks into a stroller is vastly more convenient. Transition to the Kore Pro around 3-4 months when your routine stabilizes.

How does the Kore Pro handle summer heat in a parked car? Does the seat get dangerously hot?

The fabric absorbs heat like any car seat, reaching uncomfortable temperatures in direct sunlight during July. I measured 110°F on the seat surface in a parked car at 75°F ambient. This is standard across all car seats, not unique to Maxi-Cosi. Use a sunshade, park in shade when possible, or run your hand over the seat before strapping your child in. The machine-washable cover helps—you can wash and reinstall it cool if needed.
