What to Look For

  • Gate-Check Friendly Design: Look for strollers that fold compactly and fit standard overhead compartments (most airlines have size restrictions around 22" x 14" x 9").   • Lightweight Yet Stable Frame: The stroller should feel solid when pushing through crowded terminals without being a heavy load to carry. Aluminum frames are your friend here—they provide durability without the weight of steel.     Frequently Asked Questions

    What size stroller fits through airport security and airplane aisles?

    Look for strollers that fold to dimensions under 22 x 14 x 9 inches, which fits most airline overhead bins and security checkpoints. Lightweight umbrella strollers or compact travel strollers typically meet these requirements, though it's best to check your specific airline's dimensions before purchasing.

    Can you bring a stroller through TSA security or do you have to check it?

    You can bring a stroller through TSA security and up to the airplane gate without any issues. At the gate, you can either carry it onto the plane if space permits or have it checked for free, and it will be returned to you as you exit the aircraft.

    What's the lightest stroller for travel on airplanes?

    Umbrella strollers are typically the lightest option, weighing between 7-12 pounds, making them easy to carry through airports and lift into overhead bins. Brands like GB Pockit and Cybex Libelle offer compact models under 10 pounds without sacrificing safety and comfort features.

    Should I buy a travel stroller or use a regular stroller for airport trips?

    A dedicated travel stroller is worth the investment if you fly frequently, as they're designed to be lightweight, compact, and durable for repeated folding and handling. However, if you only travel occasionally, a regular lightweight stroller works fine as long as it's under 15 pounds and folds relatively small.
